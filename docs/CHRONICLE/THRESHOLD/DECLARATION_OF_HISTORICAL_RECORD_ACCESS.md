## Preamble

Be it known to all who approach this Threshold:

The materials preserved within the CHRONICLE Archive are maintained not in celebration, nor
in condemnation, but in the faithful discharge of a historical and legal duty — the duty to
know, accurately and completely, the laws by which this nation has governed itself, including
those which, in the fullness of time and the progress of conscience, have been found to
contradict, violate, or stand in opposition to the foundational principles upon which this
Republic was established.

---

## I. The Nature of This Archive

The CHRONICLE Archive contains primary source legal materials, historical statutes,
regulations, judicial opinions, and governing instruments, some of which embody language,
doctrine, and legal construction that is, by the standards of the present day, offensive,
discriminatory, or otherwise objectionable.

The presence of such material within this Archive reflects the law as it existed at the time
of its enactment — not the law as it stands today, and not the values of those who preserve
this record. History does not improve by erasure. It improves by accountability. These
materials are preserved precisely because the chain of their existence — their enactment,
their enforcement, their consequence, and their ultimate repudiation or reform — is essential
to the complete and accurate understanding of the legal infrastructure of this nation, and to
the compliance standards, protections, and rights that were built, in many cases, in direct
response to the wrongs these materials represent.

To remove them would be to sever the chain. A severed chain cannot be verified. An
unverified history cannot be trusted. A history that cannot be trusted cannot protect
anyone.

---

## II. The Standard Applied

All material within the CHRONICLE Archive is subject to the Traceable Standard — the
governing principle that every fact presented is sourced, every chain of events is
documented, and every historical claim is independently verifiable. No material is admitted
to this Archive on the basis of opinion, assumption, or convenience. The law as it was
written, the history as it occurred, and the record as it stands — these alone constitute
the contents of this Archive.

Access to this material is granted strictly and exclusively for the purposes of legal
research, historical documentation, compliance analysis, regulatory accountability, and the
lawful advancement of justice under law. No other purpose is sanctioned by this Declaration.

language. The records that document those harms do not serve justice less because they
contain words that wound. Those who were wronged by these laws deserved better. The least

trace exactly how the law permitted it, and build — from that honest foundation — something

That is what this Archive is for.

That is what this platform is for.
