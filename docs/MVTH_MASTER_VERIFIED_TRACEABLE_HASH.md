## Definition

**Master Verified Traceable Hash (MVTH)** is the highest-level artifact the Vernen engine
produces — the master case record that represents a complete, verified, traceable compliance
engagement from intake through finding. A Master Verified Traceable Hash is the culmination
of every Traceable Standard that ran, every Statutory Query Ledger that was consulted, and
every Judicial Standard of Notice that was produced in the course of a compliance engagement.
It is sealed by a single cryptographic hash that anchors the entire engagement to the
verification chain.

---

## Plain Language

A Master Verified Traceable Hash is the complete record. Not a single finding — the entire
case. Every document that was processed, every standard that was applied, every rule that
was queried, every notice that was produced — all of it is traced, verified, and sealed into
one hash. If you have the MVTH, you have everything. If the hash matches, nothing was
altered. If nothing was altered, the record is complete.

## Legal Significance

In traditional legal and compliance practice, a complete case record exists across
multiple systems, held by multiple parties, with no single verifiable master. Documents
are lost, versions conflict, chains of custody break.

A Master Verified Traceable Hash is a single artifact that proves the entire engagement
is complete, unaltered, and traceable to its origin. It does not replace the underlying
documents — it proves they are all accounted for and none of them have changed since the
record was sealed.
