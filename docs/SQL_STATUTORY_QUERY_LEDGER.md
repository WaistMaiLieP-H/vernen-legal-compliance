## Definition

**Statutory Query Ledger (SQL)** is the structured, version-controlled record of legal
requirements, compliance rules, and governing standards that powers the Vernen engine.
A Statutory Query Ledger is not a passive database — it is a living legal library, seeded
from primary statutory sources, versioned in the public repository, and queryable by every
Citizen in the network. Every rule it contains traces to a specific law, regulation, or
governing standard. Every change to it is recorded with a timestamp and cryptographic hash.

---

## Plain Language

A Statutory Query Ledger is the law library the engine reads from. Before a Citizen can
audit a document, it queries the Ledger to understand what the law requires. The Ledger
holds the answer. That answer traces to a statute, a regulation, or a governing standard —
not to opinion, not to interpretation, not to what someone remembered the rule was.

The Statutory Query Ledger is why Vernen findings are grounded. The law is in the Ledger.
The Ledger is in the chain. The chain is public.

---

## What Lives in a Statutory Query Ledger

Every Statutory Query Ledger entry contains:

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Rule | The specific legal requirement |
| Authority | The statute, CFR citation, or governing standard it derives from |
| Jurisdiction | Federal, state, or local scope |
| Effective date | When this rule became law |
| Category | Which compliance layer it belongs to |
| Cross-references | Related rules and standards |

No entry is valid without a traceable authority. An entry without a citation is not a
Statutory Query Ledger entry — it is an opinion. The Ledger holds law, not opinion.

---

## The Six-Layer Anchor

Every Statutory Query Ledger is organized across the six compliance layers:

| Order | Layer | Function |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Regulatory Frameworks | The statutes and regulations the Ledger encodes. |
| 2 | Governing Guidelines | The mission and ethics standards recorded in the Ledger. |
| 3 | GRC Frameworks | The risk and governance rules the Ledger holds. |
| 4 | Standards of Creation | The structural blueprints recorded as Ledger entries. |
| 5 | Procedures | The operational rules the Ledger makes queryable. |
| 6 | SOC / External Audits | The audit standards the Ledger validates against. |

---

## Relationship to TS and JSON

| Layer | Acronym | Role |
|-------|---------|------|
| Law library | SQL — Statutory Query Ledger | What the law requires |
| Engine | TS — Traceable Standard | Logic that queries the Ledger and applies the law |
| Output | JSON — Judicial Standard of Notice | The anchored finding that constitutes legal notice |

The Statutory Query Ledger is the foundation. The Traceable Standard reads it. The
Judicial Standard of Notice records what was found.

---

## Verifiability

A Statutory Query Ledger achieves its designation through:

1. **Primary source citations** — every entry traces to a specific statute or regulation
2. **Version control** — every change is committed with timestamp and hash
3. **Public availability** — the Ledger is open to independent inspection
4. **Immutable history** — prior versions of the Ledger are preserved; no rule can be
   silently removed or altered after it has been queried against
