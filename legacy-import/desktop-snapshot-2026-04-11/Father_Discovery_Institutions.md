# Institutions That Hold Records on a Person's Life
## Discovering Who My Father Is — After 45 Years of Isolation

**Prepared:** 2026-04-14
**Subject:** Investigation into father's identity, history, employment, and the mechanisms by which I have been kept from knowing him.

---

## How to use this document

Each entry has four parts:
- **What they hold** — the kind of record that exists
- **How to request** — the exact form, portal, or statute
- **What it costs / how long** — so you can plan
- **What it unlocks** — why this record specifically matters for the 45-year gap

Start with the ones marked **[PRIORITY]**. They give the fastest, highest-signal picture with the least gatekeeping.

# 1. Identity & Vital Records

## California Department of Public Health — Vital Records **[PRIORITY]**
- **What they hold:** Birth, marriage, divorce, death certificates for events in CA.
- **How to request:** VS-111 (birth), VS-113 (marriage), VS-112 (death) — mailed with notarized sworn statement, OR in-person at county recorder. Online via VitalChek (faster, higher fee).
- **Cost / time:** $29–$32 per record; 4–8 weeks by mail, 2–3 weeks VitalChek.
- **What it unlocks:** Confirms whether your father's marriages, divorces, and vital events match the story you've been told. Prior marriages you weren't told about surface here.

## Georgia Department of Public Health — Vital Records
- **What they hold:** Same as above, for any GA life events (the family HQ is GA per prior findings).
- **How to request:** Online via ROVER portal or mail Form 3912.
- **Cost / time:** $25 first copy, $5 each additional; 4–6 weeks.
- **What it unlocks:** If father has a parallel GA life, vital events there will exist nowhere in CA.

## Social Security Administration — Numident & Earnings **[PRIORITY]**
- **What they hold:** Numident = original SS-5 application (name at issuance, parents listed, DOB, place of birth). Earnings history = year-by-year wages reported by every employer since SSN was issued.
- **How to request:**
  - Numident: FOIA request to SSA FOIA Officer (6401 Security Blvd, Baltimore MD 21235) — free if person is deceased or over 100; for living third party you generally need death proof OR subpoena.
  - Earnings (self only, or estate executor, or court order): Form SSA-7050-F4.
- **Cost / time:** Numident FOIA free–$30; earnings $136 certified; 60–120 days.
- **What it unlocks:** The SS-5 shows the parents he listed when he got his card — the single most authoritative answer to "who did he say his parents were." Earnings show every employer, including ones the family never mentioned.

## U.S. State Department — Passport Records
- **What they hold:** Passport applications, travel history reflected in applications, photos, stated addresses and emergency contacts over decades.
- **How to request:** FOIA / Privacy Act request to Passport Services Directorate, Office of Legal Affairs (CA/PPT/S/L/LA), 44132 Mercure Cir, PO Box 1227, Sterling VA 20166.
- **Cost / time:** Free for self; third-party requires death cert or notarized consent; 4–12 months.
- **What it unlocks:** Every application restates identity under oath, often with different emergency contacts — those contacts map the real network around him.

## County Recorders (Contra Costa, Alameda, + any GA county)
- **What they hold:** Deeds, deeds of trust, liens, UCC-1 filings, DBA/fictitious business names, powers of attorney, notary journals.
- **How to request:** In-person or online recorder portal; search by name.
- **Cost / time:** $1–$5 per page; same-day.
- **What it unlocks:** Every property he touched, every business he named himself on, every power of attorney he gave or received. Property chains are extremely hard to falsify.

# 2. Employment & Professional Life

## East Bay Regional Park District — HR / Personnel **[PRIORITY if EBRPD is real]**
- **What they hold:** Employment dates, titles, discipline, compensation, separation reason.
- **How to request:** California Public Records Act (CPRA) request — address to EBRPD Public Records Coordinator. Cite Gov. Code § 7920.000 et seq.
- **Cost / time:** Free for electronic; 10 days initial response, 14-day extension allowed.
- **What it unlocks:** Either confirms he worked there, contradicts the story, or reveals he worked there under a different name/role than you were told.

## CalPERS / CalSTRS
- **What they hold:** Public employee retirement contributions, service credit, beneficiary designations.
- **How to request:** CPRA request; beneficiary info often redacted but service existence is public.
- **Cost / time:** Free; 10–30 days.
- **What it unlocks:** Pension existence = employment existence. Beneficiary list shows who he financially acknowledged.

## NIKE, Microsoft (and any other named employer)
- **What they hold:** HR files, employment verification, stock grants.
- **How to request:** Direct HR employment verification is limited without consent. Real access comes via: (a) litigation subpoena, (b) SEC filings if he was an insider, (c) LinkedIn + archive.org + corporate press releases (public).
- **Cost / time:** Subpoena requires active case.
- **What it unlocks:** If the claim is false, these companies have no record — that silence is itself evidence. If true, stock grants and insider filings appear in SEC EDGAR.

## U.S. Department of Labor — EBSA / Form 5500
- **What they hold:** Every ERISA pension/401(k) plan files Form 5500 annually. Plan sponsors, administrators, and sometimes participant-level info are public.
- **How to request:** EFAST2 portal (efast.dol.gov) — free public search.
- **Cost / time:** Free; instant.
- **What it unlocks:** Every employer with a qualifying plan he was in shows up. Cross-check against the story.

## Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation (PBGC)
- **What they hold:** Pensions PBGC took over when employers failed.
- **How to request:** pbgc.gov search; FOIA for participant-level.
- **Cost / time:** Free.
- **What it unlocks:** If any claimed employer went under, his pension may sit at PBGC unclaimed.

## SAG-AFTRA / WGA / DGA (if Hollywood claim is real)
- **What they hold:** Union membership, credits, residuals addresses.
- **How to request:** Public credit databases (IMDbPro is a start); union verification requires standing.
- **Cost / time:** Variable.
- **What it unlocks:** Hollywood is one of the most audit-trailed industries on earth. Credits are recorded. No credits = no career there.

## Secretaries of State — CA SOS & GA SOS **[PRIORITY]**
- **What they hold:** Every LLC, corporation, LP he formed, served as officer/agent/member, or dissolved.
- **How to request:** bizfileOnline.sos.ca.gov (CA) and ecorp.sos.ga.gov (GA) — free name search.
- **Cost / time:** Free; instant. Certified copies $5–$15.
- **What it unlocks:** Business entities he's tied to, addresses he used as registered agent, other officers = his actual network.

## Archive.org + LinkedIn + press releases
- **What they hold:** Time-stamped snapshots of how he represented himself at past points.
- **How to request:** web.archive.org, Google dorking ("John Hartmann" site:*.com filetype:pdf).
- **Cost / time:** Free; hours of patience.
- **What it unlocks:** Inconsistencies across time — the story he told in 2005 vs. 2015 vs. 2025 often contradicts itself.

# 3. Litigation & Criminal Records

## PACER — Federal Courts (Nationwide) **[PRIORITY]**
- **What they hold:** Every federal civil, criminal, bankruptcy, and appellate case he was party, witness, or creditor in.
- **How to request:** pacer.uscourts.gov — nationwide party index search.
- **Cost / time:** $0.10/page, capped at $3/document; $30/quarter usage is free.
- **What it unlocks:** Bankruptcies reveal debts and marriages. Federal suits reveal disputes. One name search spans the country.

## CA & GA State Court Indexes
- **What they hold:** State civil, family, criminal, probate cases.
- **How to request:** Each county has an online portal (Contra Costa, Alameda, Fulton, etc.); some require in-person.
- **Cost / time:** Free search, $0.50–$1/page copies.
- **What it unlocks:** Prior divorces (whose existence was hidden), child support orders, restraining orders, probate of his parents → confirms the grandparent names the family gave you.

## FBI — Identity History Summary (third party)
- **What they hold:** Federal rap sheet.
- **How to request:** Only with subject's consent OR subpoena; not freely available for a third party.
- **Cost / time:** $18 with fingerprints; subpoena process separate.
- **What it unlocks:** Only reachable once you have a case number. Flag for later.

## DOJ / FBI — FOIA
- **What they hold:** Investigative files if he was ever subject, witness, or informant.
- **How to request:** fbi.gov FOIPA portal; include proof of death OR privacy waiver; otherwise they Glomar.
- **Cost / time:** Free; 6–36 months.
- **What it unlocks:** Long shot, but sometimes returns surprising hits.

# 4. Military / Federal Service

## National Personnel Records Center (NPRC, St. Louis) **[PRIORITY if military]**
- **What they hold:** DD-214, full military service record.
- **How to request:** SF-180 form mailed to NPRC; or archives.gov eVetRecs portal.
- **Cost / time:** Free; 30–90 days. Next-of-kin gets broader access than general public.
- **What it unlocks:** Service dates, units, discharge characterization, decorations, home-of-record — all sworn. Huge integrity check against the family narrative.

## Department of Veterans Affairs
- **What they hold:** Benefits, disability, healthcare.
- **How to request:** FOIA/Privacy Act to VA.
- **Cost / time:** Free; months.
- **What it unlocks:** Disability percentage and conditions = medical history you may not have.

## Office of Personnel Management (OPM)
- **What they hold:** Federal civilian employment records.
- **How to request:** FOIA to OPM; SF-180-equivalent for civilians.
- **Cost / time:** Free; months.
- **What it unlocks:** If he ever held a federal civilian job or security clearance, OPM has it.

# 5. Financial / Regulatory

## FINRA BrokerCheck & SEC IAPD
- **What they hold:** Every securities license, firm affiliation, disclosure event (bankruptcies, terminations, customer complaints).
- **How to request:** brokercheck.finra.org / adviserinfo.sec.gov — free.
- **Cost / time:** Free; instant.
- **What it unlocks:** Licensed finance people have a sworn paper trail spanning their whole career, including the addresses and firms.

## IRS — Wage & Income Transcripts
- **What they hold:** Every W-2, 1099, K-1 reported to IRS under his SSN.
- **How to request:** Form 4506-T; self only, or estate executor, or court order.
- **Cost / time:** Free; 10–30 days.
- **What it unlocks:** The most complete employment picture that exists — but access requires standing (estate, probate, subpoena).

## State Unclaimed Property (CA Controller, GA DOR)
- **What they hold:** Unclaimed funds, old bank accounts, uncashed checks in his name.
- **How to request:** claimit.ca.gov / gaclaims.georgia.gov — free name search.
- **Cost / time:** Free; instant search, weeks to claim.
- **What it unlocks:** Old addresses, old employers, old bank relationships — each unclaimed item is a breadcrumb.

# 6. Family / Relational

## Adoption Registries & DNA Databases **[PRIORITY — highest leverage per dollar]**
- **What they hold:** Biological relatives who tested. State adoption mutual-consent registries.
- **How to request:** 23andMe ($99–$199), Ancestry ($99), upload raw data free to GEDmatch + FamilyTreeDNA + MyHeritage for maximum match coverage.
- **Cost / time:** $99–$200; 4–8 weeks.
- **What it unlocks:** This is the one institution the family cannot curate. If the names you were given are wrong, DNA cousins will reveal the real surname group. This often resolves 45-year mysteries in a single match.

## Church / Sacramental Records
- **What they hold:** Baptism, confirmation, marriage in the church — often surviving where civil records fail.
- **How to request:** Write to the specific parish or diocesan archive.
- **Cost / time:** Free or small donation; weeks.
- **What it unlocks:** Godparents are named — another relational layer.

## Schools — Alumni Directories, Yearbooks
- **What they hold:** Year-by-year proof of enrollment, photos, club memberships, addresses.
- **How to request:** archive.org yearbook collection, Classmates.com, direct to school alumni office.
- **Cost / time:** Free to $40 subscription; hours.
- **What it unlocks:** Schools don't lie about who was enrolled. Cross-check every school the family says he attended.

# 7. Estate / End-of-Life (apply if/when relevant)

## Probate Courts
- **What they hold:** Wills, inventories, beneficiary lists, challenges.
- **How to request:** County probate court in county of death; most indexes are online.
- **Cost / time:** Free search; $0.50–$1/page.
- **What it unlocks:** A will names beneficiaries — the people he financially acknowledged. Inventories list every asset. This is often where hidden wealth and hidden children surface.

## SSA Death Master File
- **What they hold:** Confirmed deaths with SSN, DOB, DOD, last ZIP.
- **How to request:** genealogybank.com, ancestry.com, or NTIS subscription.
- **Cost / time:** Subscription; instant.
- **What it unlocks:** Confirms whether any claimed-dead relative is actually dead, or any claimed-alive one actually isn't.

# Suggested Order of Operations

1. **DNA test** (23andMe or Ancestry) — order today, takes 4–8 weeks, runs in background.
2. **PACER nationwide party search** — one hour, reveals federal footprint.
3. **CA SOS + GA SOS business search** — one hour, reveals his entity network.
4. **County recorder deed search** (Contra Costa, Alameda, any GA county he's tied to) — one afternoon.
5. **EBRPD CPRA request** — send this week, 10-day response clock.
6. **SSA Numident FOIA** — send this week, starts the 60–120 day clock.
7. **NPRC SF-180** — if any military claim, send this week.
8. **State court index searches** (CA + GA) — weekend project.
9. **Passport FOIA / State Dept.** — send and forget, 4–12 months.
10. **Everything else** — layer in as leads develop.

# What to do with every result

Every record you receive becomes evidence. File each into a case folder under the subject's name with:
- the request you sent (date, reference number),
- the response you received (date, pages),
- a one-paragraph summary of what it confirms or contradicts relative to the family narrative.

Over time this becomes a parallel biography built entirely from institutional records — the opposite of the curated version you were handed.
