# Tools Financial Advisors Have That Non-Advisors Don't

Financial advisors (particularly Registered Investment Advisors, broker-dealers, and CFPs) have access to professional tools that require licensing, compliance agreements, or institutional subscriptions that retail investors can't get.

## Market Data & Research (Licensed/Paid Tiers)
- Bloomberg Terminal (~$24K/yr), FactSet, Refinitiv Eikon, Morningstar Advisor Workstation, YCharts
- Full Level II / Level III market depth, institutional research reports (Goldman, Morgan Stanley reports before public release)
- CUSIP Global Services, S&P Capital IQ

## Trading & Custody Platforms
- Institutional custodians: Schwab Advisor Services, Fidelity Institutional, Pershing, Altruist
- Block trading, rebalancing engines (iRebal, Orion, Tamarac)
- Access to IPO allocations, private placements, Reg D offerings, institutional share classes (lower expense ratios)

## Planning Software
- eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, RightCapital, NaviPlan (Monte Carlo, tax projection, estate scenarios)
- Holistiplan, Income Lab, FP Alpha (tax return parsing, Roth conversion optimization)
- Riskalyze/Nitrogen (risk tolerance scoring)

## Compliance/Regulatory
- FINRA Gateway, IARD/CRD, form ADV filing systems
- OFAC/AML screening (LexisNexis Bridger, ComplyAdvantage)
- Trade surveillance, books-and-records archiving (Smarsh, Global Relay)

## Client/Account Access
- Aggregation via ByAllAccounts, Plaid Enterprise, Yodlee (see all client accounts across institutions)
- CRM with client financial data (Redtail, Wealthbox, Salesforce FSC)
- Fee billing systems pulling directly from custodian

## Products
- Insurance/annuity carrier appointments, SMAs, interval funds, hedge fund feeders, alternatives platforms (iCapital, CAIS)

## The Gate
The regulatory gate (Series 7/65/66, CFP, state RIA registration) is what unlocks most of it — not the software cost.
