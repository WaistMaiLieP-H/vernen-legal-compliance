## Section 3 — Registration Workflow (Step by Step)

1. **Create a Login.gov account** at login.gov if you don't have one. Enable two-factor authentication (authenticator app, not SMS — SIM-swap risk is real for Vernen). Use an email address you fully control and will keep for years.
2. **Sign in to SAM.gov** at sam.gov using the Login.gov credentials. First-time sign-in prompts you to accept the SAM.gov terms.
3. **Go to "Entity Registration"** from the Workspace dashboard and choose **"Register New Entity"**. When asked the purpose, select **"All Awards"** (not "Federal Assistance Only" — you want both contract and grant eligibility).
4. **Complete the form sections in order:**
   - **Core Data**
     - Validate entity (this is where SAM calls the entity validation service — it checks your legal name + address against authoritative sources). If the match fails, SAM prompts you to upload formation docs.
     - Enter TIN / EIN and consent to IRS TIN match (IRS runs a batch validation overnight; mismatches surface 2–5 days later).
     - Enter physical address, mailing address, congressional district, start date, fiscal year end.
     - Enter EFT banking details and ACH contact.
     - Receive the **UEI** — automatically generated once entity validation succeeds. Write it down.
   - **Assertions**
     - Enter NAICS codes (primary + 5–10 secondaries).
     - Enter PSCs (optional but recommended).
     - Disaster response registry (opt in or out — recommend opt out unless you intend FEMA work).
     - Size metrics (annual receipts, employee count — pre-revenue = $0 receipts, 1 employee).
   - **Representations & Certifications**
     - Answer FAR/DFARS reps & certs. In 2026, this section is **shorter than it used to be** — procurement-specific certifications were moved out of entity registration into individual solicitations. What remains are core entity-level reps (debarment/suspension status, delinquent federal debt, tax compliance, Drug-Free Workplace Act, Buy American Act, Iran sanctions, etc.). Read every question. Do not guess.
     - If you're applying for financial assistance (grants), also complete the grants-specific reps.
   - **Points of Contact**
     - Enter all POCs (see Section 1.8). Mandatory POCs typically include E-Biz POC and Government Business POC; others are optional but recommended.
5. **Review and Submit.** SAM.gov runs a final validation before you can submit. Errors are listed at the top of each section.
6. **IRS TIN validation** runs overnight, typically 2–5 business days. If the legal name doesn't match the IRS CP-575 character-for-character, validation fails and you fix it and resubmit.
7. **CAGE code assignment** by DLA follows IRS validation: typically 3–10 business days. New registrations sometimes take longer if DLA has questions.
8. **Registration status becomes "Active."** You'll receive an email. Log in and verify the status says **Active** and the expiration date is exactly 365 days from submission.
9. **Record your UEI, CAGE code, and expiration date** in a secure place. Set a calendar reminder for 305 days from activation (i.e., 60 days before expiration) to begin renewal.

**Realistic total time:** 10 to 15 business days once the form is submitted, assuming everything validates cleanly. Three to six weeks is more realistic for a first-time registrant. Six weeks+ if there are name-match errors.

## Section 4 — Common Gotchas (from 2026 Consultant Sources)

1. **The #1 failure: Legal name mismatch with IRS.** "Vernen Compliance LLC" vs "Vernen Compliance, LLC" (comma) vs "VERNEN COMPLIANCE LLC" can all trip the TIN match. Copy the name **verbatim** from the IRS CP-575 letter, including exact comma placement and capitalization. Do not rely on the CA SOS filing or your own memory.
2. **Address formatting must match USPS exactly.** "Street" vs "St", "Suite" vs "Ste", missing apartment numbers — all break entity validation. Run the address through usps.com/ZIP Code Lookup first and copy their output character-for-character.
3. **P.O. boxes are rejected for the physical address.** You need a real street address. If you work from home, that's fine — use the home address. You can use a P.O. box for the mailing address only.
4. **Request a UEI separately before starting full registration → duplicate records → weeks of delay.** Do NOT use the "Get Unique Entity ID only" option first if you intend to do a full registration. Start the full registration directly.
5. **Bank account NOT in the LLC's legal name.** SAM.gov EFT fields must match the bank's records. A personal account or a DBA-only account triggers an ACH prenote failure and can stall activation.
6. **Pre-revenue businesses and the "annual receipts" question.** Enter `$0`. Do not leave blank. Do not inflate to look bigger — this affects small business size standard classification.
7. **Single-email POC trap.** Using the same email address for all POCs is allowed, but if that email bounces even once, the entire registration can go into manual review. Best practice: set up a domain (vernenlegal.com) with at least 2 aliases that forward to Michael.
8. **Login.gov email must persist.** If you switch Login.gov emails later, recovering access to SAM.gov is painful. Pick an email you'll still control in 5 years.
9. **Reps & Certs rushed through.** The 2026 reps are shorter than before but still legally binding certifications. False certifications are federal offenses (False Claims Act). Read each one. When in doubt, check "No" rather than guess "Yes".
10. **SBA size standards change.** The 541512 size standard has been $34M for several years but SBA periodically revisits. Verify at sba.gov/size-standards before final NAICS selection.
11. **The "annual renewal with no grace period" trap.** SAM.gov expires at exactly 365 days. On day 366 you are ineligible for new awards. Even a current federal grant application in review can be rejected if your SAM expired. Renew 60 days early.
12. **Third-party "SAM registration services" that charge $500–$1500.** Registration is **free** at sam.gov. Paying a third party is optional and they cannot speed up IRS or DLA validation. The government itself does not charge.
13. **Foreign address or foreign ownership.** Irrelevant for Vernen (CA LLC, US citizen sole member) but it's the single biggest source of delays for registrations that do have it, stretching to months.
14. **"Entity Administrator" notarized letter.** No longer universally required (since 2022), but SAM.gov can still require it as a fraud-prevention measure for new entities. If requested, it's a notarized letter from Michael appointing himself as Entity Administrator of Vernen Compliance LLC, on the LLC's letterhead, signed by Michael as Managing Member, notarized. Budget $15 and a trip to a UPS Store notary.
15. **2026 change: procurement-specific reps & certs moved to bid-level.** This actually **simplifies** your SAM registration but means you'll re-certify per-solicitation when bidding. Don't expect "set it and forget it."

## Section 5 — Renewal Cycle

- **Expiration:** Exactly 365 days after submission date. Not activation date — submission date. No grace period.
- **Consequence of expiration:** Immediately ineligible for new federal awards. Pending applications can be rejected. Existing payments on active awards typically continue but new modifications may be blocked.
- **Renewal process:**
  1. Log in to SAM.gov 60 days before expiration.
  2. Open the entity record and click "Update/Renew Entity Registration."
  3. Review every section. SAM forces you to re-certify all reps & certs even if nothing changed.
  4. Submit. Renewal revalidation typically takes 2–3 weeks (faster than initial because TIN and CAGE already exist).
- **Calendar discipline:** Set THREE reminders — 90 days, 60 days, 30 days before expiration. If the 30-day reminder fires and you haven't submitted the renewal yet, drop everything and do it immediately.
- **UEI:** Never expires. Only the registration does. The UEI stays with the entity permanently.
- **CAGE code:** Renews alongside SAM registration.

## Section 6 — What Happens After SAM.gov Is Active

An active SAM.gov registration unlocks a cascade of downstream registrations. Each must be set up separately.

| System | Purpose | Relationship to SAM.gov | Action required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Grants.gov** | Portal for submitting all federal grant applications (LSC TIG, NIST, DOJ, most civilian grants) | Pulls entity data from SAM.gov automatically. The E-Biz POC designated in SAM receives an authorization code from Grants.gov to activate the org account. | Create org account → E-Biz POC accepts auth code → assign Authorized Organization Representative (AOR) roles → each submitter creates individual Grants.gov account linked to org |
| **Research.gov** | NSF-specific proposal portal (NSF SBIR, NSF grants) | Requires active SAM.gov UEI. Separate registration on research.gov. | Org must be registered in Research.gov before any NSF submission. Create org account, then individual user accounts. |
| **SBA Company Registry (SBIR.gov)** | Required for all SBIR/STTR applications across all 11 participating agencies | Independent of SAM.gov but cross-references UEI | Register at sbir.gov/registration → get SBC Control ID. Required BEFORE submitting any SBIR/STTR proposal. |
| **eRA Commons (NIH)** | NIH-specific proposal and grant management | Requires active SAM.gov | Only if pursuing NIH SBIR/STTR. Separate registration at era.nih.gov. |
| **FedConnect / Agency-specific portals** | Various agency contracting portals (DOE, DHS, etc.) | Use SAM.gov UEI as primary identifier | Register per agency as needed. |
| **GSA eLibrary / GSA Advantage** | For GSA Schedule contracts | Requires active SAM | Optional; pursue only if Vernen gets a GSA Schedule |
| **USPTO trademark account** | Unrelated to SAM.gov but needed for trademark filings (Vernen has 16 common-law marks to consider registering) | Independent | uspto.gov TEAS account |
| **CMMC / FedRAMP** | Only if pursuing DoD or federal cloud work | Independent; requires third-party assessors | Long-term consideration |
| **DSBS (Dynamic Small Business Search)** | SBA's public-facing directory of small businesses | Auto-populates from SAM.gov when "Include in Public Search" is checked | Verify your profile on dsbs.sba.gov 2–3 weeks after SAM activation |

**Recommended sequence for Vernen's near-term targets:**
1. SAM.gov active → 2. SBA Company Registry (sbir.gov) → 3. Grants.gov org account → 4. Research.gov org account (if pursuing NSF SBIR) → 5. Individual submitter accounts everywhere.
