## Executive Summary

The City of Stockton's FY2024 Single Audit identified 6 findings across all three major HUD entitlement programs — CDBG, ESG, and HOME. Four of the six findings are concentrated in CDBG alone, covering payroll controls, procurement, federal reporting, and environmental review. This is not four isolated deficiencies — it is a systemic breakdown in CDBG program administration.

The SAM verification failure (2024-005) carries the most immediate legal risk. If any CDBG procurement was awarded to a suspended or debarred entity, the City faces potential False Claims Act exposure under 31 USC 3729. This finding should be investigated and closed first.

This plan addresses the systemic CDBG gap as one program administration fix, with separate targeted actions for the ESG and HOME findings. The goal is closure of all findings before FY2025 fieldwork.

---

## Systemic Analysis

### Pattern 1: CDBG Program Administration Gap (Findings 2024-004, 2024-005, 2024-006, 2024-007)

Four findings in a single program in a single audit year is not coincidence. When payroll, procurement, reporting, and environmental review all fail simultaneously, the root cause is not in any one of those areas — it is in the overall program administration framework that should be ensuring compliance across all of them.

The likely root cause: the City's CDBG program lacks a single compliance officer or function responsible for cross-cutting federal requirements. Each area (finance, procurement, planning) operates independently without a unified compliance checkpoint. The corrective action must address this structural gap, not just patch each finding individually.

### Pattern 2: HUD-Wide Compliance Controls

Findings span all three HUD programs (CDBG, ESG, HOME). While the ESG and HOME findings are distinct, they suggest the internal control weaknesses are not limited to CDBG. A compliance framework built for CDBG should be extended to cover ESG and HOME to prevent findings from migrating across programs.

## Finding 2024-004

**Program:** CDBG Entitlement (14.218)
**Compliance Area:** Allowable Costs — Payroll Expenditure Controls
**Severity:** HIGH
**Regulation:** 2 CFR 200.403 (Allowable Costs), 2 CFR 200.430 (Compensation)

### Root Cause

Inadequate cost allocation methodology or missing documentation for expenditure classification. Payroll charged to CDBG without time-and-effort documentation per 2 CFR 200.430.

### Corrective Actions

| # | Action | Responsible | Deadline | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Review and update written policies for Allowable Costs — Payroll Expenditure Controls | CDBG Program Manager | Week 2 | Updated policy document with approval signatures |
| 2 | Implement time-and-effort documentation for all staff charging time to CDBG | Finance Director | Week 3 | Time certification forms, semi-annual certification schedule |
| 3 | Secondary review of all CDBG payroll charges before posting | Finance Director | Week 3 | Dual-approval workflow documentation |
| 4 | Map ESG obligation/expenditure/payment deadlines into financial tracking system | Grants Manager | Week 4 | ESG compliance calendar with automated reminders |
| 5 | Monthly ESG expenditure reconciliation against obligation timeline | Finance Director | Week 4 | Monthly reconciliation template |
| 6 | Train responsible staff on updated procedures | Program Manager | Week 4 | Training attendance log with signatures |
| 7 | Quarterly internal monitoring review through next audit cycle | Internal Audit | Quarterly | Monitoring memo documenting control effectiveness |

## Finding 2024-005

**Program:** CDBG Entitlement (14.218)
**Compliance Area:** Procurement — SAM Verification Failures
**Severity:** CRITICAL
**Regulation:** 2 CFR 200.214 (Suspension and Debarment), 31 USC 3729 (False Claims Act exposure)

### Root Cause

Procurement policies not aligned with 2 CFR 200.320 or missing verification steps in vendor selection workflow.
