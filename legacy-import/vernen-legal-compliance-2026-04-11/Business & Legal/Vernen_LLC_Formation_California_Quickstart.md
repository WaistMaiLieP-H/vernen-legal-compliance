## STEP 1 — Pick the Legal Name

This is the most strategic step. The name you put on the Articles of Organization is the **legal entity name** that will appear on grant applications, federal forms, contracts, and bank accounts. It is technically separate from the **brand name** you use publicly.

### The "legal" word problem

California Corporations Code §17708.02 and the State Bar of California Business & Professions Code §6125 restrict the use of words like **"legal," "law," "lawyer," "attorney"** in business names if the business is not a law firm. The State Bar has actively pursued companies with names like "LegalZoom" and others over the years, requiring disclaimers and restrictions.

**You currently call the project "Vernen Legal Compliance."** That name almost certainly cannot be the legal entity name without triggering bar association scrutiny.

### Recommended name strategy

**Form the legal entity under a different name. Use "Vernen Legal Compliance" as a public-facing trade name (DBA) on top of it.**

**Recommended legal entity name (pick one):**

1. **Vernen Compliance LLC** ← my pick. Clean, descriptive, no protected words, easy to spell, easy to verify, covers all of Vernen's actual activities.
2. **Vernen Forensics LLC** — also good. "Forensics" describes the Layer 7 audit work accurately and isn't a protected word.
3. **Vernen Stewardship LLC** — captures the foundation ethic but is less descriptive.
4. **Vernen Verification LLC** — captures the verification protocol but excludes the compliance/audit story.
5. **Hartmann Compliance Holdings LLC** — uses the founder name; less brand-aligned but completely safe.

**My strong recommendation: `Vernen Compliance LLC`.** It's descriptive of the actual work, contains no protected words, registers cleanly with both state and federal databases, and pairs naturally with the trade name "Vernen Legal Compliance" via a DBA filing.

### How DBA / Fictitious Business Name works in California

After the LLC is formed under "Vernen Compliance LLC," you file a **Fictitious Business Name Statement** in the county where the business operates (Contra Costa County for you). This authorizes the LLC to do business publicly under the trade name "Vernen Legal Compliance" — including on the website, in marketing, in client contracts, etc.

The legal entity remains "Vernen Compliance LLC" for IRS/SAM.gov/federal/banking purposes. The public-facing name remains "Vernen Legal Compliance." Both names refer to the same entity.

**Filing fee for the DBA:** $26-$60 in Contra Costa County, plus newspaper publication requirement (~$50-$150). Done at the County Clerk-Recorder's office in Martinez.

**Total name + DBA cost:** ~$80-$210.

### Name availability check

Before filing anything, verify the name is available:

1. **California Secretary of State business search:** https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov/search/business
   - Search for "Vernen Compliance"
   - Must return "no results found" or only inactive/dissolved entities
2. **Trademark check (USPTO):** https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/search
   - Search for "Vernen" — confirm no conflicting active registrations
3. **Domain check:** verify `vernencompliance.com` is available (or skip; you already own `vernenlegal.com`)
4. **Cross-check social media handles** if you care about brand consistency

## STEP 2 — File Articles of Organization (Form LLC-1)

This is the document that legally creates the LLC.

**Where:** California Secretary of State, online at **https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov**

**Form:** LLC-1 (Articles of Organization)

**Filing fee:** **$70** (paid by credit/debit card during online filing)

**What the form asks for:**

| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| 1. LLC name | `Vernen Compliance LLC` (or your chosen name; must end in "LLC", "L.L.C.", or "Limited Liability Company") |
| 2. Business address | Your physical address (street; not a PO box) |
| 3. Mailing address | Same as business address, or different if you have a separate one |
| 4. Agent for service of process | Your name and address (you can be your own agent), OR a registered agent service ($100-$300/year) |
| 5. Type of management | **"One Manager"** or **"Member-Managed"** — pick **Member-Managed** for a single-member LLC |
| 6. Purpose | Pre-filled standard language — leave it as default |
| 7. Organizer signature | Your signature (typed name during online filing) |

**Time to complete:** ~30 minutes including the name search.

**Approval timeline:** Online filings are typically processed within **5-10 business days**. Pay an extra $350 for "guaranteed 24-hour" processing if you're in a rush. The standard 5-10 days is fine for Vernen's purposes.

**What you get back:** A stamped, certified copy of the Articles of Organization with a **California LLC Number** (12-digit). This is your proof of legal formation. You'll need it for the EIN, the SAM.gov registration, the business bank account, and every federal form going forward.

### Should you use a registered agent service?

You can be your own agent for service of process — it's free and works fine for a single-person LLC. The downside is that **legal documents (lawsuits, summonses, subpoenas) get served at your home address, which becomes part of the public record.** For most founders this is a minor concern.

**Registered agent services** (Northwest Registered Agent, Harbor Compliance, ZenBusiness, etc.) cost $100-$300/year and provide a privacy buffer. They forward all legal mail to you electronically.

**Recommendation for Vernen:** **Be your own agent for now.** The privacy concern is minor at your scale and you save $100-$300/year. You can switch to a service later if Vernen ever has reason to keep the founder address out of public records.

## STEP 3 — Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS

The EIN is the federal tax ID for the LLC. You need it for:
- Opening a business bank account
- Registering on SAM.gov
- Filing federal grant applications
- Filing federal taxes
- Issuing 1099s to contractors
- Almost every other federal form

**Where:** IRS online application at **https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online**

**Cost:** **Free.** The IRS does not charge for EINs. **Avoid any third-party site that charges to "obtain an EIN" — they're scams or unnecessary middlemen.**

**Time to complete:** **~10-15 minutes online.** The EIN is issued immediately at the end of the application (you can download a confirmation letter PDF).

**Important:** You must wait until the LLC is officially formed (Step 2 approved) before applying for the EIN. The IRS application asks for the LLC's California state ID number.

**What you'll need to fill in:**
- Legal entity name (Vernen Compliance LLC)
- California LLC number (from Step 2)
- Business address
- Responsible party name and SSN (yours, as the single member)
- Date the LLC was formed
- Reason for applying ("Started new business")
- Principal business activity (select "Other" → describe as "Compliance auditing and document forensic analysis")
- Number of employees expected in next 12 months (0 if you're not hiring; can be increased later)

**The form spits out a CP 575 letter immediately** with your EIN. **Save this PDF in multiple places.** You will need it forever.

## STEP 4 — Draft an Operating Agreement

California law **requires** every LLC to have an Operating Agreement, even single-member LLCs. **It does not need to be filed with the state** — it just needs to exist as a written document on file with the LLC's records.

Why it matters:
- Establishes the legal separation between you (Michael) and the LLC (Vernen Compliance LLC). This is what gives you "limited liability" protection — it makes it harder for someone suing the LLC to also come after your personal assets.
- Required by most banks before they'll open a business account
- Required by some federal forms
- Defines what happens if you ever sell the LLC, dissolve it, take on a member, etc.

### Template for a single-member LLC Operating Agreement

You can use the template below as a starting point. **Customize the bracketed fields, then save it as a PDF or Word doc on your computer.** You don't file it with anyone; you just keep it on hand.

```
OPERATING AGREEMENT OF VERNEN COMPLIANCE LLC

This Operating Agreement (the "Agreement") of Vernen Compliance LLC
(the "Company") is entered into and effective as of [DATE], by Michael
Hartmann (the "Member"), being the sole member of the Company.

1. FORMATION. The Company was formed as a California limited liability
   company on [DATE] by filing Articles of Organization with the California
   Secretary of State.

2. NAME. The name of the Company is Vernen Compliance LLC.

3. PURPOSE. The Company is organized to engage in any lawful business
   activity, including but not limited to: developing, deploying, and
   licensing software for compliance auditing, document forensic analysis,
   and verifiable AI infrastructure; providing professional consulting
   services in connection with such software; and any other activities
   consistent with the foregoing.

4. PRINCIPAL OFFICE. The principal office of the Company is located at
   [ADDRESS], or such other location as the Member may designate.

5. MEMBER. The Member of the Company is Michael Hartmann, who owns 100%
   of the membership interests.

6. MANAGEMENT. The Company is member-managed. The Member has full,
   exclusive, and complete authority and discretion in the management
   and control of the business of the Company.

7. CAPITAL CONTRIBUTION. The Member's initial capital contribution to
   the Company is described in the Company's records.

8. DISTRIBUTIONS. Distributions of cash and other assets shall be made
   to the Member at such times and in such amounts as the Member shall
   determine in their sole discretion.

9. LIMITED LIABILITY. The Member shall not be personally liable for any
   debt, obligation, or liability of the Company solely by reason of
   being a member of the Company, in accordance with California
   Corporations Code §17703.04.

10. INDEMNIFICATION. The Company shall indemnify the Member to the
    fullest extent permitted by California law for any liability arising
    from acts taken in good faith on behalf of the Company.

11. ACCOUNTING. The Company shall maintain its books and records on the
    cash method of accounting. The fiscal year of the Company shall be
    the calendar year.

12. DISSOLUTION. The Company shall be dissolved upon the written
    election of the Member or upon the occurrence of any event requiring
    dissolution under California law.

13. AMENDMENT. This Agreement may be amended only by a written
    instrument signed by the Member.

14. GOVERNING LAW. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the
    State of California, without regard to conflict of law principles.

15. ENTIRE AGREEMENT. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement of
    the Member with respect to the subject matter hereof.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned has executed this Operating
Agreement as of the date first written above.

MEMBER:

_________________________________
Michael Hartmann, sole Member
Date: ____________
```

**Print, sign, scan, save.** That's it. The Operating Agreement exists.

## STEP 5 — File Statement of Information (Form LLC-12)

Within **90 days of forming the LLC**, California requires you to file a Statement of Information. This provides the state with current contact information for the LLC.

**Where:** Same site as Step 2 — **https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov**

**Form:** LLC-12

**Filing fee:** **$20**

**What the form asks for:**
- LLC name and California LLC number
- Business address
- Mailing address (if different)
- Name and address of the agent for service of process
- Name(s) of manager(s) or member(s)
- Type of business
- A signature

**Time to complete:** ~15-20 minutes.

**Recurring filing:** You must file a new Statement of Information **every 2 years** thereafter. The fee remains $20. If you forget, the state imposes a $250 penalty and may eventually suspend the LLC. Set a calendar reminder.

## STEP 6 — Open a Business Bank Account

The LLC needs its own bank account, separate from your personal account, **for the limited liability protection to actually work.** If you commingle personal and business funds, courts can "pierce the corporate veil" and treat the LLC as if it doesn't exist for liability purposes.

**Documents you'll need to bring to the bank:**
1. Articles of Organization (filed copy from Step 2)
2. EIN confirmation letter (from Step 3)
3. Operating Agreement (from Step 4)
4. Government-issued photo ID
5. Initial deposit (most banks require $25-$100 to open)

**Bank options for a small LLC in California:**

| Bank | Monthly Fee | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mercury** | $0 | No monthly fee, no minimum balance, online-only, designed for startups, integrates with Stripe | Online-only — no branch banking |
| **Relay** | $0 | Similar to Mercury, no fees, multiple sub-accounts | Online-only |
| **Chase Business Complete Banking** | $15 (waivable with $2K balance) | Major bank, branches everywhere, integrates with most financial software | $15/month if balance drops |
| **Bank of America Business Advantage** | $16 (waivable) | Major bank, branches | Fees if balance drops |
| **Wells Fargo Initiate Business** | $10 (waivable) | Major bank, branches | Fees if balance drops |
| **Local credit union (Travis CU, Patelco, etc.)** | $0-$10 | Local, friendly, low fees | Varies by institution |

**My recommendation:** **Mercury or Relay.** Both are designed for early-stage businesses, have $0 monthly fees, no minimum balance requirements, and integrate cleanly with Stripe (which you're already using on the Vernen platform). Online-only is fine for a software business that doesn't need to handle cash.

**Time to open:** Mercury/Relay applications are online and typically approved within 1-3 business days. Traditional banks require an in-branch appointment (~30-60 minutes).

## STEP 7 — Pay the California $800 Minimum Franchise Tax

This is the unavoidable cost of doing business as an LLC in California. Every LLC owes **$800/year** to the Franchise Tax Board, regardless of revenue. There is no exemption for new LLCs (a previous "first-year free" provision expired in 2024).

**When the first payment is due:**
- The **15th day of the 4th month** after the LLC is formed.
- Example: if you form the LLC in April 2026, the first $800 is due by **August 15, 2026**.

**How to pay:**
- Online via the California Franchise Tax Board: **https://www.ftb.ca.gov**
- Use **Form 3522** (LLC Tax Voucher)
- Pay by credit card or bank transfer

**Subsequent years:** $800 is due every year by **April 15** (same as personal income tax).

**Plan for this in your cash flow.** $800 is not a large amount but it's a recurring obligation that has to be met or the FTB will suspend the LLC.

## STEP 8 — Local Business License (Antioch / Contra Costa County)

If your business has a physical location in a California city, that city typically requires a local business license.

**For Antioch, CA:** https://www.antiochca.gov/government/finance/business-license/

**Cost:** Typically $30-$150/year for a small home-based business.

**Time to complete:** ~30 minutes online or in person at City Hall.

**What you need:**
- LLC name and EIN
- Business address
- Description of business activities
- NAICS code (see Step 9)

**Note:** If you operate from a home office, some cities also require a "home occupation permit." Antioch generally allows home-based software/consulting businesses without a separate permit, but check the current rules at the link above.

## STEP 9 — NAICS Code for Vernen

**NAICS** (North American Industry Classification System) codes are how the federal government classifies businesses for statistical and procurement purposes. You'll need one (or several) for SAM.gov, federal grants, and the local business license.

**Recommended NAICS codes for Vernen Compliance LLC:**

| Code | Title | Why It Fits |
|---|---|---|
| **541512** | Computer Systems Design Services | Catches the platform engineering side |
| **541611** | Administrative Management & General Management Consulting Services | Catches the compliance audit consulting side |
| **541990** | All Other Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services | Catch-all for the verification protocol / forensic audit work that doesn't fit the other categories |
| **541219** | Other Accounting Services | Optional; only if you want the financial-audit angle |
| **519130** | Internet Publishing & Broadcasting | Optional; only if Vernen monetizes content/data |

**For SAM.gov, you can register multiple NAICS codes.** Pick the **primary NAICS** as the one that best describes your largest activity. **541512 is the strongest primary** because it captures the platform engineering work and is recognized by every federal grant program.

Set the others as **secondary NAICS** to broaden your eligibility for grants and contracts in adjacent areas.

## STEP 10 — Optional: Trademark Registration

**Not required for entity formation,** but worth knowing about. You currently use "VERNEN™" and "CITIZEN™" as common-law trademarks (per the project memory). Common-law trademarks are real and enforceable but limited to the geographic area where you actually use them.

**Federal trademark registration** (through the USPTO) gives you nationwide protection and stronger enforcement rights. It costs **$250-$350 per class** and takes **8-12 months** to process.

**Should Vernen federally register the trademarks?**

- **Pros:** Stronger legal protection, lookup in the federal trademark database (which is what Anthropic and other major partners check), allows use of the ® symbol.
- **Cons:** $250-$350 per class is real money; Vernen has at least two marks (VERNEN, CITIZEN) so you're looking at $500-$700 minimum. The trademark must already be in use in commerce to qualify for registration.

**My recommendation:** **Defer trademark registration until after the first paying client.** Continue using ™ for now. Once Vernen has actual revenue, the cost of trademark registration is trivial relative to the protection it provides. Until then, the common-law marks are sufficient.

## What This Does NOT Do

To be clear about what the LLC alone does and doesn't accomplish:

- ❌ **Does not give you tax advantages.** A single-member LLC is a "pass-through entity" by default — taxes flow to your personal return on Schedule C. There's no tax benefit until you elect S-Corp status (which only makes sense once revenue is significant).
- ❌ **Does not protect against your own personal misconduct.** If you personally do something tortious or fraudulent, the LLC veil doesn't protect you. The LLC protects against contractual obligations and ordinary business liabilities.
- ❌ **Does not make Vernen a 501(c)(3) nonprofit.** If you ever want nonprofit status (for some grants), that's a separate, more involved process.
- ❌ **Does not authorize the unauthorized practice of law.** The §6 UPL protections in the Forensic Audit Service Agreement template still apply. The LLC name should not contain "Legal" — that's why the recommendation above is "Vernen Compliance LLC" with "Vernen Legal Compliance" as a DBA.
