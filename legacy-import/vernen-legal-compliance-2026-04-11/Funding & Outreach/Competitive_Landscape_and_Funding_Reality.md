### What the Competition Covers (Honest Assessment)

**Westlaw / LexisNexis / Casetext** — Exceptional legal research libraries. They tell you what the law says. They do not tell you whether the document in front of you complies with it. The attorney still has to do that manually.

**Harvey AI** — General legal AI assistant. Drafts, summarizes, researches. No autonomous compliance agents. No statutory audit engine. No enforcement-grade findings. It's a very capable paralegal that doesn't specialize.

**Ironclad / Kira** — Contract AI. Corporate contracts, enterprise clients. Civil rights, family law, criminal defense, victim compensation: outside scope by design.

**OneTrust / TrustArc** — Enterprise privacy program management. GDPR/CCPA policy infrastructure for corporations. Zero individual-facing capability. Zero autonomous agents. Zero statutory audit against real document content.

**Relativity** — eDiscovery. Processing documents for litigation that has already started. Vernen prevents the litigation from being necessary.

**DoNotPay** — The closest in spirit to Vernen. Individual-facing, accessible. Narrow scope (parking tickets, small claims, form letters), no compliance engine, no statutory audit, no Citizens, no evidence chain. Shut down its attorney chatbot feature after bar association pressure.

**The gap:** Every competitor serves either (a) attorneys who already know the law, or (b) corporations with legal teams. Nobody serves the person in the room who needs to know right now whether what is happening to them is legal.
