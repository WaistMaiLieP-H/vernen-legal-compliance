## THE DESIRABILITY FILTER

A persona is desirable when it passes all four:

1. **Volume** — 500+ consumer-facing compliance interactions per year in state
2. **Gap** — the regulatory surface is meaningfully unverified in real time
3. **Impact** — the compliance failure reaches an individual citizen, not just a business
4. **Intake surface** — the failure produces a document, recording, or interaction a citizen would submit

Below threshold on two or more = routes to a broader Citizen, not its own persona.

## NATIONAL — 340 MILLION PEOPLE

### Methodology
The 2,700+ distinct licensed occupations nationally include heavy state-to-state redundancy — a "licensed contractor" in Texas and a "licensed contractor" in California are different entries in the IJ database but are mostly the same persona with different statute citations. Unique professional roles with consumer-facing compliance gaps and meaningful case volume: narrower.

## THE RATIO THAT MATTERS

```
California:  1 desirable compliance persona per 263,000 residents
National:    1 desirable compliance persona per 212,000 residents
```

The ratio is consistent. It doesn't matter if it's California or Mississippi —
the human need for a compliance verification layer scales almost exactly with population.

Mississippi doesn't need a forensic accountant Citizen the way New York does.
But Mississippi needs a family law Citizen, a criminal defense Citizen,
a consumer protection Citizen, a contractor compliance Citizen,
and a court translation Citizen just as badly as California does —
and those four Citizens alone cover the compliance failures
that reach the most people in any state, regardless of economic density.

**The sophistication of the economy changes which personas matter most.**
**The existence of civil rights, family courts, and consumer fraud does not.**
