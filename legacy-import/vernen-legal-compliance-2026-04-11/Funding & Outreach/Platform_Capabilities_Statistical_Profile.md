## WHAT THE ENGINE CAN DO — DEMONSTRATED CAPABILITIES

### Capability 1 — Document Compliance at Point of Submission
A document is submitted. The engine:
1. Classifies the document by type (contract · police report · medical consent · court filing · government notice)
2. Identifies every applicable legal framework based on classification
3. Runs triple-constraint audit: Governing Guidelines · Standards of Creation · SOC Integrity
4. Returns a compliance finding with specific statute, severity rating, and evidence
5. Routes to the appropriate Citizen for professional-level analysis

**Demonstrated:** Live on compliance.vernenlegal.com. No human required between submission and finding.

### Capability 2 — Autonomous App Compliance Audit
An app identifier is submitted. The engine:
1. Fetches app metadata, Data Safety declarations, and privacy policy
2. Retrieves and parses the privacy policy content
3. Runs three-layer contradiction detection (Declaration vs. Policy · Policy vs. Permissions · Policy vs. SDK behavior)
4. Applies GDPR, CCPA, COPPA, FTC, and platform policy rules
5. Returns per-violation findings with statute citations

**Demonstrated:** 560 apps audited, 99 flagged, 5 critical findings confirmed in first two full audits.

### Capability 3 — Corpus-Level Exposure Quantification
Given a set of apps or documents, the engine aggregates:
- Total population affected
- Total PII exposure estimate
- Fraud signal concentration
- Regulatory framework distribution

**Demonstrated:** 4.08B install exposure quantified across 560-app corpus in a single analytical pass.

### Capability 4 — Legal Standard Application
Given a statute or regulation, the engine applies it against submitted evidence with:
- Current statutory text
- Historical evolution chain
- Binding case precedent
- Cross-references to related standards
- Verifiable provenance for every citation

**Demonstrated:** 574 standards live with five-layer structure. Citizens apply these autonomously without instruction.
