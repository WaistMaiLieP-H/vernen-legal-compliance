## What Vernen Brings

Vernen Legal Compliance has built a working AI-powered compliance platform — not a concept, not a pitch deck, but a deployed system at compliance.vernenlegal.com. The platform includes:

- **Automated regulatory compliance analysis** across federal, state, and local law — covering healthcare, housing, employment, civil rights, and benefits eligibility
- **A standards library of 574+ compliance standards** with 155 cross-references, organized by jurisdiction and subject matter
- **Audit simulation and risk profiling** that identifies compliance failures before they become enforcement actions or denials of benefits
- **AI-powered document forensics** capable of detecting inconsistencies in government-issued documents, court filings, and benefits determinations
- **Six compliance intelligence pipelines** drawing from public federal databases (FAC, HHS, EDGAR, SBA, USAspending, Federal Register) to identify patterns of institutional failure

This technology was purpose-built for the exact population BayLegal serves: low-income individuals navigating government systems that are supposed to protect them but frequently fail them through administrative error, regulatory complexity, or institutional noncompliance.

## The Proposal: AI-Assisted Legal Triage and Compliance Verification

I propose we jointly apply with the following project scope:

**Problem:** Low-income Bay Area residents face denials of public benefits, housing protections, and healthcare access not because they are ineligible, but because the government agencies serving them fail to comply with their own rules. These failures are invisible to clients and often invisible to legal aid attorneys until deep into a case.

**Solution:** Deploy Vernen's AI compliance verification tools within BayLegal's intake and case assessment workflow to:

1. **Automated compliance screening at intake** — When a client contacts BayLegal, AI-assisted triage identifies whether the government agency involved has known compliance failures, recent audit findings, or patterns of regulatory noncompliance
2. **Benefits denial verification** — Cross-reference denial letters against current regulatory requirements to flag denials that violate the agency's own rules
3. **Document integrity analysis** — Detect unsigned documents, missing certifications, expired authorizations, and procedural defects in government actions affecting clients
4. **Proactive case identification** — Use federal compliance databases to identify agencies with systemic failures and proactively reach affected communities before individual harm escalates

**Impact Metrics:**
- Number of benefits denials overturned through compliance verification
- Reduction in time-to-resolution for compliance-related cases
- Number of systemic compliance failures identified and reported
- Client outcomes (benefits restored, housing preserved, healthcare accessed)
