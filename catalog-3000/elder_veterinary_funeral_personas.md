# THE TRIPLE CONSTRAINT

Every persona in this catalog is evaluated against three mandatory dimensions:

1. **GOVERNING GUIDELINES** — The laws, regulations, and professional standards that define what the professional MUST do
2. **STANDARDS OF CREATION** — The education, licensure, certification, and continuing education requirements that authorize the professional to practice
3. **SOC CONTROLS / DOCUMENT AUDIT OBLIGATIONS** — The specific document types the professional creates, signs, certifies, or is accountable for, and the controls that govern those documents

A persona is cataloged ONLY when all three constraints produce document audit obligations.

## Subspecialty A1: Facility Administration & Licensing

---

### A1-01: Skilled Nursing Facility Administrator (LNHA/NHA)

**Governing Body:** California Department of Public Health (CDPH); Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS); California Board of Nursing Home Administrators (under DCA)

**Governing Guidelines:**
- 42 CFR Part 483 — Requirements for States and Long-Term Care Facilities
- 42 CFR § 483.70 — Administration (facility must be governed by a body with a licensed administrator)
- 42 CFR § 483.75 — Quality Assurance and Performance Improvement (QAPI)
- Cal. Health & Safety Code § 1250 et seq. — Health Facility Licensing
- Cal. Health & Safety Code § 1261.4 — Medical Director Requirements for SNFs
- Cal. Health & Safety Code § 1265-1268.5 — Licensing Applications and Standards
- 22 CCR Division 5 — Skilled Nursing Facilities
- 22 CCR § 72501-72527 — Administrative Requirements

**Standards of Creation:**
- Bachelor's degree (minimum); master's preferred
- Complete state-approved Administrator-in-Training (AIT) program (1,000 hours)
- Pass NAB (National Association of Long Term Care Administrator Boards) examination
- California LNHA license from Board of Nursing Home Administrators
- 40 hours continuing education per renewal cycle (2 years)
- Background check (DOJ/FBI Live Scan)

**Document Types Audited:**
- CMS-2567 (Statement of Deficiencies and Plan of Correction)
- CDPH facility inspection reports
- QAPI program documentation
- Staffing ratio compliance reports (Cal. HSC § 1276.2)
- Admission agreements (HSC § 1599.77, § 1599.85)
- Facility emergency/disaster plans
- Theft and loss program documentation (HSC § 1289.4)
- Resident rights postings and documentation
- Transfer/discharge notices (HSC § 1439.6)
- License applications and renewal documents
