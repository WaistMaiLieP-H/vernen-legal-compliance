#### PERSONA A.1.1: Designated 5150 Evaluator (Licensed Professional)

**Professional Title:** Designated 5150 Evaluator / Professional Person in Charge
**Governing Body:** County Behavioral Health Department; DHCS (State Department of Health Care Services)

**Governing Guidelines:**
- Cal. Welf. & Inst. Code § 5150 (72-hour involuntary detention authority)
- Cal. Welf. & Inst. Code § 5150.2 (peace officer transport/detention time limits)
- Cal. Welf. & Inst. Code § 5151 (admission and detention procedures)

- 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (civil rights liability for wrongful detention)

**Standards of Creation:**
1. Application for 72-Hour Detention for Evaluation and Treatment (form)
2. Probable cause statement documenting danger to self, danger to others, or grave disability
3. Clinical assessment within time-of-admission window
4. Patient advisement of rights (Patients' Rights Advocate notification)
5. Documentation of least restrictive alternative analysis
6. Notification to county behavioral health director or designee per § 5152.1
7. Discharge plan or certification decision within 72 hours

**SOC Controls:**
- SOC-1: Credentialing verification (designated facility authorization by county)
- SOC-2: Time-stamp audit (72-hour clock compliance)
- SOC-3: Rights advisement documentation (informed of right to attorney, habeas corpus)
- SOC-4: Clinical criteria documentation (specific facts supporting each criterion)
- SOC-5: Least restrictive alternative analysis present in record

**Document Types Audited:** 5150 applications, clinical assessments, rights advisement forms, discharge summaries, county notification records, patient property inventories

#### PERSONA A.2.2: Riese Hearing Judge / Hearing Officer

**Governing Guidelines:**

- In re Qawi (2004) 32 Cal.4th 1 (MDO medication rights)
- Washington v. Harper (1990) 494 U.S. 210 (federal due process for forced medication)

**Standards of Creation:**
1. Petition documenting specific medications proposed and clinical necessity
2. Capacity assessment by treating psychiatrist
3. Notice to patient and patient's attorney/advocate
4. Hearing with testimony regarding patient's capacity to make treatment decisions
5. Judicial finding on capacity (not dangerousness — capacity to understand/weigh)
6. Order specifying medications, dosages, duration, and review date
7. Documentation of less intrusive alternatives considered

**SOC Controls:**
- SOC-1: Capacity standard applied (not dangerousness or treatment refusal alone)
- SOC-2: Specific medication and dosage identified in order
- SOC-3: Time-limited order with mandatory review date
- SOC-4: Patient represented by counsel or advocate
- SOC-5: Less intrusive alternative analysis documented

**Document Types Audited:** Riese petitions, capacity assessments, hearing transcripts, medication orders, review/renewal petitions, patients' rights advocate reports
