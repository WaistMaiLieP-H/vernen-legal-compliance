## THE TRIPLE CONSTRAINT — Applied to Every Persona

Each persona is bound by three interlocking obligations:

| Constraint | Definition | Audit Question |
|---|---|---|
| **GOVERNING GUIDELINES** | The law, regulation, or professional standard that creates the obligation | "What rule requires this document to exist?" |
| **STANDARDS OF CREATION** | The format, content, timing, and certification requirements for the document | "Was this document created correctly?" |
| **SOC CONTROLS** | The internal controls (access, retention, integrity, chain of custody) governing the document lifecycle | "Was this document handled correctly after creation?" |

**SOC Classification Tiers:**
- **S.o.C.-1**: Organization-level controls (policies, procedures, access management)
- **S.o.C.-2**: Trust services criteria (security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, privacy)
- **S.o.C.-3**: Public-facing assurance (simplified reporting for external stakeholders)

## A1. Alcoholic Beverage Compliance (3 Personas)

### A1-001: Alcohol Beverage Control Compliance Officer
- **Governing Body**: State ABC Boards; TTB (Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau, U.S. Dept. of Treasury)
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - 27 U.S.C. §§ 201-219a (Federal Alcohol Administration Act)
  - 27 CFR Parts 1-31 (TTB Regulations)
  - Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code §§ 23000-25762 (CA ABC Act)
  - State-specific ABC codes (all 50 states)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - License applications must include premises diagram, ownership disclosure, background check authorization
  - Renewal filings per state-mandated cycle (annual/biennial)
  - Posted license must match premises address and licensee name exactly
  - Conditional use permits must precede ABC approval
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: License access restricted to management; renewal calendar maintained
  - S.o.C.-2: Original license retained on-premises; copies authenticated
  - S.o.C.-3: Public posting of license number and type at point of sale
- **Document Types Audited**: Liquor license applications and renewals, ABC compliance documents, special event permits

### A1-002: Responsible Beverage Service Trainer
- **Governing Body**: State ABC Boards; TIPS (Training for Intervention ProcedureS); ServSafe Alcohol (NRA)
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - State mandatory server training statutes (e.g., Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 25681 — CA RBS Act)
  - 27 CFR § 31.221 (Federal occupational tax records)
  - OSHA 29 CFR § 1910.1030 (Bloodborne pathogens — bar incidents)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Server certification cards must bear issuer accreditation number
  - Training completed within state-mandated timeframe (e.g., CA: 60 days of hire)
  - Certification renewal per state cycle (typically 3-5 years)
  - Training records must include date, provider, score, certificate number
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: HR maintains certification roster; expiration alerts automated
  - S.o.C.-2: Training provider must be state-accredited; certificate serial tracked
  - S.o.C.-3: Certification status verifiable by enforcement upon request
- **Document Types Audited**: ABC compliance documents, food handler certifications (alcohol component)

### A1-003: Liquor License Renewal Specialist
- **Governing Body**: State ABC Boards; Local Planning/Zoning Commissions
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - State ABC renewal statutes (varies by jurisdiction)
  - Local conditional use permit ordinances
  - 27 U.S.C. § 203 (Federal basic permit requirements)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Renewal application filed within statutory window (typically 30-90 days before expiration)
  - Fee payment documented with receipt and confirmation number
  - Any ownership changes trigger new application, not renewal
  - Compliance history attached to renewal in states requiring clean record
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Renewal calendar with 90/60/30-day alerts
  - S.o.C.-2: Payment receipts retained for audit period (typically 7 years)
  - S.o.C.-3: Renewal status publicly verifiable through state ABC database
- **Document Types Audited**: Liquor license applications and renewals, ABC compliance documents

## A2. Food Safety & Health (4 Personas)

### A2-001: Food Safety Manager (CFPM)
- **Governing Body**: FDA; State/County Health Departments; Conference for Food Protection (CFP)
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - 21 CFR Parts 110-117 (Current Good Manufacturing Practice / HARPC)
  - FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA), Pub. L. 111-353
  - FDA Food Code (model code adopted by states)
  - Cal. Health & Safety Code §§ 113700-114437 (CA Retail Food Code)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - CFPM certification from ANSI-CFP accredited provider (ServSafe, NRFSP, Prometric)
  - At least one CFPM per establishment during all operating hours
  - HACCP plans documented with critical control points, limits, monitoring, corrective actions
  - Temperature logs maintained with time/date/initials
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: HACCP plans reviewed annually; staff access to food safety SOPs
  - S.o.C.-2: Temperature monitoring devices calibrated per manufacturer spec; logs immutable
  - S.o.C.-3: Health department inspection scores posted publicly (where required)
- **Document Types Audited**: Health department inspection reports, food handler certifications, catering permits

### A2-002: Health Inspector / Environmental Health Specialist (REHS)
- **Governing Body**: State/County Health Departments; NEHA (National Environmental Health Association)
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - FDA Food Code (2022 edition, adopted by reference in most states)
  - 42 U.S.C. § 243 (Federal authority for health regulations)
  - Cal. Health & Safety Code § 113725 et seq.
  - State REHS licensing statutes
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Inspection reports must use standardized form (FDA 3-purpose form or state equivalent)
  - Violations classified as critical/non-critical with corrective timeline
  - Follow-up inspection documented within mandated timeframe
  - Inspector must hold REHS credential or equivalent state certification
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Inspection scheduling randomized; inspector rotation policies
  - S.o.C.-2: Reports signed, dated, and delivered to permit holder at conclusion of inspection
  - S.o.C.-3: Inspection results publicly available (online databases, posted grades)
- **Document Types Audited**: Health department inspection reports, pool/spa health permits, bed bug disclosure documents

### A2-003: Food Handler Certification Administrator
- **Governing Body**: State Health Departments; ANSI-CFP; Local jurisdictions
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - State food handler card requirements (e.g., Cal. Health & Safety Code § 113948)
  - FDA Food Code § 2-102.12 (Certified Food Protection Manager)
  - County-specific training mandates
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Food handler card issued upon passing accredited exam
  - Card valid for state-mandated period (CA: 3 years)
  - Card must display: name, date, expiration, provider, accreditation number
  - Training must cover: foodborne illness, time/temperature, cross-contamination, hygiene, allergens
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Employer maintains copies; new hire training within statutory period (CA: 30 days)
  - S.o.C.-2: Provider accreditation verified through ANSI-CFP database
  - S.o.C.-3: Card presented to inspector upon request
- **Document Types Audited**: Food handler certifications

### A2-004: HACCP Plan Coordinator
- **Governing Body**: FDA; USDA-FSIS; Codex Alimentarius Commission
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - 21 CFR Part 120 (Juice HACCP)
  - 21 CFR Part 123 (Seafood HACCP)
  - 9 CFR Part 417 (USDA Meat/Poultry HACCP)
  - FSMA Preventive Controls Rule, 21 CFR Part 117
  - Codex Alimentarius CAC/RCP 1-1969, Rev. 4 (2003)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Written HACCP plan per product/process with 7 principles documented
  - Hazard analysis signed by HACCP-trained individual (minimum 16-hour curriculum)
  - Critical limits based on scientific evidence or regulatory standards
  - Verification procedures documented with frequency
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: HACCP plan access limited to food safety team; version control maintained
  - S.o.C.-2: Monitoring records retained per regulatory requirement (1-2 years minimum)
  - S.o.C.-3: Plan available for regulatory inspection within 24 hours of request
- **Document Types Audited**: Health department inspection reports, food handler certifications, catering permits

## A3. Building & Fire Safety (4 Personas)

### A3-001: Fire Marshal / Fire Prevention Inspector
- **Governing Body**: State Fire Marshal; NFPA (National Fire Protection Association); Local Fire Departments
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - NFPA 1 (Fire Code)
  - NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code)
  - NFPA 72 (National Fire Alarm Code)
  - NFPA 13 (Sprinkler Systems)
  - 29 CFR § 1910.36-39 (OSHA Means of Egress)
  - Cal. Health & Safety Code §§ 13100-13268 (CA State Fire Marshal)
  - IFC (International Fire Code) where adopted
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Inspection reports on standardized jurisdiction form
  - Occupancy load posted per NFPA 101 calculations
  - Fire alarm/suppression system test reports per NFPA 25 (ITM schedule)
  - Deficiency correction orders with compliance deadline
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Annual inspection schedule maintained; correction tracking system
  - S.o.C.-2: Inspection reports signed by certified inspector; system test records retained 5+ years
  - S.o.C.-3: Occupancy permits and inspection status available to public/guests
- **Document Types Audited**: Fire marshal inspection reports, building/occupancy permits, special event permits

### A3-002: Building Code Compliance Officer
- **Governing Body**: ICC (International Code Council); State/Local Building Departments
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - IBC (International Building Code)
  - IRC (International Residential Code)
  - State-adopted building codes (e.g., Cal. Building Code, Title 24)
  - 42 U.S.C. § 12183 (ADA new construction)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Certificate of Occupancy (CO) issued after final inspection
  - CO must state: occupancy classification, allowable occupant load, construction type
  - Building permits must precede any construction/renovation
  - Plan review approval documented before permit issuance
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Permit files retained for life of building; CO posted in visible location
  - S.o.C.-2: Inspection sequence documented (foundation → framing → final)
  - S.o.C.-3: Permit status searchable in public database
- **Document Types Audited**: Building/occupancy permits, fire marshal inspection reports

### A3-003: ADA Accessibility Compliance Specialist
- **Governing Body**: U.S. DOJ Civil Rights Division; U.S. Access Board; State equivalents
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - 42 U.S.C. §§ 12181-12189 (ADA Title III — Public Accommodations)
  - 28 CFR Part 36 (DOJ ADA Title III Regulations)
  - ADA Standards for Accessible Design (2010)
  - Cal. Civ. Code §§ 54-55.3 (Unruh Civil Rights Act / CA accessibility)
  - Cal. Gov. Code § 4450 et seq. (State accessibility standards)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - CASp (Certified Access Specialist) inspection report for CA properties
  - Accessibility survey documenting route, parking, entrance, restroom, signage compliance
  - Barrier removal plan with timeline for non-compliant elements
  - New construction must meet 2010 ADA Standards at time of CO
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Accessibility audit schedule maintained (recommended every 3 years)
  - S.o.C.-2: CASp report retained as litigation protection (Cal. Civ. Code § 55.53)
  - S.o.C.-3: Disability access notice posted per state requirement
- **Document Types Audited**: ADA accessibility compliance documents, building/occupancy permits

### A3-004: Elevator/Escalator Safety Inspector
- **Governing Body**: State Elevator Safety Boards; ASME (American Society of Mechanical Engineers)
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - ASME A17.1/CSA B44 (Safety Code for Elevators and Escalators)

  - S.o.C.-2: Inspection certificate original in cab; copies in management office
  - S.o.C.-3: Current certificate visible to all passengers
- **Document Types Audited**: Elevator/escalator inspection certificates, building/occupancy permits

## A4. Hospitality Operations & Tax (4 Personas)

### A4-001: Hotel Occupancy Tax Compliance Officer
- **Governing Body**: State/County/City Tax Authorities; IRS
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - State transient occupancy tax (TOT) statutes (e.g., Cal. Rev. & Tax Code § 7280 et seq.)
  - Local TOT ordinances (rates vary 6-15%+)
  - 26 U.S.C. § 7701 (Federal tax definitions)
  - IRS Rev. Proc. 2020-22 (Employment tax adjustments)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - TOT collected at point of sale; recorded per transaction
  - Monthly/quarterly returns filed per jurisdiction deadline
  - Registration certificate displayed at front desk
  - Exemption certificates (government, 30+ day stays) documented and retained
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Tax collection segregated in accounting; reconciliation monthly
  - S.o.C.-2: Returns filed with confirmation receipt; exemption docs retained 4+ years
  - S.o.C.-3: Registration certificate publicly displayed
- **Document Types Audited**: Hotel occupancy tax records, hotel/motel registration records

### A4-002: Tip Reporting Compliance Specialist
- **Governing Body**: IRS; DOL Wage and Hour Division
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - 26 U.S.C. § 6053 (Employee tip reporting)
  - IRS Form 8027 (Employer's Annual Information Return of Tip Income)
  - 26 CFR § 31.6053 (Tip reporting regulations)
  - 29 U.S.C. § 203(m) (Tip credit under FLSA)
  - DOL Final Rule on Tip Regulations (29 CFR Part 531)
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Form 8027 filed annually if establishment employs 10+ tipped employees
  - Employee tip reports (Form 4070) collected monthly
  - Tip allocation calculations documented when tips < 8% of gross receipts
  - Tip pool agreements in writing with employee acknowledgment
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Tip records maintained separately from payroll; 4-year retention
  - S.o.C.-2: 8027 filed by Feb 28 (paper) or Mar 31 (electronic); receipt retained
  - S.o.C.-3: Allocated tips reported on employee W-2
- **Document Types Audited**: Tip reporting documents (IRS 8027)

### A4-003: Guest Privacy & Data Protection Officer
- **Governing Body**: FTC; State AGs; PCI SSC; State privacy statutes
- **Key Governing Guidelines**:
  - 15 U.S.C. § 45 (FTC Act — Unfair/Deceptive Practices)
  - Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1798.100-1798.199.100 (CCPA/CPRA)
  - Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.81.5 (Data breach notification)
  - PCI-DSS v4.0 (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)
  - GDPR Articles 5-9 (for international guests)
  - State hotel guest record privacy statutes
- **Standards of Creation**:
  - Privacy policy posted at registration and on website
  - Guest consent obtained for data collection beyond stay requirements
  - Data breach response plan documented and tested annually
  - PCI-DSS SAQ completed annually (or ROC for Level 1 merchants)
- **SOC Controls**:
  - S.o.C.-1: Access to guest PII restricted by role; encryption at rest and in transit
  - S.o.C.-2: Breach notification within statutory window (CA: 72 hours to AG if 500+)
  - S.o.C.-3: Privacy policy publicly available; opt-out mechanism functional
- **Document Types Audited**: Guest privacy documents, hotel/motel registration records
