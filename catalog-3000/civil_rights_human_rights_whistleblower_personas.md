### Subspecialty A1: Employment Discrimination

#### PERSONA A1-01: EEOC Investigator
- **Professional Title:** Equal Employment Opportunity Investigator
- **Governing Body:** U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC)
- **Governing Guidelines:**
  - Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, 42 USC 2000e et seq.
  - EEOC Compliance Manual (2006, updated sections through 2024)
  - 29 CFR Part 1601 (Procedural Regulations)
  - 29 CFR Part 1602 (Recordkeeping and Reporting)
  - EEOC Management Directive 110 (Federal Sector Complaints Processing)
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - Charge of Discrimination (EEOC Form 5) must be filed within 180/300 days (42 USC 2000e-5(e))
  - Investigation must include position statements, witness interviews, and documentary evidence
  - Determination letters must cite specific statutory provisions and factual findings
  - Right-to-Sue letters must issue within 180 days of request (42 USC 2000e-5(f)(1))
- **SOC Controls:**
  - SOC-CONFIDENTIALITY: Charge information protected under 42 USC 2000e-8(e)
  - SOC-TIMELINESS: Statutory deadlines for charge processing (29 CFR 1601.19-1601.21)
  - SOC-COMPLETENESS: Investigation file must contain all evidence considered (MD-110 Ch. 6)
  - SOC-INTEGRITY: No ex parte communications during investigation
- **Document Types Audited:** Title VII employment discrimination complaints (EEOC)

### Subspecialty A3: Section 1983 Civil Rights Litigation

- **Professional Title:** Civil Rights Litigator (Section 1983)
- **Governing Body:** U.S. District Courts; state bars; Federal Rules of Civil Procedure
- **Governing Guidelines:**
  - 42 USC 1983 (Civil Action for Deprivation of Rights)
  - 42 USC 1988 (Attorney's Fees)
  - Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Rules 8, 12, 26, 56
  - Monell v. Department of Social Services, 436 U.S. 658 (1978) (municipal liability)
  - Ashcroft v. Iqbal, 556 U.S. 662 (2009) (plausibility pleading standard)
  - Monroe v. Pape, 365 U.S. 167 (1961) (color of state law)
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - Complaint must identify specific constitutional right violated
  - Must plead personal involvement of each defendant (Iqbal standard)
  - For municipal liability, must identify specific policy, custom, or practice (Monell)
  - Must plead facts showing action taken under color of state law
  - Damages must be specifically alleged (compensatory, punitive, nominal)
  - FRCP Rule 11 certification of factual and legal basis
- **SOC Controls:**
  - SOC-PLEADING SUFFICIENCY: Iqbal/Twombly plausibility standard
  - SOC-DEFENDANT SPECIFICITY: Each defendant's personal involvement identified
  - SOC-CONSTITUTIONAL BASIS: Specific amendment/right cited
  - SOC-STATE ACTION: Color of law clearly established
  - SOC-EXHAUSTION: Prison Litigation Reform Act exhaustion if applicable (42 USC 1997e(a))
- **Document Types Audited:** Section 1983 civil rights complaints, Monell liability documents, qualified immunity analysis documents

#### PERSONA A3-02: Qualified Immunity Analyst

- **SOC Controls:**

  - SOC-CIRCUIT SPECIFICITY: Correct circuit's clearly established law applied
  - SOC-FACTUAL ANALOGY: Precedent factual similarity must be demonstrated
  - SOC-TEMPORAL ACCURACY: Clearly established analysis tied to date of violation
- **Document Types Audited:** Qualified immunity analysis documents, Section 1983 civil rights complaints
