# Universal Persona Agent Catalog
# WASTE MANAGEMENT / WATER-WASTEWATER / PEST CONTROL / TOWING-VEHICLE RECOVERY

**Catalog Date:** 2026-03-22
**Governing Jurisdiction:** California (primary), Federal (secondary)
**Triple Constraint Applied:** Each persona evaluated against (1) Governing Guidelines, (2) Standards of Creation, (3) SOC Controls

---

## METHODOLOGY: THE TRIPLE CONSTRAINT

For every professional persona identified:

| Constraint | Definition | Audit Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Governing Guidelines** | Statutes, regulations, and administrative codes that define the profession and its document obligations | A document exists that falls under the professional's regulatory authority |
| **Standards of Creation** | Technical, procedural, and formatting requirements that govern HOW the document must be produced | The document was created, and the creation process itself must be auditable |
| **SOC Controls** | Segregation of concerns: who can create, who can approve, who can certify, who can file, and who must be independent | The document requires signature, certification, or attestation from a qualified individual |

A persona is cataloged when ALL THREE constraints produce document audit obligations.

## A1. SOLID WASTE FACILITY MANAGEMENT

### PERSONA A1-01: Solid Waste Facility Operator / Manager

- **Professional Title:** Solid Waste Facility Operator
- **Governing Body:** CalRecycle (Department of Resources Recycling and Recovery); Local Enforcement Agency (LEA)
- **Governing Guidelines:**
  - Cal. Public Resources Code (PRC) 44001-44018 (Solid Waste Facility Permits)
  - PRC 43200-43209 (LEA Certification and Enforcement)
  - PRC 40000 et seq. (Integrated Waste Management Act)
  - 27 CCR 20005-21900 (Minimum Standards for Solid Waste Handling and Disposal)
  - 40 CFR 258 (Federal Criteria for Municipal Solid Waste Landfills)
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - Facility permit applications per PRC 44004 (design/operation changes require permit amendment)
  - Operating records per 27 CCR 20510 (daily log, tonnage, special waste)
  - Monitoring reports per 27 CCR 20380-20435 (groundwater, surface water, vadose zone)
  - Closure/post-closure maintenance plans per 27 CCR 21769-21900
- **SOC Controls:**
  - Operator prepares reports; LEA reviews and issues permits (PRC 44009)
  - CalRecycle concurs or objects to LEA permit actions within 60 days (PRC 44009)
  - RWQCB issues separate waste discharge requirements (Water Code 13260)
  - Financial assurance reviewed independently (27 CCR 22200-22230)
- **Document Types Audited:**
  - Solid waste facility permits
  - Landfill monitoring reports
  - Closure/post-closure plans
  - Financial assurance documents
  - Waste characterization studies
  - Construction/demolition debris recycling compliance

### PERSONA A1-02: Landfill Environmental Compliance Officer

- **Professional Title:** Environmental Compliance Officer / Environmental Manager
- **Governing Body:** CalRecycle; RWQCB (Regional Water Quality Control Board); CalEPA
- **Governing Guidelines:**
  - PRC 43000-43101 (Regulatory Reform)
  - PRC 40060 (Landfill Siting Near Water)
  - Water Code 13260 (Waste Discharge Reports)
  - 27 CCR 20380-20435 (Environmental Monitoring)
  - CEQA: PRC 21151.1 (EIR for Solid Waste Facilities)
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - Groundwater monitoring reports per 27 CCR 20415 (quarterly/semi-annual)
  - Gas monitoring data per 27 CCR 20921-20935
  - Corrective action plans per 27 CCR 20430
  - CEQA documents per PRC 21151.1 (mandatory EIR for new/expanded facilities)
- **SOC Controls:**
  - Monitoring performed by facility; reviewed by LEA and RWQCB independently
  - Corrective action plans require RWQCB approval separate from LEA
  - Third-party laboratory analysis required for water quality samples
  - Professional Geologist or Engineer certification on monitoring reports
- **Document Types Audited:**
  - Landfill monitoring reports (groundwater, gas, surface water)
  - Waste characterization studies
  - Closure/post-closure plans
  - Financial assurance documents

## CROSS-SECTION REGULATORY OVERLAPS

1. **RWQCB** governs both waste facility discharge (Section A) and wastewater discharge (Section B)
2. **DPR** governs pesticide use across all pest control subspecialties (Section C) and intersects with hazardous waste when pesticide waste is generated (Section A)
3. **CHP** governs both tow operations (Section D) and hazardous waste transportation enforcement on highways (Section A)
4. **CUPA** (Certified Unified Program Agency per HSC 25404) consolidates hazardous materials, hazardous waste generator, underground storage tanks, and aboveground petroleum storage at the local level, touching Sections A and B
5. **Cal/OSHA** intersects with worker safety in pest control (Section C, C4-01), hazardous waste handling (Section A), and heavy tow operations (Section D)
6. **CEQA** requires environmental review for new waste facilities (Section A), water infrastructure (Section B), and water rights (Section B)
