# THE TRIPLE CONSTRAINT — Applied to Each Persona

For every persona identified below, three constraints bind simultaneously:

1. **GOVERNING GUIDELINES** — The law, regulation, or professional standard that creates the obligation
2. **STANDARDS OF CREATION** — The technical/procedural requirements the document must satisfy to be valid
3. **SOC CONTROLS** — The audit, retention, access, and integrity controls that protect the document lifecycle

A persona exists at the intersection of all three. If any constraint is absent, there is no auditable document obligation.

## A1. LICENSING & TAX COMPLIANCE (6 Personas)

### A1-01. Hotel Licensing Compliance Officer
- **Professional Title:** Hotel Licensing Compliance Officer
- **Governing Body:** California Department of Public Health (CDPH); Local City/County Business License Division
- **Key Governing Guidelines:**
  - Cal. Health & Safety Code §§ 17000–17062.5 (State Housing Law — hotels as employee housing and transient occupancy)
  - Cal. Health & Safety Code § 17921 (building standards for hotels)
  - Local municipal codes (e.g., SF Health Code Art. 1; LA Municipal Code Ch. V)
  - 42 USC § 2000a (Civil Rights Act — public accommodations)
- **Standards of Creation:** Application must include premises description, occupancy classification, fire/life safety certification, zoning confirmation, and current business tax certificate. Annual renewal with inspection clearance.
- **SOC Controls:** SOC 2 Type II — CC6.1 (logical/physical access to license records), CC7.2 (monitoring of license status changes), CC8.1 (change management for license conditions). Retention: permanent during operation + 7 years post-closure.
- **Document Types Audited:** Hotel operator license documents

- **Standards of Creation:** Monthly/quarterly TOT returns must include: gross room receipts, exempt receipts with documentation, net taxable receipts, tax calculated at applicable rate (typically 8–15.5%), and operator certification under penalty of perjury.
- **SOC Controls:** SOC 2 Type II — CC6.1 (access to financial records), CC7.1 (monitoring tax filing deadlines), PI1.1 (privacy of guest financial data). Retention: 7 years per Cal. CCP § 337 (statute of limitations on tax obligations).
