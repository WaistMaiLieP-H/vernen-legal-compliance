## THE TRIPLE CONSTRAINT FRAMEWORK

Every persona listed below exists because their professional standards create **document audit obligations** — meaning documents cannot be deemed compliant without verification against the standards this professional enforces.

- **Governing Guidelines (Law):** The statute or regulation that makes the document legally required
- **Standards of Creation (Blueprints):** The professional standard that defines HOW the document must be created
- **SOC (Proof):** The integrity/security framework that verifies the document was created correctly

# GROUP 1: PHYSICIAN AND CLINICAL DOCUMENTATION

## Persona 1: Attending Physician
- **Professional Title:** Attending Physician (MD/DO)
- **Governing Body:** State Medical Board; ABMS (American Board of Medical Specialties) for board certification
- **Governing Guidelines:**
  - 42 CFR Part 482 (Hospital Conditions of Participation — medical staff documentation requirements)
  - 42 CFR Part 485 (Critical Access Hospitals)
  - State Medical Practice Acts (each state)
  - 42 U.S.C. § 1395x (Medicare definitions of physician services)
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - AMA Code of Medical Ethics (Opinions 1.2.12, 3.3.1 — documentation integrity)
  - AMA CPT Documentation Guidelines (E/M documentation standards)
  - The Joint Commission (TJC) RC.01.02.01 (medical record entries must be authenticated)
  - CMS Conditions of Participation: physician must authenticate all orders and entries
- **Document Types Audited:** 1 (medical records — all types), 6 (mental health records — psychiatric), 8 (nursing notes — physician co-signature), 15 (informed consent), 16 (advance directives), 17 (death certificates — certifying physician), 25 (disability evaluations), 27 (IME)
- **Overlap:** Overlaps with Medical Records Director on documentation completeness; overlaps with Clinical Coder on E/M documentation sufficiency; unique authority as the authenticating clinician
