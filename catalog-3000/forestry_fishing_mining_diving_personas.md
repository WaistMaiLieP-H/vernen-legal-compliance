## THE TRIPLE CONSTRAINT

Every persona identified below satisfies ALL THREE constraints simultaneously:

1. **GOVERNING GUIDELINES** — A regulatory body publishes binding rules that create document obligations
2. **STANDARDS OF CREATION** — Professional or technical standards dictate HOW documents must be created, formatted, signed, and retained
3. **SOC CONTROLS** — Segregation of duties, access controls, review/approval chains, and audit trail requirements apply to those documents

A professional title appears ONLY if all three constraints generate document audit obligations.

## A1. Timber Harvest Planning & Operations

### PERSONA A1-001: Registered Professional Forester (RPF)
- **Governing Body:** California Board of Forestry and Fire Protection; Society of American Foresters (SAF)
- **Governing Guidelines:**
  - Cal. PRC 4582-4587 (Timber Harvest Plan preparation requirements)
  - Cal. PRC 4571-4576.1 (Timber Operator Licensing)
  - 14 CCR 895-1115 (California Forest Practice Rules)
  - 36 CFR 219 (National Forest System Land Management Planning)
  - SAF Code of Ethics and Standards of Practice
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - THPs must contain watershed assessment, cumulative impacts analysis, species surveys, and RPF certification signature
  - 14 CCR 1032-1038 (THP content requirements: silvicultural prescriptions, erosion control, watercourse protections)
  - Plans must be filed on Board-prescribed forms with original RPF stamp and signature
  - Minimum 3-year retention; THP monitoring reports retained for plan life plus 3 years
- **SOC Controls:**
  - RPF who prepares THP cannot also serve as CAL FIRE reviewing officer (segregation)
  - RPF license number must appear on all submitted documents
  - CAL FIRE Pre-Harvest Inspection (PHI) is independent verification
  - Multi-agency review (CDFW, RWQCB, CGS) creates three-party check
- **Document Types Audited:** Timber harvest plans, forest management plans, reforestation compliance documents, streamside management zone compliance, log scaling/grading documents

### PERSONA A1-002: Licensed Timber Operator (LTO)
- **Governing Body:** California Board of Forestry and Fire Protection
- **Governing Guidelines:**
  - Cal. PRC 4571 (license required before timber operations)
  - Cal. PRC 4572 (application form and content)
  - Cal. PRC 4573-4574 (denial, suspension, revocation grounds; 2-year validity)
  - 14 CCR 1020-1104 (Forest Practice Rules — operational standards)
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - License application on Board-prescribed form with proof of workers' comp insurance
  - Daily operations logs must reference THP number and specific harvest unit
  - Erosion control completion reports must be filed within 1 year of operations
  - Equipment maintenance and calibration logs for scaling equipment
- **SOC Controls:**
  - LTO cannot self-certify compliance with THP conditions (RPF or CAL FIRE inspector required)
  - LTO license number must accompany all load tickets and scaling records
  - Cal. PRC 4576.1 bars alter egos from operating under suspended license
- **Document Types Audited:** Timber operator license documents, log scaling/grading documents, forest practice rules compliance

# TRIPLE CONSTRAINT VERIFICATION SUMMARY

Each of the 53 personas above was verified against all three constraints:

**Constraint 1 — Governing Guidelines:** Every persona maps to specific statutory authority (USC, CFR, state code, or international convention) that MANDATES document creation.

**Constraint 2 — Standards of Creation:** Every persona maps to professional or technical standards (ADCI, AWS, MSHA forms, HACCP protocols, ASME PVHO-1, etc.) that dictate HOW documents must be created — format, content, signing authority, retention period.

**Constraint 3 — SOC Controls:** Every persona maps to at least one segregation of duties requirement:
- Preparer vs. reviewer separation (e.g., RPF cannot be CAL FIRE reviewer)
- Independent certification bodies (e.g., ADCI independent of employer)
- Multi-agency review chains (e.g., MSHA + state + operator three-way check)
- Temporal controls (e.g., training before assignment, pre-dive before dive)
- Information barriers (e.g., medical records to physician, fitness cert to employer)

No persona was included unless all three constraints independently generate document audit obligations.
