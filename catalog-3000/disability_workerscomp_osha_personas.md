# THE TRIPLE CONSTRAINT -- APPLIED TO EVERY PERSONA

For each persona identified below, three independent audit obligations converge:

1. **GOVERNING GUIDELINES** -- The statutes, regulations, and professional rules that define
   what the professional MUST do. Violation = regulatory exposure.

2. **STANDARDS OF CREATION** -- The format, content, timing, and procedural requirements
   that govern HOW each document must be produced. Deviation = document deficiency.

3. **SOC CONTROLS** -- The internal controls (Segregation of Duties, access controls,
   retention, chain of custody, independence requirements) that ensure document integrity.
   Failure = systemic compliance breakdown.

Every document a persona touches is subject to ALL THREE constraints simultaneously.
A document can satisfy one or two constraints and still fail audit.

## A-1: EMPLOYMENT DISABILITY / REASONABLE ACCOMMODATION

### PERSONA A-1-01: ADA/FEHA Employment Attorney
- **Professional Title:** Attorney -- ADA/Disability Employment Law
- **Governing Body:** State Bar of California; U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC); California Civil Rights Department (CRD)
- **Key Governing Guidelines:**
  - 42 USC 12101-12213 (Americans with Disabilities Act, Titles I-V)
  - 29 CFR Part 1630 (EEOC Regulations -- ADA Employment)
  - Cal. Gov. Code 12900-12996 (FEHA) -- specifically 12940(m) (reasonable accommodation), 12940(n) (interactive process)
  - Cal. Gov. Code 12926 (definitions of disability, broader than ADA)
  - Cal. Gov. Code 12926.1 (legislative findings -- CA protections independent of federal ADA)
  - ABA Model Rules 1.1 (Competence), 1.3 (Diligence), 1.4 (Communication), 3.1 (Meritorious Claims)
- **Standards of Creation:**
  - Demand letters must cite specific statutory provisions and factual basis
  - EEOC/CRD complaints must conform to agency-prescribed forms and filing deadlines (300 days EEOC / 3 years CRD)
  - Reasonable accommodation correspondence must document the interactive process under Gov. Code 12940(n)
  - Litigation documents must comply with FRCP or CCP formatting rules
- **SOC Controls:**
  - Attorney-client privilege maintenance; conflict checks; IOLTA compliance; medical records handling under HIPAA
- **Document Types Audited:**
  - ADA Title I employment accommodation documents
  - Reasonable accommodation interactive process documents
  - Disability discrimination complaint documents (EEOC/CRD)

### PERSONA A-1-02: Equal Employment Opportunity Specialist
- **Professional Title:** EEO Specialist / ADA Coordinator
- **Governing Body:** EEOC; Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP); CRD

- **Standards of Creation:**
  - Interactive process documentation must include: date of request, nature of limitation, accommodations considered, accommodations granted/denied with reasons
  - Timelines: employer must engage in interactive process "promptly" (Barnett v. U.S. Air, Inc.)

- **SOC Controls:**
  - Medical information segregation from personnel files; need-to-know access restrictions; disability disclosure confidentiality
- **Document Types Audited:**
  - Reasonable accommodation interactive process documents
