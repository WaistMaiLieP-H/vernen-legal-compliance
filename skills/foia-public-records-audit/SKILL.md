# FOIA Public Records Audit

**Purpose**

Systematically audit FOIA and public records requests, agency responses, redaction logs, and fee determinations to identify response deadline failures, improper exemption claims, inadequate searches, unlawful withholding, Vaughn index deficiencies, and fee waiver denials that violate the public's statutory right of access to government records under federal and California law.

## Governing Standards

### Federal Statutes

- **5 USC § 552** -- Freedom of Information Act (FOIA), full text
- **5 USC § 552(a)(2)** -- Affirmative disclosure and reading room requirements
- **5 USC § 552(a)(3)** -- Request processing requirements
- **5 USC § 552(a)(4)(A)** -- Fee provisions (commercial use, educational/scientific, news media, other)
- **5 USC § 552(a)(4)(A)(iii)** -- Fee waiver standard (likely to contribute significantly to public understanding)
- **5 USC § 552(a)(4)(B)** -- Judicial review and de novo standard
- **5 USC § 552(a)(6)(A)** -- 20 business day response deadline
- **5 USC § 552(a)(6)(B)** -- 10 business day extension for unusual circumstances
- **5 USC § 552(a)(6)(C)** -- Expedited processing
- **5 USC § 552(a)(6)(E)** -- Multitrack processing
- **5 USC § 552(b)(1)-(9)** -- Nine FOIA exemptions
- **5 USC § 552(b)(1)** -- Classified national security information
- **5 USC § 552(b)(2)** -- Internal personnel rules and practices
- **5 USC § 552(b)(3)** -- Statutory exemptions (Exemption 3 statutes)
- **5 USC § 552(b)(4)** -- Trade secrets and confidential commercial/financial information
- **5 USC § 552(b)(5)** -- Deliberative process, attorney-client, attorney work product privileges
- **5 USC § 552(b)(6)** -- Personal privacy
- **5 USC § 552(b)(7)** -- Law enforcement records (A through F)
- **5 USC § 552(b)(8)** -- Financial institution examination records
- **5 USC § 552(b)(9)** -- Geological and geophysical information
- **5 USC § 552(b)** -- Reasonably segregable standard (non-exempt portions must be disclosed)
- **5 USC § 552(c)** -- Exclusions (treatment records, informant records, classified FBI records)
- **5 USC § 552a** -- Privacy Act of 1974 (first-party access and amendment rights)
- **5 USC § 552a(b)** -- Conditions of disclosure under Privacy Act
- **5 USC § 552a(d)** -- Individual access and amendment rights
- **44 USC § 3101-3107** -- Federal Records Act (records management obligations)
- **OPEN Government Act of 2007** -- FOIA amendments (tracking numbers, FOIA Public Liaisons)
- **FOIA Improvement Act of 2016** -- Foreseeable harm standard, 25-year sunset on Exemption 5

### California Statutes

- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6250** -- Declaration of public policy (access to information)
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6251** -- Short title: California Public Records Act (CPRA)
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6252** -- Definitions (public records, state agency, local agency, writing)
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6253** -- Right to inspect and copy; 10-day response deadline
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6253(c)** -- 14-day extension for unusual circumstances
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6253.1** -- Agency duty to assist requesters
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6253.9** -- Electronic records and format requirements
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6254** -- Exemptions from disclosure (enumerated)
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6254(a)** -- Preliminary drafts and notes
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6254(b)** -- Records of pending litigation
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6254(c)** -- Personnel, medical, and similar files (privacy)
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6254(f)** -- Law enforcement investigatory records
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6254(k)** -- Records exempt by other statutes
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6254.5** -- Waiver of exemption by prior disclosure
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6255** -- Catch-all balancing test (public interest vs. nondisclosure interest)
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6258** -- Right to judicial enforcement; burden on agency
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6259** -- Court proceedings; attorney fees to prevailing requester
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 6270** -- Prohibition on requester identity inquiry (purpose-blind access)
- **Cal. Constitution Art. I, § 3(b)** -- Constitutional right of access to public records

### Federal Regulations and Guidance

- **28 CFR Part 16** -- DOJ FOIA regulations (model for other agencies)
- **DOJ Office of Information Policy (OIP) Guidance** -- Foreseeable harm standard implementation
- **OMB Memoranda** -- Government-wide FOIA policy guidance
- **Executive Order 13526** -- Classified National Security Information
- **Executive Order 12600** -- Predisclosure notification (submitter notice) for Exemption 4
- **Vaughn v. Rosen** -- Vaughn index requirement for in camera review justification

## Audit Triggers

- FOIA or CPRA request letter or submission
- Agency acknowledgment or tracking number assignment
- Agency determination letter (full grant, partial grant, full denial)
- Redacted records or withholding log
- Vaughn index or exemption justification
- Fee estimate or fee waiver request and determination
- Administrative appeal and appeal determination
- Expedited processing request and determination
- Agency search methodology description
- Glomar response (neither confirm nor deny)
- Referral or consultation correspondence between agencies
- FOIA litigation complaint or court order
- Agency annual FOIA report data
- Privacy Act access or amendment request
- Agency reading room or proactive disclosure records
- Requester correspondence challenging adequacy of response

## Audit Checklist

### Request Processing Compliance

- [ ] FOIA request acknowledged within 10 business days
- [ ] FOIA determination issued within 20 business days (5 USC § 552(a)(6)(A))
- [ ] Extension properly invoked and limited to 10 additional business days (unusual circumstances)
- [ ] Requester notified of right to seek assistance from FOIA Public Liaison
- [ ] CPRA response issued within 10 calendar days (Cal. Gov. Code § 6253)
- [ ] CPRA 14-day extension properly justified (unusual circumstances only)
- [ ] Multitrack processing queue assignment is reasonable
- [ ] Expedited processing requests decided within 10 calendar days
- [ ] Tracking number assigned and requester informed
- [ ] No improper inquiry into requester identity or purpose (Cal. Gov. Code § 6270)

### Search Adequacy

- [ ] Search reasonably calculated to uncover all responsive records
- [ ] All offices likely to have responsive records searched
- [ ] Electronic records and databases included in search
- [ ] Search terms and methodology documented
- [ ] Custodians of responsive records identified and tasked
- [ ] "No responsive records" determination supported by adequate search description
- [ ] Destroyed or transferred records accounted for with explanation

### Exemption Application

- [ ] Each withheld record or redaction tied to specific exemption with explanation
- [ ] Foreseeable harm analysis documented for each withholding (FOIA Improvement Act of 2016)
- [ ] Exemption 5 (deliberative process) applied only to pre-decisional, deliberative materials
- [ ] Exemption 5 materials older than 25 years released (sunset provision)
- [ ] Exemption 4 (trade secrets/commercial information) properly justified
- [ ] Submitter notice provided for Exemption 4 withholdings (EO 12600)
- [ ] Exemption 6 and 7(C) privacy balancing properly conducted
- [ ] Exemption 7 law enforcement threshold established (compiled for law enforcement purposes)
- [ ] Exemption 3 identifies specific withholding statute
- [ ] CPRA exemptions cite specific Cal. Gov. Code § 6254 subsection
- [ ] CPRA catch-all (§ 6255) balancing test explicitly conducted and documented
- [ ] No blanket exemption claims without document-by-document review

### Segregability and Redaction

- [ ] Reasonably segregable non-exempt information released (5 USC § 552(b))
- [ ] Redactions clearly marked with applicable exemption code
- [ ] Redaction log or Vaughn index provided for each withholding
- [ ] Vaughn index provides sufficient detail to permit judicial review
- [ ] Over-redaction of non-exempt material identified and challenged
- [ ] Metadata and embedded data considered for segregability

### Glomar Responses

- [ ] Glomar response (neither confirm nor deny existence) limited to Exemptions 1, 3, 6, 7(A), 7(C)
- [ ] Glomar justification articulates specific harm from confirming or denying
- [ ] Glomar not used as blanket avoidance of disclosure obligation
- [ ] CPRA equivalent: existence of records neither confirmed nor denied only where authorized

### Fee Determinations

- [ ] Requester fee category properly determined (commercial, educational, news media, other)
- [ ] Fee estimate provided before incurring costs exceeding requester's stated willingness to pay
- [ ] Fee waiver request evaluated under statutory standard (public understanding contribution)
- [ ] Fee waiver denial explains how each statutory factor was weighed
- [ ] CPRA: fees limited to direct costs of duplication (no search fees for public records)
- [ ] No fee used as deterrent to discourage access

### Administrative Appeals

- [ ] Appeal rights and procedures clearly stated in determination letter
- [ ] Appeal decided within 20 business days (FOIA)
- [ ] Appeal determination addresses each ground raised by requester
- [ ] Requester informed of right to judicial review and OGIS mediation services
- [ ] CPRA: requester informed of right to seek judicial enforcement (Cal. Gov. Code § 6258)

### Privacy Act Compliance (First-Party Requests)

- [ ] Privacy Act request properly identified and processed
- [ ] Identity verification procedures applied (not overly burdensome)
- [ ] Access provided to records in system of records
- [ ] Amendment request processed within 10 business days acknowledgment, 30 days determination
- [ ] Exemptions from Privacy Act access or amendment properly cited
- [ ] Accounting of disclosures provided upon request

### Proactive Disclosure and Reading Room

- [ ] Agency maintains electronic reading room with frequently requested records
- [ ] Records disclosed three or more times posted to reading room (5 USC § 552(a)(2))
- [ ] FOIA logs and annual reports publicly available
- [ ] Agency FOIA reference guide published and updated

## Output Protocol

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Document ID | Unique identifier for audited document |
| Document Type | Request / Acknowledgment / Determination / Redacted Record / Vaughn Index / Fee Waiver / Appeal / Glomar Response / Other |
| Agency Name | Federal agency, California state agency, or local agency |
| Jurisdiction | Federal (FOIA) / California (CPRA) / Both |
| Request Tracking Number | Agency-assigned tracking or case number |
| Statute Cited | Specific FOIA section, Privacy Act provision, or Cal. Gov. Code section |
| Finding | Narrative description of the compliance issue |
| Severity | CRITICAL / HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / INFORMATIONAL |
| Exemption Claimed | Specific exemption number(s) and justification adequacy |
| Deadline Violation | YES / NO -- with number of days overdue if applicable |
| Search Adequacy | ADEQUATE / INADEQUATE / INDETERMINATE |
| Segregability Failure | YES / NO -- whether non-exempt material was improperly withheld |
| Remediation | Required corrective action with regulatory citation |
| Litigation Risk | Likelihood of successful judicial challenge under de novo review |
| Attorney Fee Exposure | Whether prevailing requester is likely to recover fees |
| Evidence Reference | Page number, section, or clause in source document |
