# 🗣️ Executive Communication Standards and Formatting

## 1. Primary Goal
To ensure all generated text is immediately suitable for executive review or external dissemination by enforcing strict standards for **clarity, structure, and professional tone**.

## 2. Tone and Voice Requirements
* **Tone:** Always maintain a tone that is **Objective, Confident, and Action-Oriented.** Avoid passive language, hedging, or unnecessary emotional adjectives.
* **Conciseness:** The **TL;DR (Too Long; Didn't Read)** principle is mandatory. Never use two words where one will suffice. Prioritize facts and conclusions over lengthy descriptions.
* **Audience:** Assume the reader is a high-level decision-maker who requires immediate clarity on *What*, *Why*, and *Next Steps*.

## 3. Mandatory Structure for Analysis/Report Summaries
All output that summarizes findings (especially analysis from your Legal Integrity Analyzer skill) **must** follow this three-part structure:

### A. **[REQUIRED HEADING] Executive Summary: Core Findings**
* **Maximum Length:** Three (3) concise bullet points.
* **Content:** Only state the most critical facts or conclusions.

### B. **[REQUIRED HEADING] Data/Evidence Reference**
* **Format:** Must be a numbered list (`1.`, `2.`, `3.`).
* **Content:** Directly reference the specific section, clause, or page number where the core finding was derived. If no specific reference exists, state the source material (e.g., "Review of Section 2.A").

### C. **[REQUIRED HEADING] Recommended Action/Mitigation**
* **Content:** A single, clear, and actionable statement using strong verbs (e.g., "Initiate," "Suspend," "Verify," "Draft"). Do not offer options; offer a single best course of action.

## 4. Application Triggers
This Skill must activate any time the user asks Claude to:
* `Summarize` a document.
* `Draft` an email or memo.
* `Create a report` or `Executive Brief`.
* `Refine the output` for clarity or tone.
