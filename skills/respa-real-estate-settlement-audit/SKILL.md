# RESPA Real Estate Settlement Audit

**Purpose**

Identifies violations of federal and California real estate settlement and mortgage servicing laws by auditing settlement statements, escrow account analyses, servicing transfer notices, loss mitigation correspondence, qualified written requests, and affiliated business arrangements against RESPA, Regulation X, TRID integrated disclosures, and the California Homeowner Bill of Rights.

## Governing Standards

### Federal Statutes
- **12 USC § 2601** — Congressional findings and purpose (RESPA)
- **12 USC § 2602** — Definitions: federally related mortgage loan, servicer, settlement services
- **12 USC § 2603** — Uniform settlement statement (HUD-1 / Closing Disclosure under TRID)
- **12 USC § 2604** — Special information booklet (Your Home Loan Toolkit)
- **12 USC § 2605** — Servicing of mortgage loans and administration of escrow accounts
- **12 USC § 2605(a)** — Servicing disclosure statement (at application and at transfer)
- **12 USC § 2605(b)** — Notice of transfer of servicing: 15 days before effective date (transferor), 15 days after (transferee)
- **12 USC § 2605(e)** — Duty to respond to borrower inquiries (qualified written requests)
- **12 USC § 2605(f)** — Damages for violations: actual damages, additional damages up to $2,000 individual / $1M class, attorney fees
- **12 USC § 2605(g)** — Administration of escrow accounts
- **12 USC § 2605(k)** — Servicer prohibitions: force-placed insurance restrictions, late fee restrictions
- **12 USC § 2605(k)(1)** — No force-placed insurance if borrower has coverage
- **12 USC § 2607** — Prohibition against kickbacks and unearned fees
- **12 USC § 2607(a)** — No person shall give or accept any fee, kickback, or thing of value for referral of settlement service business
- **12 USC § 2607(b)** — No person shall give or accept any portion, split, or percentage of charges other than for services actually performed
- **12 USC § 2607(c)** — Permitted payments: reasonable compensation for services rendered, bona fide salary
- **12 USC § 2607(d)** — Criminal penalties: fine up to $10,000 and/or imprisonment up to 1 year
- **12 USC § 2608** — Seller prohibited from requiring buyer to use particular title insurer
- **12 USC § 2609** — Escrow accounts: limits on amounts retained, annual escrow analysis
- **12 USC § 2609(a)** — No excess escrow deposits at settlement or monthly
- **12 USC § 2609(b)** — Two-month cushion maximum
- **12 USC § 2610** — Prohibition of fees for preparation of certain documents (RESPA disclosures)
- **12 USC § 2614** — Statute of limitations: 1 year for § 2607 violations, 3 years for § 2605 violations
- **12 USC § 2617** — Authority of CFPB

- **Cal. Civil Code § 2924.12** — Material violation: injunctive relief before sale, damages after sale

- **Cal. Civil Code § 2924.17** — Servicer must review documents for accuracy and completeness before recording or filing
- **Cal. Civil Code § 2924.18** — Small servicer exemption provisions
- **Cal. Civil Code § 2924b** — Notice of default and notice of sale requirements

## Audit Checklist

### Settlement and Closing (12 USC § 2603 / 12 CFR §§ 1024.7-1024.10)

- [ ] Closing Disclosure (or HUD-1) accurately reflects all settlement charges
- [ ] Charges on CD match or fall within tolerance of Loan Estimate
- [ ] No charges for services not actually performed (unearned fees — § 2607(b))
- [ ] Settlement agent provided copy of CD/HUD-1 to borrower for inspection
- [ ] Special information booklet (Your Home Loan Toolkit) provided within 3 business days of application
- [ ] Servicing disclosure statement provided at application or within 3 business days
- [ ] Seller not required to use particular title insurance company (§ 2608)
- [ ] No fee charged for preparation of RESPA-required documents (§ 2610)

### Anti-Kickback and Referral Fee Provisions (12 USC § 2607 / 12 CFR § 1024.14)

- [ ] No fee, kickback, or thing of value exchanged for referral of settlement service business
- [ ] No fee splitting except for services actually performed
- [ ] Payments to employees are bona fide compensation (not referral incentives)
- [ ] Marketing arrangements do not constitute disguised referral fees
- [ ] Desk rental / co-marketing agreements do not provide value for referrals
- [ ] Computer loan origination system fees reasonable for services rendered

### Affiliated Business Arrangements (12 CFR § 1024.15)

- [ ] AfBA disclosure provided at or prior to time of referral
- [ ] Disclosure identifies affiliated relationship
- [ ] Disclosure provides estimated charge or range of charges
- [ ] Disclosure states consumer is not required to use affiliated provider
- [ ] No required use of affiliate as condition of loan or settlement
- [ ] Affiliate provides actual, necessary services (not sham arrangement)
- [ ] AfBA disclosed on appropriate form (Appendix D)

### Escrow Accounts (12 USC § 2609 / 12 CFR § 1024.17)

- [ ] Initial escrow deposit does not exceed amount needed plus 2-month cushion
- [ ] Monthly escrow payments calculated using aggregate accounting method
- [ ] Annual escrow analysis performed
- [ ] Annual escrow statement provided within 30 days of analysis completion
- [ ] Surplus (> $50): returned to borrower within 30 days of analysis
- [ ] Shortage: borrower given option to pay over 12 months
- [ ] Deficiency (≤ 1 month escrow payment): borrower given option to pay over 30 days or 12 months
- [ ] Deficiency (> 1 month): borrower given option to pay over at least 12 months
- [ ] No requirement to deposit more than amounts permitted under § 2609
- [ ] Escrow account properly established for higher-priced mortgage loans

### Servicing Transfer (12 USC § 2605(b) / 12 CFR § 1024.33)

- [ ] Transferor (old servicer) provides notice at least 15 days before transfer effective date
- [ ] Transferee (new servicer) provides notice within 15 days after transfer effective date
- [ ] Combined notice permitted if sent at least 15 days before effective date
- [ ] Notice includes: effective date, old servicer name/address/phone, new servicer name/address/phone
- [ ] Notice includes: date old servicer stops accepting payments, date new servicer starts
- [ ] 60-day safe harbor: no late fees during transfer period if payment sent to wrong servicer
- [ ] Transfer of escrow account funds properly handled
- [ ] Loss mitigation applications in process at time of transfer: new servicer obtains documents and continues evaluation

### Qualified Written Requests and Error Resolution (12 CFR §§ 1024.35-1024.36)

- [ ] Written acknowledgment of QWR/NOE within 5 business days of receipt
- [ ] Substantive response within 30 business days (one 15-day extension permitted with written notice)
- [ ] Response includes: correction of error, or explanation why account is correct, or statement that information is unavailable
- [ ] No adverse credit reporting during error investigation period
- [ ] Account not placed in foreclosure during QWR response period
- [ ] Information request response within 30 business days (one 15-day extension)
- [ ] Contact information for further assistance provided
- [ ] No charge to borrower for responding to QWR

### Force-Placed Insurance (12 CFR § 1024.37)

- [ ] First notice sent at least 45 days before charging for force-placed insurance
- [ ] First notice includes: borrower's insurance obligation, servicer's authority to force-place, estimated cost, how to avoid
- [ ] Second (reminder) notice sent at least 30 days after first, before charging
- [ ] No force-placed insurance charged if borrower provides evidence of coverage
- [ ] Force-placed insurance canceled and premiums refunded within 15 days of receiving evidence of borrower's coverage
- [ ] Force-placed insurance charges are bona fide and reasonable
- [ ] No force-placed insurance that includes coverage for servicer's interest at borrower's expense beyond dwelling coverage

### Loss Mitigation (12 CFR § 1024.41 / Cal. Civil Code §§ 2923.4-2924.12)

- [ ] Servicer has policies for identifying and evaluating all loss mitigation options
- [ ] Acknowledgment of receipt of loss mitigation application within 5 business days
- [ ] Exercise reasonable diligence to obtain complete application
- [ ] Complete application evaluated within 30 days
- [ ] Borrower notified of determination in writing: approval, denial (with reasons), or incomplete
- [ ] Denial includes specific reasons and notice of right to appeal within 14 days
- [ ] Appeal decided within 30 days by different personnel than initial evaluation
- [ ] No dual tracking: no foreclosure referral if complete application received 37+ days before sale
- [ ] No dual tracking: no foreclosure sale if complete application pending
- [ ] California HBOR: single point of contact assigned (§ 2923.7)
- [ ] California HBOR: dual tracking prohibition (§ 2923.6)
- [ ] California HBOR: contact borrower to explore options 30 days before recording NOD (§ 2923.55)
- [ ] California HBOR: written acknowledgment of loss mitigation application within 5 business days (§ 2924.10)
- [ ] California HBOR: servicer review documents for accuracy before recording (§ 2924.17)

### Early Intervention and Continuity of Contact (12 CFR §§ 1024.39-1024.40)

- [ ] Good faith effort to establish live contact by 36th day of delinquency
- [ ] Written notice of loss mitigation options by 45th day of delinquency
- [ ] Written notice includes: account information, loss mitigation options, how to apply, loss mitigation application
- [ ] Single point of contact (SPOC) accessible to borrower
- [ ] SPOC has access to current loan information
- [ ] SPOC can take action on borrower's account (or timely coordinate with those who can)
- [ ] SPOC provides timely, accurate information about loss mitigation options and status

### Periodic Statements (12 CFR § 1026.41 — TILA/Reg Z but RESPA-related)

- [ ] Monthly periodic statement provided for each billing cycle
- [ ] Statement includes: amount due, due date, late fee, past due amount, total outstanding
- [ ] Statement includes: payment breakdown (principal, interest, escrow, fees)
- [ ] Statement includes: transaction activity since last statement
- [ ] Statement includes: partial payment information
- [ ] Delinquency information: date borrower became delinquent, total amount past due, notice of possible foreclosure
- [ ] Loss mitigation information: available programs, how to apply, CFPB contact info

## Output Protocol

Each finding must be recorded in the following format:

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Finding ID** | RESPA-YYYY-NNN |
| **Document Reviewed** | [filename / document title] |
| **Date of Document** | [date on document face] |
| **Audit Date** | [date audit performed] |
| **Statute Violated** | [specific USC section, Reg X section, or Cal. Civil Code section] |
| **Violation Category** | Settlement / Kickback / AfBA / Escrow / Servicing Transfer / QWR / Force-Placed Insurance / Loss Mitigation / Dual Tracking / Early Intervention |
| **Party at Fault** | [servicer name, settlement agent, referral source] |
| **Severity** | Critical / High / Medium / Low |
| **Finding Description** | [plain-language description of the violation] |
| **Evidence** | [quoted text, calculations, timeline, or absence noted] |
| **Legal Requirement** | [what the statute or regulation requires] |
| **Actual Condition** | [what the document shows or fails to show] |
| **Financial Impact** | [dollar amount of overcharge, escrow error, or borrower harm] |
| **Timeline Compliance** | [required deadline vs. actual response date] |
| **Remediation** | [specific corrective action required] |
| **Damages Exposure** | [actual damages + up to $2,000 statutory (individual) / $1M (class) + attorney fees] |
| **Criminal Exposure** | [§ 2607: fine up to $10,000 / imprisonment up to 1 year, if applicable] |
| **Cross-References** | [related TILA findings, escrow analyses, or prior QWRs] |
