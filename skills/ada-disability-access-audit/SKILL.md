# ADA Disability Access Audit

**Purpose**

Identifies violations of federal and California disability rights laws by auditing employment policies, accommodation requests, public accommodation records, government service documentation, and digital accessibility against the ADA, Rehabilitation Act, FEHA, Unruh Civil Rights Act, and applicable design standards.

## Governing Standards

- **42 USC § 12112** — Discrimination prohibited: hiring, advancement, discharge, compensation, training
- **42 USC § 12112(b)(5)** — Failure to make reasonable accommodations
- **42 USC § 12112(d)** — Medical examinations and inquiries (pre-offer, post-offer, employment)
- **42 USC § 12113** — Defenses: qualification standards, direct threat, religious entities
- **42 USC § 12131-12165** — Title II: Public services (state and local government)
- **42 USC § 12132** — No qualified individual excluded from services, programs, or activities
- **42 USC § 12134** — Regulations by Attorney General for Title II
- **42 USC § 12141-12150** — Title II Subtitle B: Public transportation
- **42 USC § 12181-12189** — Title III: Public accommodations and services operated by private entities
- **42 USC § 12182** — Prohibition of discrimination by public accommodations
- **42 USC § 12182(b)(2)(A)(ii)** — Failure to make reasonable modifications in policies
- **42 USC § 12182(b)(2)(A)(iii)** — Failure to provide auxiliary aids and services
- **42 USC § 12182(b)(2)(A)(iv)** — Failure to remove architectural barriers where readily achievable
- **42 USC § 12183** — New construction and alterations (readily accessible and usable)
- **42 USC § 12188** — Enforcement: private civil actions, AG pattern/practice suits
- **42 USC § 12203** — Retaliation and coercion prohibited
- **29 USC § 701 et seq** — Rehabilitation Act of 1973
- **29 USC § 794** — Section 504: nondiscrimination in federally funded programs
- **29 USC § 794d** — Section 508: electronic and information technology accessibility

## Audit Triggers

- Reasonable accommodation request or denial letter
- Interactive process documentation or correspondence
- Job descriptions and essential functions analysis
- Medical examination or fitness-for-duty evaluation
- Architectural plans or site survey for public accommodation
- Website, mobile application, or kiosk accessibility report
- Public entity program accessibility self-evaluation or transition plan
- Service animal policy or incident report
- Disability-related complaint or charge filed with EEOC, CRD, or DOJ
- Employment termination involving employee with known disability
- Leave of absence documentation (FMLA/CFRA intersection)
- Facility inspection or ADA compliance survey
- Parking, signage, or wayfinding documentation
- Auxiliary aids and services request or denial
- Construction or renovation permits for places of public accommodation
- Digital accessibility audit report (VPAT, WCAG conformance)

## Audit Checklist

### Title I: Employment Discrimination

- [ ] Employer has 15+ employees (ADA) or 5+ employees (FEHA)
- [ ] Job descriptions accurately identify essential functions
- [ ] Pre-employment inquiries do not ask about disability or medical history
- [ ] Conditional job offers precede any medical examinations
- [ ] Post-offer medical exams are uniformly required for all entering employees in same job category
- [ ] Disability-related inquiries during employment are job-related and consistent with business necessity
- [ ] Qualification standards do not screen out individuals with disabilities unless job-related
- [ ] Direct threat determination based on individualized assessment, not generalized assumptions

### Reasonable Accommodation (Interactive Process)

- [ ] Accommodation request recognized regardless of form (oral, written, third party)
- [ ] Interactive process initiated promptly upon request or known need
- [ ] Interactive process documented at each step
- [ ] Employer identified and considered all possible accommodations
- [ ] Employee given opportunity to suggest preferred accommodation
- [ ] Chosen accommodation is effective in enabling performance of essential functions
- [ ] Undue hardship analysis documented with specific cost/resource data (if accommodation denied)
- [ ] Reassignment to vacant position considered as accommodation of last resort
- [ ] Temporary accommodations provided during interactive process if feasible
- [ ] No retaliation for requesting accommodation (42 USC § 12203)
- [ ] FEHA interactive process engaged in timely, good faith manner (Gov. Code § 12940(n))

### Title II: State and Local Government Services

- [ ] Self-evaluation of programs, services, and activities completed
- [ ] Transition plan developed for structural changes needed (50+ employees)
- [ ] Designated ADA coordinator (50+ employees)
- [ ] Grievance procedure adopted and published
- [ ] Programs accessible when viewed in their entirety (program accessibility standard)
- [ ] Effective communication provided (qualified interpreters, auxiliary aids)
- [ ] Modifications to policies and procedures made unless fundamental alteration
- [ ] Surcharges not imposed on individuals with disabilities for accommodation costs

### Title III: Public Accommodations

- [ ] Facility meets 2010 ADA Standards for Accessible Design (new construction/alterations after 3/15/2012)
- [ ] Barrier removal in existing facilities where readily achievable
- [ ] Barrier removal priorities: (1) entrance, (2) goods/services access, (3) restrooms, (4) other
- [ ] Alternative methods of service provided when barrier removal not readily achievable
- [ ] Reasonable modifications to policies, practices, and procedures
- [ ] Auxiliary aids and services provided for effective communication
- [ ] No surcharge for furnishing auxiliary aids or making modifications
- [ ] Service animals permitted in all areas open to the public
- [ ] Only two permissible service animal inquiries made (task trained, what task)
- [ ] Emotional support animals distinguished from service animals under ADA

### Architectural and Physical Access

- [ ] Accessible parking: number, dimensions, signage, access aisle, van-accessible
- [ ] Accessible route from parking to entrance (firm, stable, slip-resistant, 36" minimum)
- [ ] Entrance doors: 32" clear width, hardware operable without grasping/twisting, 5 lb max force
- [ ] Interior circulation: corridors 36" min, protruding objects max 4" (27"-80" height range)
- [ ] Restrooms: turning space, grab bars, lavatory clearance, mirror height, accessible dispensers
- [ ] Signage: raised characters, Braille, non-glare, proper mounting height and location
- [ ] Elevators: car dimensions, controls height, audible/visual indicators, Braille floor designations
- [ ] Counter heights: 36" max for sales/service counters, or equivalent facilitation
- [ ] Assembly areas: dispersed wheelchair spaces, companion seats, lines of sight

### Digital Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 Level AA)

- [ ] Perceivable: text alternatives for images, captions for video, color not sole means of conveying information
- [ ] Operable: keyboard navigable, no keyboard traps, sufficient time limits, no seizure-inducing content
- [ ] Understandable: readable text, predictable navigation, input assistance for forms
- [ ] Robust: compatible with assistive technologies, valid markup
- [ ] Voluntary Product Accessibility Template (VPAT) completed and current
- [ ] Website accessibility statement published with contact information
- [ ] Third-party content and embedded media meet same standards
- [ ] Mobile application accessibility tested

### California-Specific Requirements

- [ ] FEHA broader disability definition applied (limits rather than substantially limits)
- [ ] Two-step test for FEHA disability: (1) physiological condition affecting body system, (2) limits major life activity
- [ ] Employer aware of interactive process obligation under Gov. Code § 12940(n)
- [ ] CASp (Certified Access Specialist) inspection obtained (optional but provides litigation benefits)
- [ ] Unruh Civil Rights Act minimum statutory damages ($4,000 per violation) noted
- [ ] Construction-related accessibility claim procedures followed (Civil Code § 55.3)

|---|---|
| **Finding ID** | ADA-YYYY-NNN |

| **Readily Achievable** | Yes / No / Requires Analysis |

| **Cross-References** | [related findings or prior audit items] |
