# Nonprofit 501(c)(3) Compliance Audit

## Purpose

Passive audit layer. When user submits Form 990 returns, bylaws, articles of incorporation, board minutes, conflict of interest policies, solicitation materials, or financial statements, audit against IRS tax-exempt requirements, California nonprofit statutes, and AG registration requirements for organizational compliance, governance adequacy, and reporting accuracy.

## Governing Standards

### Federal

- **26 USC § 501(c)(3)** — Tax-exempt status requirements (organized and operated exclusively for exempt purposes)
- **26 USC § 501(c)(3) (last sentence)** — Prohibition on private inurement
- **26 USC § 4958** — Excess benefit transactions / intermediate sanctions (excise taxes on insiders)
- **26 USC § 4958(c)(1)** — Definition of excess benefit transaction
- **26 USC § 4958(f)(1)** — Disqualified persons
- **26 USC § 6033** — Annual information return filing requirements (Form 990)
- **26 USC § 501(h)** — Lobbying expenditure election (§ 501(h) election)
- **26 USC § 4911** — Excise tax on excess lobbying expenditures
- **26 USC § 501(c)(3)** — Absolute prohibition on political campaign intervention
- **26 USC § 511-514** — Unrelated business income tax (UBIT)
- **26 USC § 170(f)(8)** — Substantiation requirements for charitable contributions ($250+)
- **26 USC § 6104(d)** — Public disclosure requirements (Form 990, application for exemption)
- **26 USC § 6710** — Penalties for failure to disclose
- **26 USC § 4966-4967** — Donor advised fund requirements
- **Rev. Proc. 2021-5** — Application procedures for tax-exempt status
- **IRS Rev. Rul. 69-545** — Community benefit standard (hospitals)

### California

- **Cal. Government Code § 12580-12599.10** — Supervision of Trustees and Charitable Trusts (AG registration)
- **Cal. Government Code § 12586** — Annual reporting requirements to AG (Form RRF-1, Form CT-TR-1)
- **Cal. Government Code § 12599-12599.7** — Commercial fundraiser and fundraising counsel registration
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 5000-5080** — Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation Law
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 5110-5120** — Formation requirements
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 5210-5260** — Directors and governance
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 5230** — Duty of care and duty of loyalty
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 5233** — Self-dealing transactions (more restrictive than IRS)
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 5250** — Indemnification of directors and officers
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 6320** — Member rights (if membership corporation)
- **Cal. Revenue & Taxation Code § 23701(d)** — California income tax exemption (FTB)
- **Cal. Business & Professions Code § 17510-17510.95** — Charitable solicitation disclosures

### Regulatory

- **IRS Form 990 / 990-EZ / 990-N** — Annual information return requirements
- **IRS Form 990 Schedule A** — Public charity status and public support test
- **IRS Form 990 Schedule B** — Schedule of contributors (confidential)
- **IRS Form 990 Schedule J** — Compensation information for certain officers/directors
- **IRS Form 990 Schedule L** — Transactions with interested persons
- **IRS Form 990 Schedule O** — Supplemental information
- **IRS Form 1023 / 1023-EZ** — Application for recognition of tax-exempt status
- **IRS Publication 557** — Tax-Exempt Status for Your Organization
- **FASB ASC 958** — Not-for-profit entity financial reporting standards

## Audit Triggers

- Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N annual returns
- Form 990 schedules (A, B, J, L, O, and others)
- Articles of incorporation or amendments
- Bylaws or bylaw amendments
- Board meeting minutes
- Conflict of interest policies and annual disclosure forms
- Financial statements (audited or reviewed)
- Solicitation materials (direct mail, online, events)
- Grant agreements or reports
- Executive compensation documentation
- California Form RRF-1 (annual registration renewal)
- Commercial fundraiser contracts

## Audit Checklist

### Organizational Test
- [ ] Verify articles of incorporation limit purposes to one or more exempt purposes under § 501(c)(3)
- [ ] Confirm articles include dissolution clause directing assets to another § 501(c)(3) or government entity
- [ ] Check that articles do not authorize non-exempt activities beyond insubstantial level
- [ ] Verify IRS determination letter on file and current
- [ ] Confirm California FTB exemption (§ 23701(d)) obtained and maintained
- [ ] Assess whether any amendments to articles affect exempt status
- [ ] Verify EIN is active and entity is in good standing with Secretary of State

### Operational Test
- [ ] Assess whether organization is operated exclusively (primarily) for exempt purposes
- [ ] Evaluate whether any substantial part of activities constitutes carrying on propaganda or influencing legislation
- [ ] Verify absolute compliance with political campaign intervention prohibition
- [ ] Assess private benefit — whether any private interests are served more than insubstantially
- [ ] Check for private inurement — whether any insider receives unreasonable compensation or benefits
- [ ] Review program vs administrative vs fundraising expense ratios
- [ ] Evaluate whether any commercial activities undermine primary exempt purpose

### Governance and Board Oversight
- [ ] Verify board size and composition meet bylaws requirements
- [ ] Assess board independence (majority independent directors recommended)
- [ ] Confirm conflict of interest policy adopted and annually disclosed by all board members/officers
- [ ] Check that board meets with adequate frequency (at least quarterly recommended)
- [ ] Verify minutes document deliberation and voting on material decisions
- [ ] Assess whether board reviews and approves Form 990 before filing
- [ ] Confirm whistleblower policy adopted
- [ ] Verify document retention and destruction policy adopted
- [ ] Check executive compensation review process (comparable data, contemporaneous documentation, independent body approval)

### Excess Benefit Transactions (§ 4958)
- [ ] Identify all transactions between organization and disqualified persons
- [ ] Assess whether compensation to any disqualified person exceeds fair market value
- [ ] Verify rebuttable presumption of reasonableness procedures followed (independent body, comparable data, contemporaneous documentation)
- [ ] Review Form 990 Schedule J for reportable compensation
- [ ] Review Form 990 Schedule L for loans, grants, and business transactions with interested persons
- [ ] Assess any below-market sales or leases to insiders
- [ ] Calculate potential excise tax exposure: 25% initial tax + 200% additional tax on uncorrected excess benefit

### Form 990 Reporting Accuracy
- [ ] Verify Form 990 filed timely (15th day of 5th month after fiscal year end)
- [ ] Confirm correct form used (990 vs 990-EZ vs 990-N based on gross receipts and assets)
- [ ] Verify Part I summary information matches underlying financial statements
- [ ] Check Part III program service accomplishments for accuracy and completeness
- [ ] Verify Part IV checklist responses trigger correct schedule attachments
- [ ] Confirm Part VII compensation disclosures complete for all required persons
- [ ] Verify Part VIII revenue properly classified (program service vs contributions vs investment)
- [ ] Assess Part IX expense allocation methodology (program vs management vs fundraising)

### Public Support Test
- [ ] Determine public charity classification: § 509(a)(1) vs § 509(a)(2) vs § 509(a)(3)
- [ ] Calculate public support percentage on Schedule A
- [ ] Verify organization meets 33-1/3% public support test or 10% facts-and-circumstances test
- [ ] Assess unusual grants exclusion if applicable
- [ ] Identify risk of reclassification to private foundation if support test failed
- [ ] Verify gross receipts test compliance if § 509(a)(2) organization

### Unrelated Business Income (UBIT)
- [ ] Identify all revenue sources and assess whether any constitute unrelated trade or business
- [ ] Verify Form 990-T filed if gross unrelated business income exceeds $1,000
- [ ] Assess applicability of UBIT modifications (volunteer labor, donated goods, convenience)
- [ ] Check advertising revenue in publications for UBIT exposure
- [ ] Evaluate rental income — debt-financed property triggers UBIT
- [ ] Assess sponsorship vs advertising distinction (qualified sponsorship payment exception)
- [ ] Calculate net unrelated business taxable income per activity (post-TCJA siloing requirement)

### California AG Registration and Solicitation
- [ ] Verify initial registration with California AG Registry of Charitable Trusts (Form CT-1)
- [ ] Confirm annual renewal filed (Form RRF-1) with required financial attachments
- [ ] Verify registration fee paid based on gross annual revenue
- [ ] Confirm compliance with commercial fundraiser registration if third-party solicitors used
- [ ] Check solicitation disclosures: organization name, purpose, whether contribution is tax-deductible
- [ ] Verify compliance with charitable solicitation point-of-solicitation disclosures (B&P § 17510)
- [ ] Assess whether organization files with other states where soliciting (multi-state registration)
