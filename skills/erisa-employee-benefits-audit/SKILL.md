# ERISA Employee Benefits Audit

**Purpose**

Systematically audit employee benefit plan documents, summary plan descriptions, claims correspondence, and fiduciary communications to identify ERISA compliance failures, fiduciary duty breaches, prohibited transactions, vesting violations, and claims procedure deficiencies that expose plan sponsors, administrators, and fiduciaries to federal enforcement, participant lawsuits, and excise tax liability.

## Governing Standards

### Federal Statutes

- **29 USC § 1001 et seq.** -- Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 (ERISA), full text
- **29 USC § 1021-1031** -- Reporting and Disclosure requirements (SPDs, SARs, annual reports)
- **29 USC § 1051-1061** -- Participation and Vesting standards
- **29 USC § 1081-1085a** -- Funding requirements for defined benefit plans
- **29 USC § 1101-1114** -- Fiduciary Responsibility (prudence, loyalty, diversification, plan document)
- **29 USC § 1104** -- Fiduciary duties: prudent man standard of care
- **29 USC § 1106** -- Prohibited transactions
- **29 USC § 1107** -- Limitation on acquisition of employer securities and real property
- **29 USC § 1109** -- Liability for breach of fiduciary duty
- **29 USC § 1132** -- Civil enforcement (participant right to sue)
- **29 USC § 1133** -- Claims procedure requirements (full and fair review)
- **29 USC § 1161-1169** -- COBRA continuation coverage
- **26 USC § 401-417** -- IRC qualified plan requirements (tax-qualified retirement plans)
- **26 USC § 401(a)** -- Qualification requirements for pension, profit-sharing, and stock bonus plans
- **26 USC § 401(k)** -- Cash or deferred arrangements (401(k) plans)
- **26 USC § 404** -- Deduction limits for employer contributions
- **26 USC § 410** -- Minimum participation standards
- **26 USC § 411** -- Minimum vesting standards
- **26 USC § 414** -- Controlled group and affiliated service group rules
- **26 USC § 415** -- Limitations on benefits and contributions
- **26 USC § 416** -- Top-heavy plan requirements
- **26 USC § 4975** -- Excise tax on prohibited transactions
- **26 USC § 4980H** -- Employer shared responsibility (ACA employer mandate)

## Audit Triggers

- Summary Plan Description (SPD) or Summary of Material Modifications (SMM)
- Plan document or adoption agreement
- Form 5500 annual report or Schedule C (service provider compensation)
- Benefit claims denial letter or Explanation of Benefits (EOB)
- Internal appeals decision or external review determination
- COBRA election notice or qualifying event correspondence
- Fiduciary committee meeting minutes
- Investment policy statement (IPS) or fund lineup change documentation
- Fee disclosure documents (404a-5 participant notice, 408(b)(2) service provider disclosure)
- Plan amendment or restatement
- Distribution or loan documentation
- QDRO determination letter
- Employer communications regarding plan changes or termination
- DOL or IRS audit correspondence
- Actuarial valuation report (defined benefit plans)
- Nondiscrimination testing results (ADP/ACP, coverage, top-heavy)
- Participant complaint or grievance correspondence
- Vendor or TPA service agreement

## Audit Checklist

### Plan Document Compliance

- [ ] Plan document exists and is current (restated within required cycle)
- [ ] Plan document contains all required provisions under 29 USC § 1102
- [ ] Named fiduciary is designated in the plan document
- [ ] Plan amendment procedures are documented and followed
- [ ] Plan document and SPD are consistent (no conflicts)
- [ ] Adoption agreement matches prototype/volume submitter terms
- [ ] IRS determination or opinion letter is current

### Summary Plan Description (SPD) Requirements

- [ ] SPD contains all elements required by 29 CFR § 2520.102-3
- [ ] SPD is written in a manner calculated to be understood by average participant
- [ ] SPD accurately describes eligibility, vesting, benefits, and claims procedures
- [ ] SPD was distributed within 90 days of coverage or 120 days of plan establishment
- [ ] Summary of Material Modifications (SMM) issued within 210 days of plan year end for changes
- [ ] SPD describes COBRA rights and procedures
- [ ] SPD describes QDRO procedures
- [ ] SPD identifies plan administrator, trustee, and agent for service of process

### Fiduciary Duty Compliance

- [ ] Fiduciaries identified and aware of their status
- [ ] Fiduciary committee meets regularly with documented minutes
- [ ] Investment decisions reflect prudent man standard (29 USC § 1104(a)(1)(B))
- [ ] Plan assets used solely for the exclusive benefit of participants and beneficiaries
- [ ] Plan expenses are reasonable and properly allocated
- [ ] Fee benchmarking conducted at regular intervals
- [ ] Service provider compensation reviewed and documented
- [ ] 408(b)(2) fee disclosures received and reviewed from covered service providers
- [ ] 404a-5 participant fee disclosures distributed annually and upon enrollment
- [ ] Investment policy statement (IPS) exists and is followed
- [ ] Fund lineup monitored and underperforming funds addressed
- [ ] ERISA fidelity bond in place covering plan fiduciaries (29 USC § 1112)

### Prohibited Transaction Review

- [ ] No transactions between plan and parties in interest (29 USC § 1106(a))
- [ ] No self-dealing by fiduciaries (29 USC § 1106(b))
- [ ] Applicable prohibited transaction exemptions (PTEs) properly documented
- [ ] Employer securities and real property within 10% limitation (29 USC § 1107)
- [ ] No loans to parties in interest outside statutory exemptions
- [ ] Form 5500 Schedule G properly reports nonexempt transactions

### Vesting and Participation

- [ ] Minimum participation age and service requirements met (26 USC § 410)
- [ ] Entry dates comply with statutory maximums
- [ ] Vesting schedule complies with 26 USC § 411 (cliff or graded)
- [ ] Hours of service properly counted (elapsed time or counting hours method)
- [ ] Breaks in service properly tracked and applied
- [ ] Year of service calculations are correct and documented

### Claims Procedure Compliance

- [ ] Written claims procedure exists and complies with 29 CFR § 2560.503-1
- [ ] Initial claim determination made within required timeframe (90 days, 45 days for disability)
- [ ] Denial notice contains specific reasons, plan provisions, and appeal rights
- [ ] Denial notice identifies additional information needed and why
- [ ] Full and fair review provided on appeal (29 USC § 1133)
- [ ] Appeals decided within required timeframe (60 days, 45 days for disability)
- [ ] Claimant informed of right to external review (group health claims)
- [ ] ACA external review procedures followed for group health plans
- [ ] Culturally and linguistically appropriate notices provided where required
- [ ] Exhaustion of administrative remedies documented before litigation

### COBRA Compliance

- [ ] COBRA qualifying events properly identified
- [ ] Election notice provided within 14 days of administrator notification
- [ ] Election period of 60 days provided
- [ ] Premium amount properly calculated (102% or 150% for disability extension)
- [ ] Coverage duration meets statutory requirements (18/29/36 months)
- [ ] Notice of unavailability provided when COBRA denied
- [ ] Certificate of creditable coverage provided at termination and upon request

### Nondiscrimination and Testing

- [ ] ADP/ACP testing performed for 401(k) plans
- [ ] Coverage testing under 26 USC § 410(b) completed
- [ ] Top-heavy testing under 26 USC § 416 completed
- [ ] Corrective distributions made timely when tests fail
- [ ] Cross-testing or gateway requirements met for new comparability plans
- [ ] Benefits, rights, and features testing performed

### Reporting and Disclosure

- [ ] Form 5500 filed by deadline (or extended deadline)
- [ ] Summary Annual Report (SAR) distributed to participants
- [ ] Participant benefit statements provided quarterly (individual account) or annually (DB)
- [ ] Blackout period notices provided at least 30 days in advance
- [ ] Automatic enrollment and QDIA notices provided annually
- [ ] Plan termination notices and procedures followed

## Output Protocol

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Document ID | Unique identifier for audited document |
| Document Type | SPD / Plan Document / Denial Letter / Form 5500 / Meeting Minutes / Fee Disclosure / Amendment / Other |
| Plan Name | Name of the employee benefit plan |
| Plan Type | Defined Benefit / Defined Contribution / Health / Welfare / Other |
| Plan Sponsor | Employer or entity sponsoring the plan |
| Statute Cited | Specific ERISA section, IRC section, or CFR provision |
| Finding | Narrative description of the compliance issue |
| Severity | CRITICAL / HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / INFORMATIONAL |
| Fiduciary Impact | Whether finding exposes personal fiduciary liability |
| Prohibited Transaction | YES / NO / POTENTIAL -- with PTE analysis if applicable |
| Participant Harm | Description of actual or potential harm to participants |
| Remediation | Required corrective action with regulatory citation |
| Deadline | Statutory or regulatory deadline for correction |
| DOL/IRS Risk | Likelihood of enforcement action based on finding |
| Evidence Reference | Page number, section, or clause in source document |
