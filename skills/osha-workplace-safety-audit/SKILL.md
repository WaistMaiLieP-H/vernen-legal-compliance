# OSHA Workplace Safety Audit

**Purpose**

Identifies violations of federal and California occupational safety and health laws by auditing Injury and Illness Prevention Programs, OSHA 300 logs, incident reports, safety data sheets, training records, and workplace safety documentation against the OSH Act, OSHA standards, and Cal/OSHA requirements.

## Governing Standards

- **29 USC § 654(a)(2)** — Comply with occupational safety and health standards
- **29 USC § 655** — Standards: promulgation, modification, revocation
- **29 USC § 656** — Administration (OSHA created within Department of Labor)
- **29 USC § 657** — Inspections, investigations, and recordkeeping
- **29 USC § 658** — Citations: issuance, contents, posting requirements
- **29 USC § 659** — Enforcement procedures: notice of contest, abatement
- **29 USC § 660** — Judicial review of commission orders
- **29 USC § 661** — Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission
- **29 USC § 666** — Civil and criminal penalties

- **29 USC § 660(c)** — Whistleblower protection (Section 11(c))

## Audit Triggers

- OSHA 300 Log of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses
- OSHA 300A Annual Summary
- OSHA 301 Incident Report (or equivalent first report of injury)
- Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP) document
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) and chemical inventory
- Written hazard communication program
- Lockout/tagout (LOTO) energy control procedures
- Fall protection plan or written program
- Respiratory protection program and fit test records
- Confined space entry permits and rescue plans
- Employer safety training records and certifications
- OSHA citation, informal conference, or settlement agreement
- Workers' compensation claim or first report of injury
- Workplace fatality, hospitalization, amputation, or loss of eye report
- Safety committee meeting minutes
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) hazard assessment
- Fire prevention plan and emergency action plan
- Job hazard analysis (JHA) or job safety analysis (JSA)
- Industrial hygiene monitoring results
- Equipment inspection records (cranes, forklifts, scaffolding)

## Audit Checklist

### Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP) — Cal. Labor Code § 3203 / 8 CCR § 3203

- [ ] Written IIPP maintained and accessible to employees
- [ ] Element 1: Person(s) with authority and responsibility for the program identified
- [ ] Element 2: System for ensuring employee compliance (training, disciplinary procedures)
- [ ] Element 3: System for communicating with employees on safety matters
- [ ] Element 4: Procedures for identifying and evaluating workplace hazards
- [ ] Element 5: Procedures to investigate occupational injury or illness
- [ ] Element 6: Methods and procedures for correcting unsafe conditions
- [ ] Element 7: Training and instruction provided to employees
- [ ] Element 8: Recordkeeping and documentation system
- [ ] IIPP reviewed and updated when new hazards introduced or after incidents

### Recordkeeping and Reporting (29 CFR 1904)

- [ ] OSHA 300 Log accurately records all recordable injuries and illnesses
- [ ] Recording criteria correctly applied (§ 1904.7: death, days away, restricted, medical treatment beyond first aid)
- [ ] OSHA 300A Annual Summary certified by company executive
- [ ] OSHA 300A posted February 1 through April 30
- [ ] Records retained for 5 years following the year they cover
- [ ] Fatality reported to OSHA within 8 hours of employer knowledge
- [ ] In-patient hospitalization, amputation, or loss of eye reported within 24 hours
- [ ] Cal/OSHA serious injury/illness/death reported immediately by phone (§ 6409.1)
- [ ] Electronic submission of 300A to OSHA (if 250+ employees or designated NAICS code with 20+ employees)

### Hazard Communication (29 CFR § 1910.1200 / 8 CCR § 5194)

- [ ] Written hazard communication program developed and maintained
- [ ] Chemical inventory list complete and current
- [ ] Safety Data Sheets (SDS) available for all hazardous chemicals
- [ ] SDS in GHS 16-section format
- [ ] Container labeling: product identifier, signal word, hazard statement, pictograms, precautionary statements, supplier info
- [ ] Employee training on HazCom provided at initial assignment and when new hazards introduced
- [ ] Training covers: hazardous chemicals present, SDS location, labeling system, protective measures
- [ ] Non-routine task hazard communication procedures established
- [ ] Multi-employer worksite coordination documented

### Lockout/Tagout — Control of Hazardous Energy (29 CFR § 1910.147)

- [ ] Written energy control procedures for each machine/equipment
- [ ] Procedures include: notification, shutdown, isolation, lockout/tagout application, verification, release
- [ ] Locks and tags provided to authorized employees
- [ ] Authorized employees trained on energy control procedures
- [ ] Affected employees trained on purpose and use of LOTO
- [ ] Periodic inspection of energy control procedures conducted annually
- [ ] Group lockout/tagout procedures established where applicable
- [ ] Shift/personnel change procedures documented
- [ ] Outside service personnel (contractors) coordination documented

### Fall Protection (29 CFR § 1926.501-503 / 29 CFR § 1910.28)

- [ ] Fall protection provided at 6 feet (construction) or 4 feet (general industry)
- [ ] Fall protection systems: guardrail, safety net, personal fall arrest system
- [ ] Guardrails meet height (42" +/- 3"), midrail, and strength requirements
- [ ] Personal fall arrest systems: full body harness, anchorage (5,000 lbs), limited free fall to 6 feet
- [ ] Fall protection plan (written) if conventional methods infeasible
- [ ] Leading edge and hole protection provided
- [ ] Training on fall hazards and protection systems documented
- [ ] Fall protection equipment inspected before each use
- [ ] Rescue procedures established for suspended workers

### Personal Protective Equipment (29 CFR § 1910.132-138)

- [ ] PPE hazard assessment performed and certified in writing
- [ ] Appropriate PPE selected based on hazard assessment
- [ ] PPE properly fitted to each employee
- [ ] Employee training on when PPE necessary, what type, proper use, maintenance, disposal
- [ ] PPE maintained, replaced, and stored properly
- [ ] Employer provides PPE at no cost to employees (with limited exceptions)
- [ ] Respiratory protection: medical evaluation, fit testing, training (§ 1910.134)

### Fire Prevention and Emergency Action (29 CFR § 1910.38-39)

- [ ] Written emergency action plan maintained
- [ ] Evacuation procedures and routes established and posted
- [ ] Procedures for reporting fires and emergencies
- [ ] Employee alarm system operational and distinct
- [ ] Designated employees for evacuation assistance
- [ ] Employee training on emergency action plan
- [ ] Fire extinguishers: proper type, mounting height, travel distance, monthly inspection, annual maintenance
- [ ] Fire prevention plan: list of fire hazards, handling/storage procedures, ignition sources

### Whistleblower Protections (29 USC § 660(c) / Cal. Labor Code § 6310)

- [ ] No adverse action against employees filing safety complaints
- [ ] No adverse action against employees reporting injuries or illnesses
- [ ] No adverse action against employees exercising rights under OSH Act
- [ ] Whistleblower complaint procedures posted or communicated
- [ ] Cal/OSHA: no discharge or discrimination for refusing unsafe work (§ 6311)
- [ ] Retaliation complaint timeline: 30 days federal (§ 11(c)), 6 months California

### Heat Illness Prevention (8 CCR § 3395 / § 3396) — California Specific

- [ ] Written heat illness prevention plan (outdoor work)
- [ ] Access to potable water: at least one quart per employee per hour
- [ ] Access to shade when temperature exceeds 80 degrees F
- [ ] High-heat procedures triggered at 95 degrees F
- [ ] Acclimatization procedures for new/returning employees (first 14 days)
- [ ] Emergency response procedures for heat-related illness
- [ ] Employee and supervisor training on heat illness prevention
- [ ] Indoor heat illness prevention plan (if applicable under § 3396)

## Output Protocol

Each finding must be recorded in the following format:

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Finding ID** | OSHA-YYYY-NNN |
| **Document Reviewed** | [filename / document title] |
| **Date of Document** | [date on document face] |
| **Audit Date** | [date audit performed] |
| **Standard Violated** | [specific CFR section, Cal. Labor Code, or CCR section] |
| **Violation Type** | Willful / Serious / Other-Than-Serious / Repeat / Failure to Abate / General Duty Clause |
| **Industry** | General Industry (1910) / Construction (1926) / Maritime / Agriculture |
| **Severity** | Critical / High / Medium / Low |
| **Finding Description** | [plain-language description of the violation] |
| **Evidence** | [quoted text, measurement, photo reference, or absence noted] |
| **Regulatory Requirement** | [what the standard requires] |
| **Actual Condition** | [what the document or workplace shows] |
| **Injury Risk** | [type of injury or illness that could result] |
| **Remediation** | [specific corrective action required] |
| **Abatement Deadline** | [immediate / 30 days / as specified] |
| **Penalty Exposure** | [applicable penalty range per violation type] |
| **Cross-References** | [related findings, prior citations, or workers' comp claims] |
