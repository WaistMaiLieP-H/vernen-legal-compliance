## I. Federal Consumer Financial Protection Statutes

### A. Truth in Lending Act (TILA) — 15 USC § 1601 et seq.
**Implementing Regulation:** Regulation Z (12 CFR Part 1026)
- § 1601 — Congressional findings and purpose
- § 1631 — Disclosure requirements for open-end credit
- § 1632 — Form of required disclosures
- § 1635 — Right of rescission (3-day cooling-off for home-secured loans)
- § 1637 — Open-end credit plan disclosures
- § 1638 — Closed-end credit transactions
- § 1639 — Requirements for certain mortgages (HOEPA high-cost)
- § 1639c — Ability-to-repay requirement
- § 1640 — Civil liability (actual + statutory damages, attorney fees)
- § 1641 — Liability of assignees

**Key Reg Z Sections:**
- 12 CFR § 1026.17 — General disclosure requirements
- 12 CFR § 1026.18 — Content of disclosures (closed-end)
- 12 CFR § 1026.19 — Certain mortgage/home equity transactions (TRID)
- 12 CFR § 1026.22 — APR determination
- 12 CFR § 1026.23 — Right of rescission
- 12 CFR § 1026.32 — High-cost mortgage requirements
- 12 CFR § 1026.35 — Higher-priced mortgage loan requirements
- 12 CFR § 1026.43 — Ability-to-repay / Qualified Mortgage

### B. Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA) — 12 USC § 2601 et seq.
**Implementing Regulation:** Regulation X (12 CFR Part 1024)
- § 2603 — Uniform settlement statement (Closing Disclosure)
- § 2604 — Special information booklet
- § 2605 — Servicing of mortgage loans
- § 2607 — Prohibition of kickbacks and unearned fees
- § 2608 — Title companies — unearned fees prohibition
- § 2609 — Escrow accounts (limits on required deposits)
- § 2614 — Jurisdiction of courts / private right of action

**Key Reg X Sections:**
- 12 CFR § 1024.17 — Escrow accounts
- 12 CFR § 1024.30 — Scope (mortgage servicing rules)
- 12 CFR § 1024.35 — Error resolution (QWR)
- 12 CFR § 1024.36 — Requests for information
- 12 CFR § 1024.37 — Force-placed insurance
- 12 CFR § 1024.38 — General servicing policies
- 12 CFR § 1024.39 — Early intervention (delinquent borrowers)
- 12 CFR § 1024.41 — Loss mitigation procedures

### C. Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) — 15 USC § 1691 et seq.
**Implementing Regulation:** Regulation B (12 CFR Part 1002)
- § 1691(a) — Prohibited discrimination bases (race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, public assistance income)
- § 1691(d) — Adverse action notice requirements
- § 1691e — Civil liability
- 12 CFR § 1002.7 — Rules concerning extensions of credit
- 12 CFR § 1002.9 — Adverse action notifications
- 12 CFR § 1002.12 — Record retention

### D. Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA) — 15 USC § 1693 et seq.
**Implementing Regulation:** Regulation E (12 CFR Part 1005)
- § 1693b — Regulations (CFPB authority)
- § 1693f — Error resolution procedures
- § 1693g — Unauthorized transfer liability limits
- § 1693h — Liability of financial institutions
- 12 CFR § 1005.6 — Liability of consumer for unauthorized transfers
- 12 CFR § 1005.11 — Error resolution procedures and timelines
- 12 CFR § 1005.17 — Requirements for overdraft services (opt-in)
- 12 CFR § 1005.18 — Prepaid account requirements

### E. Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) — 15 USC § 1692 et seq.
- § 1692c — Communication in connection with debt collection
- § 1692d — Harassment or abuse
- § 1692e — False or misleading representations
- § 1692f — Unfair practices
- § 1692g — Validation of debts (5-day notice requirement)
- § 1692k — Civil liability (actual damages + statutory up to $1,000 + attorney fees)

### F. Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) — 15 USC § 1681 et seq.
- § 1681b — Permissible purposes
- § 1681i — Reinvestigation procedures (30 days)
- § 1681m — Adverse action notice requirements
- § 1681n — Civil liability for willful noncompliance
- § 1681o — Civil liability for negligent noncompliance
- § 1681s-2 — Furnisher responsibilities

### G. Expedited Funds Availability Act (EFAA) — 12 USC § 4001 et seq.
**Implementing Regulation:** Regulation CC (12 CFR Part 229)
- § 4002 — Expedited funds availability schedules
- § 4003 — Safeguard exceptions
- 12 CFR § 229.10 — Next-day availability (cash, electronic payments, certain checks)
- 12 CFR § 229.12 — Availability schedule (local/non-local checks)
- 12 CFR § 229.13 — Exception holds (new accounts, large deposits, repeated overdrafts)

### H. Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) — 15 USC § 6801 et seq.
**Implementing Regulation:** Regulation P (12 CFR Part 1016)
- § 6802 — Obligations with respect to financial privacy
- § 6803 — Disclosure of institution privacy policy
- 12 CFR § 1016.4 — Initial privacy notice
- 12 CFR § 1016.5 — Annual privacy notice
- 12 CFR § 1016.7 — Opt-out requirements

### I. UDAAP Authority — 12 USC § 5531
- § 5531(a) — CFPB authority to declare acts unfair, deceptive, or abusive
- § 5531(c) — Definition: Unfair (substantial injury, not reasonably avoidable, not outweighed by countervailing benefits)
- § 5531(d) — Definition: Abusive (materially interferes with consumer's ability to understand, or takes unreasonable advantage)

## II. California State Statutes

### A. California Financial Code
- FIN § 1000 et seq. — Bank licensing and operations
- FIN § 1800 et seq. — Savings association law
- FIN § 2000 et seq. — Credit union operations
- FIN § 14000 et seq. — California Residential Mortgage Lending Act (CRMLA)
- FIN § 22000 et seq. — California Financing Law (CFL)
- FIN § 22700 — CFL licensee prohibited practices
- FIN § 22750 — CFL borrower protections
- FIN § 50000 et seq. — California Consumer Financial Protection Law (effective 2024)

### B. California Civil Code — Consumer Protection
- CIV § 1632 — Translation requirements (contracts negotiated in Spanish, Chinese, Tagalog, Vietnamese, or Korean must provide translation)
- CIV § 1750 et seq. — Consumer Legal Remedies Act (CLRA)
  - § 1770 — Prohibited unfair or deceptive acts
  - § 1780 — Remedies (actual damages, injunctive relief, punitive damages)
  - § 1781 — Class actions
- CIV § 1788 et seq. — Rosenthal Fair Debt Collection Practices Act
  - § 1788.10 — Harassment/abuse prohibitions
  - § 1788.11 — Threats and coercion
  - § 1788.12 — Unfair practices
  - § 1788.13 — Deceptive practices
  - § 1788.14 — Restrictions (time-barred debt disclosure requirement)
  - § 1788.17 — FDCPA incorporation (original creditors covered)
  - § 1788.30 — Remedies (statutory damages $100–$1,000 per violation)
- CIV § 2954 — Impound accounts
- CIV § 2954.1 — Interest on impound accounts
- CIV § 2955 — Hazard insurance impound requirements

### C. California Business & Professions Code
- BPC § 17200 et seq. — Unfair Competition Law (UCL)
  - Unlawful, unfair, OR fraudulent business practice
  - Standing: any person who has suffered injury in fact and lost money/property
  - Remedies: restitution, injunctive relief
- BPC § 17500 et seq. — False Advertising Law

### D. California Government Code — Lending Discrimination
- GOV § 12955 — Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA)
  - Prohibits discrimination in financial assistance for housing
  - Protected classes include all federal plus additional state protections

## III. Regulatory Bodies & Enforcement

### Federal
- **CFPB** (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau) — Primary federal consumer financial regulator
  - Complaint portal: consumerfinance.gov/complaint
  - Supervision authority: banks >$10B, non-bank mortgage/payday/student lenders
- **OCC** (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency) — National bank supervision
- **FDIC** (Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation) — State-chartered bank supervision
- **Federal Reserve** — Bank holding company / state member bank supervision
- **NCUA** (National Credit Union Administration) — Credit union supervision
- **FTC** (Federal Trade Commission) — Non-bank financial service oversight

### California
- **DFPI** (Department of Financial Protection and Innovation) — Primary CA financial regulator
  - Complaint portal: dfpi.ca.gov/file-a-complaint
  - Licenses: CFL, CRMLA, money transmitters, escrow agents
- **DOJ** (Attorney General) — UCL/CLRA enforcement
- **DRE** (Department of Real Estate) — Real estate broker lending oversight

## IV. Consumer Remedy Matrix

| Violation Type | Federal Remedy | California Remedy |
|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| TILA disclosure error | Statutory damages $200–$4,000 (individual) + actual + atty fees (§ 1640) | UCL restitution + CLRA damages |
| RESPA kickback/fee | 3x amount of charge + atty fees (§ 2607) | UCL + CLRA |
| ECOA discrimination | Actual + punitive (capped) + atty fees (§ 1691e) | FEHA unlimited actual + punitive |
| Reg E error resolution | Treble damages for intentional (§ 1693h) | UCL + CLRA |
| FDCPA violation | Actual + $1,000 statutory + atty fees (§ 1692k) | Rosenthal: $100–$1,000/violation + actual + atty fees |
| FCRA violation | $100–$1,000 statutory (willful) + actual + atty fees | CCRAA: $2,500/violation |
| UDAAP | CFPB enforcement action + civil penalties | UCL: restitution + injunction |
| Unfair business practice | FTC Act enforcement | UCL/CLRA: restitution + actual + injunction |

## V. Key Timelines

| Event | Deadline | Authority |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Loan Estimate delivery | 3 business days from application | TRID (Reg Z § 1026.19) |
| Closing Disclosure delivery | 3 business days before consummation | TRID (Reg Z § 1026.19) |
| Right of rescission | 3 business days from consummation | TILA § 1635 |
| EFT error resolution | 10 business days (45 with provisional credit) | Reg E § 1005.11 |
| Debt validation notice | Within 5 days of initial communication | FDCPA § 1692g |
| QWR response | 30 days (5 day acknowledgment) | RESPA § 2605(e) |
| Servicing transfer notice | 15 days before effective date | Reg X § 1024.33 |
| Adverse action notice | Within 30 days of adverse action | ECOA / Reg B § 1002.9 |
| FCRA dispute reinvestigation | 30 days (45 with consumer info) | FCRA § 1681i |
| TILA statute of limitations | 1 year (damages) / 3 years (rescission) | 15 USC § 1640(e) |
| FDCPA statute of limitations | 1 year | 15 USC § 1692k(d) |
| Rosenthal statute of limitations | 1 year | CIV Code § 1788.30 |
| UCL statute of limitations | 4 years | BPC § 17208 |
| CLRA statute of limitations | 3 years | CIV Code § 1783 |
