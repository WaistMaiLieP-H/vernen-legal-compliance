# SOX Corporate Governance Audit

## Purpose

Passive audit layer. When user submits audit reports, financial statements, internal control assessments, corporate governance documents, board minutes, or executive certifications, audit against Sarbanes-Oxley Act requirements, PCAOB standards, SEC rules, and exchange listing standards for compliance deficiencies and material weaknesses.

## Governing Standards

### Federal

- **15 USC § 7201 et seq** — Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 (SOX)
- **SOX § 302 (15 USC § 7241)** — CEO/CFO certification of financial reports
- **SOX § 404 (15 USC § 7262)** — Management assessment of internal controls over financial reporting (ICFR)
- **SOX § 906 (18 USC § 1350)** — Criminal penalties for false certification
- **SOX § 806 (18 USC § 1514A)** — Whistleblower protection (retaliation prohibited)
- **SOX § 301 (15 USC § 78j-1)** — Audit committee requirements (independence, complaint procedures)
- **SOX § 303 (15 USC § 78m(b)(2))** — Improper influence on audits prohibited
- **SOX § 304 (15 USC § 7243)** — Forfeiture of bonuses and profits upon restatement (clawback)
- **SOX § 402 (15 USC § 78m(k))** — Prohibition on personal loans to executives
- **SOX § 407 (15 USC § 7265)** — Financial expert on audit committee disclosure
- **SOX § 409 (15 USC § 78m(l))** — Real-time issuer disclosures (material changes)

### California

- **Cal. Corporations Code § 300-318** — Board of directors duties and governance
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 1500-1507** — Annual reports and financial statements
- **Cal. Government Code § 12586** — Nonprofit governance and accountability (if applicable)

### Regulatory

- **PCAOB Auditing Standard No. 5 (AS 2201)** — Audit of internal control over financial reporting integrated with financial statement audit
- **COSO Internal Control — Integrated Framework (2013)** — Five components: control environment, risk assessment, control activities, information/communication, monitoring
- **SEC Regulation S-K** — Non-financial statement disclosure requirements
- **SEC Regulation S-X** — Financial statement form and content requirements
- **SEC Rule 13a-14 / 15d-14** — Certifications required under § 302
- **SEC Rule 13a-15 / 15d-15** — Controls and procedures
- **SEC Interpretive Release 33-8810** — Management guidance on evaluating ICFR
- **NYSE Listed Company Manual § 303A** — Corporate governance listing standards
- **NASDAQ Listing Rules § 5605-5606** — Board independence and audit committee standards
- **SEC Rule 10A-3** — Audit committee listing standards

## Audit Triggers

- Annual or quarterly financial statements (10-K, 10-Q)
- Management ICFR assessment reports
- External auditor reports on ICFR
- CEO/CFO certification documents (§ 302, § 906)
- Audit committee charters or meeting minutes
- Corporate governance guidelines or board policies
- Proxy statements (DEF 14A) with governance disclosures
- Restatement notices or amended filings
- Internal audit reports identifying control deficiencies
- Whistleblower complaint procedures or ethics hotline documentation

## Audit Checklist

### CEO/CFO Certifications (§ 302 / § 906)
- [ ] Verify § 302 certification accompanies each 10-K and 10-Q filing
- [ ] Confirm certifying officers have reviewed the report
- [ ] Verify certification states report does not contain material misstatements or omissions
- [ ] Confirm officers certify they are responsible for establishing and maintaining disclosure controls
- [ ] Check that officers disclose all significant ICFR deficiencies to auditors and audit committee
- [ ] Verify § 906 criminal certification accompanies each periodic report
- [ ] Assess whether officers have proper basis for certifications
- [ ] Flag any certification with modifications, qualifications, or non-standard language

### Internal Controls Over Financial Reporting (§ 404)
- [ ] Verify management's annual assessment of ICFR effectiveness is included in 10-K
- [ ] Confirm assessment uses recognized framework (COSO 2013)
- [ ] Determine whether external auditor's ICFR attestation report is included (accelerated filers)
- [ ] Identify any material weaknesses disclosed in assessment
- [ ] Distinguish material weakness from significant deficiency from deficiency
- [ ] Assess remediation plans for identified material weaknesses
- [ ] Verify entity-level controls evaluated (tone at the top, competence, board oversight)
- [ ] Check process-level controls for significant accounts and disclosures

### COSO Framework Evaluation
- [ ] Assess control environment: integrity, ethical values, board oversight, organizational structure
- [ ] Evaluate risk assessment: fraud risk, change management, objective-setting
- [ ] Review control activities: authorization, segregation of duties, IT general controls
- [ ] Assess information and communication: internal/external reporting, whistleblower channels
- [ ] Evaluate monitoring activities: ongoing evaluations, separate evaluations, deficiency remediation
- [ ] Verify all five components present and functioning together in an integrated manner
- [ ] Assess IT general controls (access management, change management, operations, SDLC)

### Audit Committee Compliance
- [ ] Verify audit committee composed entirely of independent directors
- [ ] Confirm at least one member qualifies as "financial expert" per SOX § 407
- [ ] Check that audit committee charter includes complaint procedures for accounting/auditing concerns
- [ ] Verify audit committee has sole authority to appoint, compensate, and oversee external auditor
- [ ] Confirm audit committee pre-approves all audit and non-audit services
- [ ] Assess adequacy of meeting frequency (minimum quarterly for 10-Q/10-K review)
- [ ] Verify confidential, anonymous complaint mechanism for employees (whistleblower hotline)
- [ ] Check that audit committee has authority to engage independent advisors

### Auditor Independence
- [ ] Verify external auditor does not provide prohibited non-audit services (§ 201)
- [ ] Confirm lead engagement partner rotation every 5 years (§ 203)
- [ ] Check concurring review partner rotation compliance
- [ ] Assess cooling-off period for former audit firm employees in financial oversight roles (§ 206)
- [ ] Verify audit committee pre-approval of all permitted non-audit services
- [ ] Confirm no improper influence on audit conduct (§ 303)
- [ ] Check PCAOB registration and inspection compliance of external auditor

### Financial Reporting and Disclosure
- [ ] Assess MD&A for completeness and accuracy of known trends and uncertainties
- [ ] Verify off-balance-sheet arrangements properly disclosed
- [ ] Check related party transactions fully disclosed (ASC 850)
- [ ] Confirm pro forma financial information reconciled to GAAP
- [ ] Assess critical accounting estimates disclosure adequacy
- [ ] Verify real-time disclosure of material events on Form 8-K (§ 409)
- [ ] Check earnings releases for non-GAAP measure reconciliation compliance (Reg G)

### Executive Compensation and Clawback
- [ ] Assess § 304 clawback applicability if accounting restatement occurred
- [ ] Verify compliance with SEC Rule 10D-1 (listing standard clawback policy adoption)
- [ ] Check that clawback policy covers incentive compensation received during 3-year lookback
- [ ] Confirm no personal loans to directors or executive officers (§ 402)
- [ ] Verify insider trading compliance and blackout period restrictions (§ 306)
- [ ] Assess executive compensation disclosures in proxy statement for completeness

### Record Retention and Destruction
- [ ] Verify audit workpapers retained for minimum 7 years (§ 802)
- [ ] Confirm no destruction, alteration, or falsification of records to impede investigation
- [ ] Assess document retention policy compliance with SOX and SEC rules
- [ ] Check email and electronic communication retention policies
- [ ] Verify litigation hold procedures in place and enforced when applicable
- [ ] Flag any evidence of record destruction during investigation or audit period
