# WARN Act Layoff Audit

**Purpose**

Systematically audit employer communications, WARN notices, mass layoff documentation, and plant closing records to identify federal and California WARN Act notice deficiency, threshold miscalculation, improper exception reliance, and penalty exposure arising from inadequate advance notification of layoffs, plant closings, and relocations that deprive workers of statutory protections.

## Governing Standards

### Federal Statutes

- **29 USC § 2101** -- Definitions (employer, plant closing, mass layoff, affected employees, single site of employment)
- **29 USC § 2101(a)(1)** -- Employer definition: 100 or more full-time employees (excluding part-time)
- **29 USC § 2101(a)(2)** -- Plant closing: permanent or temporary shutdown of a single site causing 50+ employee employment losses
- **29 USC § 2101(a)(3)** -- Mass layoff: (A) 500+ employees or (B) 50-499 employees if 33% of workforce at single site
- **29 USC § 2101(a)(6)** -- Employment loss: termination (other than for cause), layoff exceeding 6 months, reduction in hours >50% in each of 6 months
- **29 USC § 2101(b)** -- Exclusions (strikes, lockouts, construction site completion)
- **29 USC § 2102(a)** -- 60-day advance written notice requirement
- **29 USC § 2102(b)(1)** -- Faltering company exception (actively seeking capital/business; notice would preclude obtaining)
- **29 USC § 2102(b)(2)(A)** -- Unforeseeable business circumstances exception
- **29 USC § 2102(b)(2)(B)** -- Natural disaster exception (earthquake, flood, drought, storm, tidal wave)
- **29 USC § 2102(b)(3)** -- Reduced notice must be as much as practicable; brief statement of reason for reduction
- **29 USC § 2103** -- Notice recipients (affected employees, union representatives, state dislocated worker unit, chief elected local government official)
- **29 USC § 2104(a)** -- Employer liability: back pay and benefits for each day of violation (up to 60 days)
- **29 USC § 2104(a)(3)** -- Civil penalty: $500 per day to local government for failure to notify
- **29 USC § 2104(a)(4)** -- Reduction of penalty for payment to employees within 3 weeks
- **29 USC § 2104(a)(5)** -- Attorney fees to prevailing party
- **29 USC § 2104(b)** -- Good faith defense reducing liability
- **29 USC § 2105** -- Federal court jurisdiction; 3-year statute of limitations (from plant closing/mass layoff date)
- **29 USC § 2107** -- Preemption: does not preempt more protective state laws
- **29 USC § 2108** -- Aggregation rule: employment losses within 90-day period aggregated unless employer demonstrates separate causes

### California Statutes

- **Cal. Labor Code § 1400** -- Definitions (covered establishment, employer, mass layoff, relocation, termination)
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1400(a)** -- Covered establishment: industrial or commercial facility with 75+ employees in preceding 12 months
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1400(d)** -- Mass layoff: layoff of 50+ employees during any 30-day period
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1400(e)** -- Relocation: move of operations 100+ miles away
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1400(f)** -- Termination: cessation or substantial cessation of operations
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1401(a)** -- 60-day advance written notice required
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1401(b)** -- Notice to EDD, local workforce investment board, chief elected local official, affected employees, and union
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1401(c)** -- Contents of notice specified
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1402** -- Employer liability: back pay and benefits for each day of violation (up to 60 days)
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1402(b)** -- Civil penalty: $500 per day to each affected employee (not just local government)
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1402.5** -- Exceptions: physical calamity, act of war, but NOT faltering company or unforeseeable business circumstances
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1403** -- Enforcement: action by affected employee, representative, or political subdivision
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1404** -- Federal WARN compliance does not excuse California WARN noncompliance
- **Cal. Labor Code § 1405** -- No waiver of rights under California WARN

### Federal Regulations and Guidance

- **20 CFR Part 639** -- WARN Act implementing regulations (DOL)
- **20 CFR § 639.1** -- Purpose and scope
- **20 CFR § 639.3** -- Definitions (single site of employment, part-time employee, operating unit)
- **20 CFR § 639.4** -- Which employers are covered
- **20 CFR § 639.5** -- When must notice be given (60-day rule, aggregation, rolling 90-day lookback)
- **20 CFR § 639.6** -- Who must receive notice
- **20 CFR § 639.7** -- Content of notice (required elements)
- **20 CFR § 639.8** -- How is notice to be served (written, specific form)
- **20 CFR § 639.9** -- Exceptions to 60-day notice (faltering company, unforeseeable, natural disaster)
- **20 CFR § 639.10** -- Sale of business provisions (seller vs. buyer responsibility)
- **DOL Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Fact Sheet**
- **DOL WARN Employer's Guide to Advance Notice of Closings and Layoffs**

## Audit Triggers

- WARN notice letter from employer to employees, union, or government
- Mass layoff or plant closing announcement
- Employer communication regarding layoffs, reductions in force, furloughs, or restructuring
- Severance agreement or separation package
- Facility closure or relocation notice
- EDD or state dislocated worker unit correspondence
- Employee complaint about inadequate notice
- Media reports of sudden layoffs
- Class action complaint alleging WARN violations
- Payroll records showing mass terminations
- SEC filings disclosing restructuring charges
- Bankruptcy petition with associated layoffs
- Sale of business or merger documentation with workforce impact

## Audit Checklist

### Threshold Analysis

- [ ] Employer has 100+ full-time employees (federal) or 75+ employees at covered establishment (California)
- [ ] Part-time employees (<20 hrs/week or <6 months) properly excluded from federal count but included in California count
- [ ] Remote workers assigned to correct single site of employment
- [ ] Multiple facilities properly analyzed as separate or combined single sites
- [ ] Employment loss count reaches plant closing threshold (50+) or mass layoff threshold (500+ or 50-499 at 33%)
- [ ] California threshold applied: 50+ employees in 30-day period
- [ ] Rolling 90-day aggregation analysis performed (29 USC § 2108)
- [ ] Employer has not artificially structured layoffs to avoid thresholds
- [ ] Constructive employment losses counted (>50% hour reduction sustained 6 months)
- [ ] Temporary layoffs exceeding 6 months counted as employment losses

### Notice Content Compliance

- [ ] Notice is in writing (20 CFR § 639.8)
- [ ] Notice provides name and address of employment site
- [ ] Notice states whether action is plant closing or mass layoff
- [ ] Notice provides expected date of first separation and schedule of separations
- [ ] Notice states whether bumping rights exist
- [ ] Notice provides name and contact information of company official
- [ ] Notice identifies each union representing affected employees (if applicable)
- [ ] Notice to union satisfies representative notice requirements (20 CFR § 639.6)
- [ ] California notice includes all elements required by Cal. Labor Code § 1401(c)
- [ ] Conditional notice, if used, clearly states conditions and expected date

### Notice Timing and Delivery

- [ ] 60-day advance notice provided before first employment loss
- [ ] Notice served to all required recipients: affected employees, unions, state dislocated worker unit, chief elected local official
- [ ] California: notice served to EDD and local workforce investment board
- [ ] Notice provided individually to non-union affected employees (not just posted)
- [ ] Notice to state and local officials served simultaneously with employee notice
- [ ] Updated notice provided if date or scope of action changes materially
- [ ] Extension of layoff beyond 6 months triggers new notice obligation

### Exception Analysis

- [ ] Faltering company exception (federal only): employer actively seeking capital and reasonably believed notice would preclude obtaining it
- [ ] Faltering company exception limited to plant closings only (not mass layoffs)
- [ ] Unforeseeable business circumstances (federal only): caused by circumstances not reasonably foreseeable at time notice would have been required
- [ ] Natural disaster exception: employment loss is direct result of natural disaster
- [ ] Exception reliance documented with brief statement of basis per 29 USC § 2102(b)(3)
- [ ] Shortened notice given as much in advance as practicable under the circumstances
- [ ] California: only physical calamity and act of war exceptions available (Cal. Labor Code § 1402.5)
- [ ] California: faltering company and unforeseeable business circumstances exceptions NOT available

### Sale of Business

- [ ] Seller provides notice for actions occurring before closing of sale
- [ ] Buyer provides notice for actions occurring on or after closing of sale
- [ ] Employees of seller treated as employees of buyer for purposes of WARN coverage
- [ ] No gap in coverage between seller and buyer responsibility

### Penalty and Liability Exposure

- [ ] Back pay liability calculated for each affected employee for each day of violation (up to 60 days)
- [ ] Benefits liability (medical, pension contributions) calculated for violation period
- [ ] California per-employee $500/day civil penalty calculated
- [ ] Federal $500/day civil penalty to local government calculated
- [ ] Good faith defense availability assessed (29 USC § 2104(b))
- [ ] Mitigation through voluntary payment within 3 weeks of closing evaluated
- [ ] Attorney fees and costs exposure assessed
- [ ] Statute of limitations verified (3 years from date of closing/layoff)

### Intersecting Obligations

- [ ] COBRA notices triggered by mass layoff or plant closing
- [ ] State unemployment insurance notification requirements met
- [ ] ERISA plan termination or amendment obligations triggered
- [ ] Collective bargaining agreement notification and bargaining obligations met
- [ ] Age discrimination analysis if layoff disproportionately affects 40+ workers (ADEA/OWBPA)
- [ ] SEC disclosure obligations for public companies (material restructuring)
- [ ] Bankruptcy Code § 363 sale notice requirements if applicable

## Output Protocol

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Document ID | Unique identifier for audited document |
| Document Type | WARN Notice / Layoff Announcement / Severance Agreement / Employer Communication / EDD Correspondence / Complaint / Payroll Record / SEC Filing / Other |
| Employer Name | Name of the employer or covered establishment |
| Action Type | Plant Closing / Mass Layoff / Relocation / Termination of Operations |
| Jurisdiction | Federal WARN / California WARN / Both |
| Employees Affected | Number of employees subject to employment loss |
| Statute Cited | Specific WARN Act section, California Labor Code section, or 20 CFR provision |

| Severity | CRITICAL / HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / INFORMATIONAL |
| Notice Deficiency | NO NOTICE / LATE NOTICE / INCOMPLETE NOTICE / ADEQUATE |
| Days Short | Number of calendar days notice was short of 60-day requirement |
| Exception Claimed | Faltering Company / Unforeseeable / Natural Disaster / None / Not Available (CA) |
| Exception Valid | YES / NO / INSUFFICIENT DOCUMENTATION |
| Back Pay Exposure | Estimated per-employee and aggregate liability |
| Penalty Exposure | Estimated civil penalty liability (federal and/or California) |
| Remediation | Required corrective action with regulatory citation |
| Litigation Risk | Likelihood of class action or individual WARN claims |
| Evidence Reference | Page number, section, or clause in source document |
