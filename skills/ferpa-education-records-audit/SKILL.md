# FERPA Education Records Audit

**Purpose**

Systematically audit education institution records, policies, disclosure logs, and correspondence to identify FERPA compliance failures, unauthorized disclosures of student education records, improper denial of access rights, directory information mishandling, and consent deficiencies that expose educational agencies and institutions to federal funding loss and civil liability.

## Audit Triggers

- Student education record or cumulative file
- Transcript request or release
- Disciplinary records or suspension/expulsion documentation
- Special education records (IEP, Section 504 plan)
- Directory information publication or release
- FERPA consent form or authorization for disclosure
- Parental or eligible student access request and response
- Record amendment request and hearing documentation
- Third-party disclosure log or access log
- Subpoena or court order for student records
- School policy on student records, privacy, or data sharing
- Technology vendor agreement or data sharing agreement
- Health or safety emergency disclosure documentation
- Annual FERPA notification
- Transfer records between institutions
- Education technology platform privacy policy
- Student complaint to Family Policy Compliance Office (FPCO)

## Audit Checklist

### Annual Notification Compliance

- [ ] Annual FERPA notification provided to parents/eligible students (34 CFR § 99.7)
- [ ] Notification describes right to inspect and review records
- [ ] Notification describes right to seek amendment
- [ ] Notification describes right to consent to disclosures
- [ ] Notification describes right to file complaint with FPCO
- [ ] Notification identifies directory information categories designated
- [ ] Notification provides opt-out period and procedure for directory information
- [ ] Notification specifies school officials with legitimate educational interest
- [ ] Notification provided through reasonably likely-to-inform method

### Access and Inspection Rights

- [ ] Access request responded to within 45 days (34 CFR § 99.10)
- [ ] Complete education records provided, not selectively redacted
- [ ] Copies provided when failure to do so would effectively deny access (34 CFR § 99.12)
- [ ] Fees for copies do not effectively prevent access
- [ ] Noncustodial parent access rights respected (unless court order restricts)
- [ ] Eligible student rights properly transferred at age 18 or postsecondary enrollment
- [ ] Access not denied based on outstanding fees, fines, or charges
- [ ] Records of other students properly redacted before providing access

### Consent and Disclosure Compliance

- [ ] Prior written consent obtained before disclosure (34 CFR § 99.30)
- [ ] Consent is signed, dated, and specifies records, purpose, and recipient
- [ ] Applicable exception properly identified and documented for each non-consent disclosure
- [ ] Legitimate educational interest defined and applied consistently (34 CFR § 99.31(a)(1))
- [ ] School official access limited to those with demonstrated legitimate educational interest
- [ ] Outsourced institutional services contractors meet school official criteria
- [ ] Health or safety emergency disclosures narrowly tailored and documented (34 CFR § 99.36)
- [ ] Judicial order or subpoena disclosures preceded by reasonable effort to notify (34 CFR § 99.31(a)(9))
- [ ] De-identified data releases comply with FERPA de-identification standards
- [ ] No disclosure of personally identifiable information from education records without authorization

### Directory Information

- [ ] Directory information categories formally designated (34 CFR § 99.37)
- [ ] Annual public notice of directory information categories provided
- [ ] Reasonable opt-out period provided before disclosure
- [ ] Opt-out requests documented and honored
- [ ] Student ID numbers not used as directory information unless non-authenticating
- [ ] Social Security numbers never designated as directory information
- [ ] Armed forces recruiter requests handled per 20 USC § 7908 and opt-out rights

### Amendment and Hearing Procedures

- [ ] Record amendment requests accepted and reviewed (34 CFR § 99.20)
- [ ] Written decision on amendment request provided within reasonable time
- [ ] If denied, parent/student informed of right to hearing
- [ ] Hearing conducted by party without direct interest in outcome (34 CFR § 99.22)
- [ ] Written hearing decision includes summary of evidence and reasons
- [ ] Right to place statement in record if amendment denied is communicated
- [ ] Statement maintained and disclosed with the contested record

### Recordkeeping of Disclosures

- [ ] Disclosure log maintained for each student record (34 CFR § 99.32)
- [ ] Log records name of recipient, legitimate interest, and date of disclosure
- [ ] Log maintained as long as education records are maintained
- [ ] Disclosures to school officials with legitimate educational interest logged (if required by policy)
- [ ] Disclosures under health/safety emergency documented with articulable and significant threat finding
- [ ] Subpoena and court order disclosures logged with notice documentation

### Technology and Third-Party Data Sharing

- [ ] Ed-tech vendor agreements include FERPA-compliant data use restrictions
- [ ] Vendors designated as school officials have direct institutional control
- [ ] Data sharing agreements limit use to designated purpose
- [ ] Re-disclosure prohibited in vendor agreements
- [ ] Data destruction or return provisions included in agreements
- [ ] Student data not used for targeted advertising by vendors
- [ ] California SOPIPA compliance verified for K-12 technology vendors
- [ ] Data breach notification procedures in place for third-party data holders

### Special Populations

- [ ] Special education records (IEP, 504) subject to additional IDEA protections
- [ ] Disciplinary records for students with disabilities handled per IDEA procedures
- [ ] Records of students involved in campus disciplinary proceedings for crimes of violence disclosed only per 20 USC § 1232g(b)(6) and (i)
- [ ] Juvenile justice system disclosures comply with state law restrictions
- [ ] Immigration status not disclosed absent specific legal authority

## Output Protocol

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Document ID | Unique identifier for audited document |
| Document Type | Education Record / Transcript / Disciplinary File / Consent Form / Disclosure Log / Policy / Vendor Agreement / Notification / Other |
| Institution Name | Name of the educational institution or agency |
| Institution Level | K-12 / Postsecondary / Both |
| Student Status | Minor (parent rights) / Eligible Student (18+/postsecondary) |
| Statute Cited | Specific FERPA section, 34 CFR provision, or California Education Code section |
| Finding | Narrative description of the compliance issue |
| Severity | CRITICAL / HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / INFORMATIONAL |
| Disclosure Violation | YES / NO -- whether unauthorized disclosure of PII from education records occurred |
| Access Denial | YES / NO -- whether lawful access to records was improperly denied |
| Records Affected | Type and scope of education records involved |
| Remediation | Required corrective action with regulatory citation |
| FPCO Complaint Risk | Likelihood of a valid Family Policy Compliance Office complaint |
| Funding Impact | Whether finding could trigger federal funding loss under 20 USC § 1232g(e) |
| Evidence Reference | Page number, section, or clause in source document |
