# Securities Broker-Dealer Audit

## Purpose

Passive audit layer. When user submits offering documents, customer agreements, trade confirmations, Form ADV, Form BD, account statements, regulatory filings, or compliance documentation, audit against federal securities statutes, SEC rules, FINRA rules, and California securities law for registration compliance, fiduciary duty adherence, anti-fraud violations, and books-and-records deficiencies.

## Governing Standards

### Federal

- **15 USC § 77a et seq** — Securities Act of 1933 (registration of securities, anti-fraud § 17(a))
- **15 USC § 77e** — Prohibition on sale of unregistered securities
- **15 USC § 77d** — Exemptions from registration (§ 4(a)(2) private placement)
- **15 USC § 78a et seq** — Securities Exchange Act of 1934
- **15 USC § 78j(b)** — Anti-fraud provision (Rule 10b-5)
- **15 USC § 78o** — Broker-dealer registration requirements
- **15 USC § 78o(l)** — Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI)
- **15 USC § 80b-1 et seq** — Investment Advisers Act of 1940
- **15 USC § 80b-6** — Investment adviser anti-fraud provisions
- **15 USC § 80a-1 et seq** — Investment Company Act of 1940
- **Dodd-Frank Act § 913** — Fiduciary duty study and standard of conduct
- **SEC v. W.J. Howey Co., 328 U.S. 293 (1946)** — Investment contract definition (Howey test)

### California

- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25000-25707** — Corporate Securities Law of 1968
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25110** — Qualification requirement for securities offerings
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25130-25141** — Exemptions from qualification (§ 25102 exemptions)
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25210** — Broker-dealer licensing requirement
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25230** — Investment adviser licensing requirement
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25401** — Anti-fraud (material misrepresentation or omission)
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25501** — Civil liability for securities fraud
- **Cal. Corporations Code § 25504** — Secondary liability (control persons, aiders and abettors)
- **10 CCR § 260.211-260.241** — California securities regulations (DFPI)

### Regulatory

- **17 CFR Part 230** — SEC rules under Securities Act (Reg D, Reg A/A+, Reg CF, Reg S, Rule 144)
- **17 CFR § 230.501-508** — Regulation D (private placements — Rule 504, 506(b), 506(c))
- **17 CFR § 230.251-263** — Regulation A / A+ (small offerings up to $75M)
- **17 CFR § 227** — Regulation Crowdfunding (Reg CF, up to $5M)
- **17 CFR Part 240** — SEC rules under Exchange Act
- **17 CFR § 240.10b-5** — Rule 10b-5 (anti-fraud)
- **17 CFR § 240.15l-1** — Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI)
- **17 CFR § 240.17a-3 / 17a-4** — Books and records requirements (broker-dealers)
- **17 CFR Part 275** — SEC rules under Investment Advisers Act
- **17 CFR § 275.206(4)-7** — Compliance programs for investment advisers
- **17 CFR Part 242** — Regulation SHO (short sales), Regulation NMS, Regulation ATS
- **FINRA Rule 2090** — Know Your Customer (KYC)
- **FINRA Rule 2111** — Suitability (legacy, applicable to recommendations not covered by Reg BI)
- **FINRA Rule 3110** — Supervision
- **FINRA Rule 4512** — Customer Account Information
- **FINRA Rule 2210** — Communications with the public
- **FINRA Rule 3270** — Outside Business Activities
- **FINRA Rule 5130** — Restrictions on new issue allocations
- **Form ADV Part 1 / Part 2A (Brochure) / Part 2B (Supplement) / Part 3 (Form CRS)** — Investment adviser registration and disclosure
- **Form BD** — Broker-dealer registration (FINRA/SEC)

## Audit Triggers

- Private placement memoranda (PPM) or offering circulars
- Subscription agreements or investor questionnaires
- Customer account agreements or advisory agreements
- Trade confirmations or account statements
- Form ADV or Form BD filings
- Form CRS (Client Relationship Summary)
- Marketing materials, pitch decks, or performance advertisements
- Compliance manuals or written supervisory procedures (WSPs)
- FINRA examination correspondence or deficiency letters
- SEC examination or enforcement correspondence
- Insider trading policies or restricted lists
- Form D filings (Regulation D notices)

## Audit Checklist

### Registration and Licensing
- [ ] Verify securities offered are either registered or exempt from registration
- [ ] If Reg D: confirm correct rule relied upon (504, 506(b), or 506(c))
- [ ] If Reg D 506(b): verify no general solicitation and maximum 35 non-accredited investors
- [ ] If Reg D 506(c): verify all purchasers are accredited and reasonable steps taken to verify
- [ ] If Reg A/A+: verify qualification with SEC and offering statement (Form 1-A) filed
- [ ] If Reg CF: verify offering through registered funding portal, $5M annual limit
- [ ] Verify Form D filed with SEC within 15 days of first sale
- [ ] Confirm broker-dealer (if involved) registered with SEC and FINRA (Form BD)
- [ ] Confirm investment adviser registered with SEC (Form ADV) or state (if AUM under $100M)
- [ ] Verify California qualification or exemption under Cal. Corp. Code § 25110 / § 25102

### Reg BI and Fiduciary Duty
- [ ] If broker-dealer recommendation: assess Reg BI compliance (Disclosure, Care, Conflict of Interest, Compliance obligations)
- [ ] Verify Form CRS (Client Relationship Summary) delivered to retail customer at or before recommendation
- [ ] Assess whether recommendation is in customer's best interest based on investment profile
- [ ] Evaluate cost considerations (among reasonably available alternatives)
- [ ] Identify material conflicts of interest and assess whether adequately disclosed and mitigated
- [ ] If investment adviser: assess fiduciary duty compliance (duty of care and duty of loyalty)
- [ ] Verify Form ADV Part 2A brochure delivered within required timeframes
- [ ] Assess whether adviser's fees and compensation are fully disclosed

### Anti-Fraud Analysis (Rule 10b-5 / § 17(a))
- [ ] Assess offering documents for material misrepresentations
- [ ] Identify material omissions (facts necessary to make statements not misleading)
- [ ] Evaluate forward-looking statements for meaningful cautionary language (PSLRA safe harbor)
- [ ] Check for misleading performance advertising or cherry-picked results
- [ ] Assess whether risk factors are adequately disclosed and not boilerplate
- [ ] Evaluate use of projections — are assumptions reasonable and disclosed
- [ ] Flag any guarantee of returns or minimization of risk without adequate disclosure
- [ ] Assess scienter indicators (knowledge or reckless disregard)

### Insider Trading Compliance
- [ ] Verify insider trading policy adopted and distributed to all access persons
- [ ] Confirm restricted list and watch list procedures in place
- [ ] Check for pre-clearance procedures for personal securities transactions
- [ ] Verify Section 16 reporting compliance for officers, directors, 10%+ holders (Forms 3, 4, 5)
- [ ] Assess Rule 10b5-1 trading plan compliance (if applicable)
- [ ] Evaluate information barriers (Chinese walls) between departments
- [ ] Check for any trading during blackout periods around material events

### Suitability and Know Your Customer
- [ ] Verify customer account information collected per FINRA Rule 4512
- [ ] Confirm KYC obligations met: reasonable basis, customer-specific, quantitative suitability
- [ ] Assess whether investment recommendations align with customer's investment profile (risk tolerance, time horizon, liquidity needs, financial situation)
- [ ] Evaluate concentration risk in customer portfolios
- [ ] Verify accredited investor status documentation for Reg D offerings
- [ ] Assess suitability of complex products (options, structured products, alternatives)
- [ ] Check for excessive trading (churning) indicators in account activity

### Supervision and Compliance
- [ ] Verify written supervisory procedures (WSPs) exist and are current
- [ ] Confirm designated chief compliance officer (CCO) and supervisory structure
- [ ] Assess branch office supervision and inspection procedures
- [ ] Verify annual compliance review conducted per Rule 206(4)-7 (advisers) or FINRA Rule 3110 (BDs)
- [ ] Check correspondence and communications review procedures
- [ ] Verify outside business activities (OBAs) and private securities transactions disclosed and approved
- [ ] Assess cybersecurity and business continuity planning
- [ ] Confirm customer complaint procedures and complaint log maintained

### Books and Records
- [ ] Verify compliance with Rule 17a-3 (records to be made) for broker-dealers
- [ ] Verify compliance with Rule 17a-4 (records to be preserved) — retention periods and format
- [ ] Assess WORM (write once, read many) compliance for electronic record storage
- [ ] Confirm trade blotters, ledgers, and customer account records maintained
- [ ] Verify order tickets include required information (time, security, quantity, price, customer)
- [ ] Check that customer confirmations delivered promptly after execution
- [ ] Verify investment adviser books and records per Rule 204-2
- [ ] Assess email and electronic communications retention and supervision

### Offering Document Review
- [ ] Assess PPM risk factors for completeness and specificity
- [ ] Verify subscription agreement includes required investor representations
- [ ] Confirm investor questionnaire adequately assesses accreditation and suitability
- [ ] Check use-of-proceeds disclosure for specificity and accuracy
- [ ] Evaluate management and sponsor disclosures (experience, conflicts, compensation)
- [ ] Verify legal opinion included (if applicable)
- [ ] Assess fee structure disclosure (management fees, carried interest, expense allocations)
- [ ] Confirm offering limitations and restrictions properly documented (transfer restrictions, lockups)
