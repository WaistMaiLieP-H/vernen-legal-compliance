# Title IX Education Discrimination Audit

**Purpose**

Systematically audit education institution policies, investigation reports, disciplinary proceedings, and compliance documentation to identify Title IX violations, inadequate sexual harassment response procedures, sex-based discrimination in programs and activities, due process failures, and institutional deliberate indifference that expose educational institutions to federal enforcement, private litigation, and loss of federal financial assistance.

## Governing Standards

### Federal Statutes

- **20 USC § 1681(a)** -- Title IX prohibition on sex-based discrimination in education programs receiving federal financial assistance
- **20 USC § 1681(a)(1)-(9)** -- Exemptions (religious organizations, military institutions, social fraternities/sororities, voluntary youth service organizations, specific admissions exemptions)
- **20 USC § 1682** -- Federal administrative enforcement authority
- **20 USC § 1683** -- Judicial review of agency actions
- **20 USC § 1686** -- Interpretation regarding living facilities
- **20 USC § 1687** -- Program or activity defined (institution-wide coverage)
- **20 USC § 1092(f)** -- Jeanne Clery Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act (Clery Act)
- **20 USC § 1092(f)(1)(C)** -- Annual security report requirements
- **20 USC § 1092(f)(1)(F)** -- Timely warning requirements
- **20 USC § 1092(f)(6)(A)** -- Clery Act crime definitions
- **20 USC § 1092(f)(8)** -- Campus security authority reporting
- **34 USC § 12291** -- Violence Against Women Act (VAWA) definitions incorporated into campus proceedings
- **42 USC § 1983** -- Civil rights enforcement (constitutional due process in public institutions)
- **42 USC § 2000d et seq.** -- Title VI cross-reference for intersectional discrimination

### California Statutes

- **Cal. Education Code § 200-262.4** -- Prohibition of discrimination in education (sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression)
- **Cal. Education Code § 210.7** -- Definition of sexual harassment in education
- **Cal. Education Code § 220** -- Prohibition on discrimination in educational programs
- **Cal. Education Code § 231.5** -- Posting of Title IX information and coordinator contact
- **Cal. Education Code § 234-234.5** -- Safe Place to Learn Act (bullying and harassment prevention)
- **Cal. Education Code § 48900.2** -- Sexual harassment as grounds for suspension/expulsion (K-12)
- **Cal. Education Code § 66281.5-66281.7** -- Sexual harassment policy requirements (postsecondary)
- **Cal. Government Code § 12940-12951** -- FEHA (intersecting employment-based claims for education employees)
- **Cal. Civil Code § 51.9** -- Sexual harassment in education relationships (professional/business)
- **Cal. Penal Code § 243.4** -- Sexual battery (criminal parallel)

### Federal Regulations

- **34 CFR Part 106** -- Title IX regulations (full part)
- **34 CFR § 106.8** -- Designation of Title IX coordinator; notice of nondiscrimination
- **34 CFR § 106.9** -- Dissemination of policy
- **34 CFR § 106.10** -- Scope of coverage (education program or activity)
- **34 CFR § 106.11** -- Application to admissions
- **34 CFR § 106.21** -- Admission standards nondiscrimination
- **34 CFR § 106.31** -- General prohibition on treatment discrimination in education programs
- **34 CFR § 106.33** -- Comparable facilities (restrooms, locker rooms)
- **34 CFR § 106.37** -- Financial assistance nondiscrimination
- **34 CFR § 106.41** -- Athletics nondiscrimination (participation, benefits, scholarships)
- **34 CFR § 106.44** -- Recipient response to sexual harassment (2020/2024 regulations)
- **34 CFR § 106.45** -- Grievance process for formal complaints of sexual harassment
- **34 CFR § 106.46** -- Postsecondary institution grievance procedures (live hearing requirements under 2020 regulations)
- **34 CFR § 106.51** -- Employment nondiscrimination in education
- **34 CFR § 106.71** -- Retaliation prohibited
- **2024 Title IX Regulations** -- Expanded sex-based harassment definition, updated grievance procedures, gender identity protections (implementation status subject to litigation)
- **34 CFR Part 668** -- Clery Act implementing regulations
- **34 CFR § 668.46** -- Institutional security policies and crime statistics

### Key Judicial Standards

- **Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629 (1999)** -- Student-on-student harassment: deliberate indifference standard
- **Gebser v. Lago Vista Independent School District, 524 U.S. 274 (1998)** -- Teacher-on-student harassment: actual knowledge and deliberate indifference
- **Franklin v. Gwinnett County Public Schools, 503 U.S. 60 (1992)** -- Private right of action for damages
- **Cannon v. University of Chicago, 441 U.S. 677 (1979)** -- Implied private right of action under Title IX
- **Jackson v. Birmingham Board of Education, 544 U.S. 167 (2005)** -- Retaliation actionable under Title IX

## Audit Triggers

- Title IX policy or nondiscrimination statement
- Sexual harassment or assault investigation report
- Formal complaint and response documentation
- Disciplinary hearing transcript or decision
- Supportive measures documentation
- Title IX coordinator appointment or training materials
- Athletics equity report or participation data
- Campus security report (Clery Act annual security report)
- Timely warning or emergency notification
- Student or employee complaint or grievance
- Notice of outcome letter
- Appeal decision documentation
- Training materials for investigators, decision-makers, and coordinators
- Climate survey or campus safety assessment
- Memorandum of understanding with local law enforcement
- Retaliation complaint or report
- Institutional response to OCR complaint or investigation

## Audit Checklist

### Title IX Coordinator and Institutional Obligations

- [ ] Title IX coordinator designated with authority to coordinate compliance (34 CFR § 106.8)
- [ ] Title IX coordinator's name, title, office address, email, and phone published
- [ ] Notice of nondiscrimination published and disseminated (34 CFR § 106.8(b))
- [ ] Nondiscrimination notice covers admissions, employment, and all programs/activities
- [ ] Nondiscrimination notice includes Title IX coordinator contact information
- [ ] Grievance procedures adopted and published (34 CFR § 106.8(c))
- [ ] Title IX coordinator not holding conflicting roles that create bias
- [ ] Institution-wide training on Title IX obligations conducted

### Sexual Harassment Response (34 CFR § 106.44)

- [ ] Institution responded promptly when on actual notice of sexual harassment
- [ ] Response not deliberately indifferent (not clearly unreasonable in light of known circumstances)
- [ ] Complainant offered supportive measures regardless of whether formal complaint filed
- [ ] Supportive measures are individualized, non-disciplinary, non-punitive
- [ ] Supportive measures designed to restore or preserve equal access
- [ ] Confidentiality of supportive measures maintained to extent possible
- [ ] No disciplinary sanctions imposed without formal grievance process
- [ ] Emergency removal (if applicable) based on individualized safety and risk analysis
- [ ] Administrative leave for employee respondent properly implemented

### Formal Complaint and Grievance Process (34 CFR § 106.45)

- [ ] Formal complaint filed by complainant or signed by Title IX coordinator
- [ ] Written notice of allegations provided to parties with sufficient detail
- [ ] Notice includes identities of parties, conduct alleged, date, and location
- [ ] Presumption of not responsible maintained throughout process
- [ ] Parties afforded equal opportunity to present witnesses and evidence
- [ ] Parties afforded equal opportunity to have advisor present (including attorney)
- [ ] Parties provided equal access to evidence directly related to allegations
- [ ] Evidence review period of at least 10 days provided before hearing or determination
- [ ] Investigative report summarizes relevant evidence and provided to parties
- [ ] Decision-maker is not the same person as investigator or Title IX coordinator
- [ ] Standard of evidence (preponderance or clear and convincing) applied consistently
- [ ] Written determination includes findings of fact, policy analysis, rationale, and sanctions
- [ ] Written determination includes appeal procedures

### Due Process Protections

- [ ] Adequate notice of charges/allegations with specificity
- [ ] Reasonable time to prepare response
- [ ] Impartial decision-maker free from bias or conflict of interest
- [ ] Opportunity to present evidence and witnesses
- [ ] Live hearing with cross-examination (postsecondary under 2020 regulations, 34 CFR § 106.46)
- [ ] Cross-examination conducted by party's advisor, not by party directly
- [ ] Rape shield protections applied (prior sexual history generally excluded)
- [ ] No reliance on statements by persons not subject to cross-examination (2020 regulations)
- [ ] Informal resolution option available with voluntary written consent
- [ ] Informal resolution not available for employee-on-student harassment
- [ ] Appeal rights provided to both parties on equal terms
- [ ] Appeal grounds include procedural irregularity, new evidence, bias/conflict

### Training Requirements

- [ ] Title IX coordinator, investigators, and decision-makers trained
- [ ] Training covers definition of sexual harassment under Title IX
- [ ] Training covers scope of education program or activity
- [ ] Training covers how to conduct investigation and grievance process
- [ ] Training covers how to serve impartially without bias
- [ ] Training materials publicly available on institution's website (34 CFR § 106.45(b)(10))
- [ ] Training does not rely on sex stereotypes
- [ ] Training on relevance determinations for decision-makers
- [ ] Training on technology used for hearings (if virtual)

### Retaliation Protections

- [ ] Anti-retaliation policy published (34 CFR § 106.71)
- [ ] No adverse action against complainant, respondent, or witnesses for participation
- [ ] Retaliation complaints investigated promptly
- [ ] Confidentiality maintained to protect against retaliation
- [ ] No charges against complainant for conduct code violations arising from same facts (unless separate, serious violation)

### Athletics Equity (34 CFR § 106.41)

- [ ] Effective accommodation of athletic interests and abilities of both sexes
- [ ] Equivalent benefits and opportunities (equipment, scheduling, travel, coaching, facilities)
- [ ] Athletic financial assistance proportional to participation rates
- [ ] Participation opportunities substantially proportionate or continuous expansion
- [ ] Laundry, medical, and training services equitable

### Clery Act Compliance

- [ ] Annual security report published and distributed by October 1
- [ ] Crime statistics compiled for required geographic areas (campus, adjacent, noncampus)
- [ ] Timely warnings issued for Clery crimes posing ongoing threat
- [ ] Campus security authorities identified and trained on reporting obligations
- [ ] Sexual assault, dating violence, domestic violence, and stalking policies included
- [ ] Victim rights and institutional procedures for VAWA offenses described
- [ ] Crime log maintained and publicly available (campus police/security departments)

### Intersectional and Expanded Protections

- [ ] Pregnancy and parental status discrimination addressed (34 CFR § 106.40)
- [ ] Reasonable accommodations for pregnant students provided
- [ ] Gender identity and sexual orientation protections implemented (per 2024 regulations, if effective)
- [ ] California Education Code sex discrimination protections applied (§ 220)
- [ ] Intersectional discrimination (race + sex, disability + sex) identified and addressed

## Output Protocol

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Document ID | Unique identifier for audited document |
| Document Type | Policy / Investigation Report / Complaint / Hearing Record / Outcome Letter / Appeal Decision / Training Material / Athletics Report / Clery Report / Other |
| Institution Name | Name of the educational institution |
| Institution Type | K-12 / Postsecondary / Both |
| Statute Cited | Specific Title IX section, 34 CFR provision, Clery Act section, or California Education Code section |
| Finding | Narrative description of the compliance issue |
| Severity | CRITICAL / HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / INFORMATIONAL |
| Discrimination Type | Sexual Harassment / Sex Discrimination / Retaliation / Athletics Inequity / Pregnancy Discrimination / Other |
| Due Process Failure | YES / NO -- with specific procedural deficiency identified |
| Deliberate Indifference | YES / NO / POTENTIAL -- whether institutional response was clearly unreasonable |
| Participant Harm | Description of actual or potential harm to complainant, respondent, or community |
| Remediation | Required corrective action with regulatory citation |
| OCR Complaint Risk | Likelihood of successful Office for Civil Rights complaint |
| Federal Funding Impact | Whether finding could trigger enforcement under 20 USC § 1682 |
| Litigation Exposure | Private right of action risk under Davis/Gebser/Franklin standards |
| Evidence Reference | Page number, section, or clause in source document |
