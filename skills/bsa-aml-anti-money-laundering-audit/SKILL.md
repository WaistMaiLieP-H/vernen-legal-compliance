# BSA/AML Anti-Money Laundering Audit

**Purpose**

Systematically audit financial institution compliance programs, suspicious activity reports, currency transaction reports, customer due diligence files, and internal controls to identify Bank Secrecy Act violations, AML program deficiencies, SAR and CTR filing failures, CDD and beneficial ownership gaps, sanctions screening lapses, and recordkeeping deficiencies that expose institutions to federal enforcement, criminal prosecution, civil money penalties, and consent orders.

## Audit Checklist

### AML Program Requirements (31 USC § 5318(h))

- [ ] Written AML compliance program adopted by board of directors
- [ ] AML program includes system of internal policies, procedures, and controls
- [ ] BSA/AML compliance officer designated with appropriate authority and resources
- [ ] Compliance officer qualifications and expertise adequate for institution size and risk
- [ ] Ongoing employee training program implemented (initial and annual)
- [ ] Training covers SAR obligations, CTR requirements, CDD, OFAC, and red flags
- [ ] Independent testing (audit) conducted at least every 12-18 months
- [ ] Independent testing scope covers all BSA/AML program components
- [ ] Independent testing findings tracked with corrective action and deadlines
- [ ] Risk assessment conducted and updated (customers, products, services, geographies)
- [ ] Risk assessment methodology documented and Board-approved
- [ ] AML program reasonably designed for institution's risk profile

### Customer Identification Program (CIP) (31 USC § 5318(l))

- [ ] Written CIP adopted and integrated into AML program
- [ ] CIP requires collection of name, date of birth, address, and identification number
- [ ] Documentary and non-documentary verification methods used
- [ ] Identity verified within reasonable time of account opening
- [ ] Adequate notice provided to customers that information is being collected for verification
- [ ] CIP records retained for 5 years after account closure
- [ ] Government list checking (314(a) and OFAC) performed at account opening
- [ ] Procedures for inability to verify identity (account restrictions, SAR filing, closure)

### Customer Due Diligence (CDD) (31 CFR § 1010.230)

- [ ] CDD procedures identify and verify beneficial owners (25%+ ownership or control)
- [ ] Beneficial ownership information collected at account opening
- [ ] Customer risk profiles established (low, medium, high, prohibited)
- [ ] Nature and purpose of customer relationship understood and documented
- [ ] Ongoing monitoring of customer transactions for suspicious activity
- [ ] Customer information updated based on risk and triggered events
- [ ] Enhanced due diligence (EDD) applied to high-risk customers
- [ ] PEP (politically exposed person) screening conducted
- [ ] Foreign correspondent and private banking accounts receive heightened scrutiny (31 USC § 5318(i))
- [ ] No accounts maintained for foreign shell banks (31 USC § 5318(j))
- [ ] Beneficial ownership re-certification procedures for trigger events

### Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

- [ ] SAR filed within 30 calendar days of initial detection (60 days if no suspect identified)
- [ ] SAR narrative provides complete and accurate description of suspicious activity
- [ ] SAR narrative includes who, what, when, where, why, and how
- [ ] SAR includes all relevant account numbers, transaction amounts, and dates
- [ ] SAR threshold met: banks $5,000+ (suspect known) or $25,000+ (no suspect); MSBs $2,000+
- [ ] Continuing activity SARs filed at least every 90 days
- [ ] SAR decision documented for both filed and not-filed determinations
- [ ] SAR confidentiality maintained (31 USC § 5318(g)(2)) -- no disclosure to subject
- [ ] Safe harbor protections understood and applied (31 USC § 5318(g)(3))
- [ ] Transaction monitoring system alerts reviewed timely
- [ ] Alert dispositioning documented with rationale
- [ ] Backlog of unreviewed alerts monitored and escalated
- [ ] SAR filing for insider abuse, fraud, and structuring
- [ ] Known or suspected violations of law reported regardless of dollar amount

### Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)

- [ ] CTR (FinCEN 104) filed for currency transactions exceeding $10,000
- [ ] Multiple currency transactions aggregated per customer per business day (31 CFR § 1010.313)
- [ ] CTR filed within 15 calendar days of transaction
- [ ] CTR contains complete and accurate customer and transaction information
- [ ] CTR exemptions properly applied only for eligible customers (31 CFR § 1020.315)
- [ ] Exemption eligibility reviewed at least annually
- [ ] Phase II exemptions (non-listed businesses) properly documented
- [ ] Structuring awareness: patterns of transactions just below $10,000 identified and investigated
- [ ] Structuring reported via SAR regardless of whether CTR threshold met

### OFAC Sanctions Compliance

- [ ] Written OFAC compliance program implemented
- [ ] SDN (Specially Designated Nationals) list screening at account opening
- [ ] Ongoing SDN screening against updated lists
- [ ] Screening covers all parties to transactions (originators, beneficiaries, intermediaries)
- [ ] Sanctions screening of wire transfers (OFAC 50% rule for owned/controlled entities)
- [ ] Country and region-based sanctions programs monitored
- [ ] Blocked transactions reported to OFAC within 10 business days
- [ ] Rejected transactions documented and reported
- [ ] False positive dispositioning documented with rationale
- [ ] Screening technology tested and validated for name matching accuracy

### Funds Transfer and Travel Rule (31 CFR § 1010.410(f))

- [ ] Transmittor information obtained and retained for funds transfers of $3,000+
- [ ] Required information passed to next financial institution in payment chain
- [ ] Recipient financial institution retains required records
- [ ] Cross-border funds transfer information complete (originator and beneficiary)
- [ ] Correspondent banking relationships include travel rule compliance provisions
- [ ] Cover payment messages include required originator information

### Recordkeeping and Retention

- [ ] SAR records retained for 5 years from filing date
- [ ] CTR records retained for 5 years from filing date
- [ ] CIP and CDD records retained for 5 years after account closure
- [ ] Funds transfer records retained for 5 years
- [ ] Monetary instrument records (cashier's checks, money orders $3,000+) retained for 5 years
- [ ] Correspondence related to BSA compliance retained
- [ ] Records maintained in retrievable format for regulatory examination

### Money Services Business (MSB) Compliance

- [ ] MSB registration filed with FinCEN (Form 107) and renewed every 2 years
- [ ] MSB state licensing requirements met (Cal. Financial Code § 1500+)
- [ ] MSB agent monitoring program implemented
- [ ] MSB AML program includes agent-specific policies
- [ ] MSB agent list maintained and available
- [ ] MSB SAR threshold applied ($2,000 for suspected money laundering)

### Information Sharing

- [ ] 314(a) requests from FinCEN processed within required timeframes
- [ ] 314(b) voluntary information sharing program participation documented (if applicable)
- [ ] Law enforcement requests for BSA data responded to appropriately
- [ ] Grand jury subpoenas and National Security Letters handled per legal requirements
- [ ] SAR confidentiality maintained in all information sharing

### Corporate Transparency Act (Beneficial Ownership)

- [ ] Beneficial ownership information reporting to FinCEN for covered entities
- [ ] Reporting company status properly determined
- [ ] Beneficial owner identification (25%+ ownership or substantial control)
- [ ] Company applicant information reported
- [ ] Updates filed within 30 days of changes to beneficial ownership
- [ ] Exemptions from reporting properly documented (23 exemption categories)

## Output Protocol

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Document ID | Unique identifier for audited document |
| Document Type | AML Program / SAR / CTR / CDD File / CIP Record / OFAC Screening / Audit Report / Exam Report / Policy / Wire Record / MSB Registration / Training Record / Other |
| Institution Name | Name of the financial institution or MSB |
| Institution Type | Bank / Credit Union / Broker-Dealer / MSB / Insurance Company / Casino / Other |
| Charter/Regulator | OCC / FDIC / Federal Reserve / NCUA / State / FinCEN / SEC |
| Statute Cited | Specific BSA section, FinCEN regulation, PATRIOT Act provision, or state law |
| Finding | Narrative description of the compliance issue |
| Severity | CRITICAL / HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / INFORMATIONAL |
| SAR Deficiency | YES / NO -- whether SAR filing obligation was missed or narrative was deficient |
| CTR Deficiency | YES / NO -- whether CTR filing obligation was missed or data was inaccurate |
| CDD/CIP Gap | YES / NO -- whether customer identification or due diligence was incomplete |
| OFAC Exposure | YES / NO -- whether sanctions screening failure or blocked transaction mishandling occurred |
| Structuring Indicators | YES / NO -- whether patterns consistent with structuring were identified |
| Remediation | Required corrective action with regulatory citation |
| Penalty Exposure | Estimated civil money penalty range under 31 USC § 5321 |
| Criminal Referral Risk | Whether finding could support criminal referral under 31 USC § 5322 or 18 USC § 1956-1957 |
| Examination Risk | Likelihood of finding in regulatory examination (MRA, MRIA, consent order) |
| Evidence Reference | Page number, section, or clause in source document |
